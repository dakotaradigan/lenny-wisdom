# Shreyas Doshi

Former PM leader at Stripe, Twitter, Google, and Yahoo. Known for sharing consistently insightful and often contrarian wisdom on the art of product management, with a large following built through his memorable frameworks on Twitter.

## Key Frameworks

### Pre-Mortem (Tigers, Paper Tigers, Elephants)
A proactive risk identification ritual run before a project launches. The team imagines the project has failed, then works backwards to identify what went wrong. Risks are categorized as Tigers (real threats that could kill the project), Paper Tigers (seeming threats that are not actually concerning), and Elephants (unspoken issues no one is addressing). Creates psychological safety and shared vocabulary for discussing risks.

### LNO Framework
All tasks fall into three categories: Leverage (L) tasks that return 10-100X the effort, Neutral (N) tasks that return roughly what you put in, and Overhead (O) tasks that return less than invested. The key insight is to apply perfectionism only to L tasks, do N tasks adequately, and minimize effort on O tasks. The same activity (e.g., filing a bug report, writing meeting notes) can be L, N, or O depending on context.

### Three Levels of Product Work
Product work operates at three levels: Impact (what outcome does this create), Execution (what does it take to get done), and Optics (creating awareness of impact and execution). Conflicts between PMs and leadership often stem from defaulting to different levels. A CEO may think at the impact level while the PM is stuck at execution level.

### Opportunity Cost over ROI
In high-leverage roles, stop chasing positive ROI (which biases toward quick wins) and instead minimize opportunity cost. ROI thinking shrinks the denominator (time), favoring low-hanging fruit. Opportunity cost thinking asks "is this the best use of my time?" rather than "is this a good use of my time?"

## Core Ideas

- Most execution problems in high-performing organizations are actually strategy problems or culture problems in disguise; the telltale sign is when bandaids keep falling off and the same issue recurs
- High agency -- combining ownership, creative execution, and resilience -- is the single trait that most differentiates PMs who exceed expectations from those who underperform relative to their credentials
- The tasks PMs procrastinate on are almost always their highest-leverage tasks; procrastination is a signal pointing toward the work that matters most
- When planning, allocate time explicitly (e.g., 60% incrementals, 30% big new initiatives, 10% stability) to prevent teams from filling all capacity with safe positive-ROI quick wins

## Notable Perspectives

> "If you do a pre-mortem right, you will not have to do an ugly post-mortem."

> "When you are in a high leverage role, you should stop doing work that simply provides a positive return on investment and start focusing on work that minimizes opportunity cost."
