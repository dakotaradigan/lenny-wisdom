# Raaz Herzberg

CMO and VP Product Strategy at Wiz, the fastest-growing software company in history ($100M ARR in 18 months). Employee #7, first PM. Previously led security products at Microsoft including Azure Sentinel. Made the unusual transition from VP Product to CMO, bringing deep product understanding to marketing.

## Key Frameworks

### Signal Quality in Customer Conversations
"Sounds interesting, we'd love to hear more" is not a signal of product-market fit. Real signals are: "How are you pricing this?", "When can we start a POV?", "Can we be your first customer?" The shift from polite interest to commercial urgency is the inflection point that separates exploring from finding.

### Dummy Explanation Test
If you cannot explain what your product does in simple terms that a non-technical person immediately understands, your positioning is wrong. Complexity in explanation often masks lack of clarity in value proposition.

### Follow the Heat
Pay attention to where energy and enthusiasm concentrate within the organization and in customer conversations. The areas generating the most heat -- the most questions, excitement, pull -- indicate where the real opportunity lies, even if the original plan pointed elsewhere.

## Core Ideas

- Wiz pivoted from network security to cloud security six weeks after founding because customer conversations were politely positive but lacked commercial urgency -- the founders had the courage to recognize the difference
- Humans have a bias toward looking for affirmation in customer conversations; you must actively counterbalance this by listening for what you do not want to hear
- The PM-to-CMO transition is powerful because understanding how the product actually works and what customers care about makes marketing dramatically more authentic and effective
- The founding team had 10-15 customer meetings per day before having a product -- the velocity of customer learning was itself a competitive advantage
