# Albert Cheng

Albert Cheng is one of the top consumer growth minds in the world. He led growth and monetization at Duolingo, Grammarly, and now Chess.com. Earlier at YouTube, he worked on streaming and gaming features used by 20+ million people. His approach blends marketing, data, strategy, and product management.

## Key Frameworks

### Explore and Exploit
Explore = finding the right mountain to climb. Exploit = climbing it effectively. Oscillate between the two: when you find a winning insight, share it broadly and run adjacent experiments. When experiments stop being statistically significant, return to exploration mode.

### Insight-Level Growth
Don't just apply explore/exploit at the macro level. Apply it at the insight level: when you find a human psychology insight (e.g., people review chess games after wins, not losses), propagate that insight across every product surface.

## Core Ideas

- At Chess.com, discovering that 80% of game reviews happened after wins (not losses) led to reframing the losing experience positively, growing game reviews by 25% and subscriptions by 20%
- At Grammarly, sampling paid suggestions into the free experience changed perception from "spelling checker" to "powerful writing tool," driving massive monetization gains
- User retention is gold for consumer subscription companies; without retention, you must monetize on day one
- AI tools like text-to-SQL Slack bots democratize data access and explode the number of questions teams ask
- High agency and clock speed matter more than deep experience, especially in the AI era where learned habits may need to be discarded
