# Noam Lovinsky

CPO at Grammarly with an exceptionally diverse career: early PM at YouTube (-1 to 1), CPO at Thumbtack (turnaround), creator of Facebook's New Product Experimentation team (0 to 1), and now scaling Grammarly. Famously under-the-radar -- no tweets, no newsletter, just doing the work.

## Key Frameworks

### Advocate for What Is Best for the Business
Even if it means killing your own project. At YouTube, Noam convinced leadership that his 50-engineer project should be wound down because other priorities mattered more. In healthy organizations, this earns trust and opens doors rather than ending careers.

### Ask to Be Layered
When struggling with a role, proactively suggest reporting to someone better suited to support you rather than pretending everything is fine. This atypical career move at YouTube led to better work, better mentorship, and eventually more responsibility.

### Diversify Growth Channels
Thumbtack's near-death experience came from depending almost entirely on SEO. When Google changed algorithms, the business cratered. A single-channel growth company is always a strategic risk regardless of how well that channel is performing.

## Core Ideas

- What kills most projects and startups is not bad ideas but loss of stamina -- when the team loses energy and faith, quality drops and the death spiral begins
- The best career moves come from understanding company strategy holistically and aligning your influence toward overall goals, even at personal cost
- Creating innovation space at large companies requires different incentive structures; if you run a zero-to-one incubator under standard biannual performance reviews, you have already killed it
- Success comes from doing what authentically fills you up rather than optimizing for external signals; you do not need to tweet or network to have an extraordinary career
