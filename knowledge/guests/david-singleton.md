# David Singleton

CTO of Stripe, responsible for engineering and design teams. Previously spent over a decade as VP of Engineering at Google. Passionate about building product-oriented engineering cultures and operationalizing craft excellence at scale.

## Key Frameworks

### Co-Creation Product Development
Find the right set of early users and build the product with them, not for them. Stripe Billing was co-created with companies like Figma and Slack through shared Slack channels and regular product reviews. Only when the alpha group was "super, super happy" did the product go to a broader audience. This is how every product at Stripe is built.

### Operating Principles as Behavioral Code
Stripe's operating principles are not abstract values but distilled behaviors from the most effective employees. "Users first" drives deep personal relationships with users. "Be meticulous in your craft" manifests in error messages that link directly to documentation. "Move with urgency and focus" balances long-term infrastructure thinking with solving immediate user needs.

## Core Ideas

- Stripe held off on hiring its first PM for years because every early engineer was deeply product-minded. The best PMs for developer-focused products are usually former engineers who bring user insight and strategy on top of technical depth.
- The hiring process must be structured, consistent, and simulate real work -- no trick questions. Written exercises for PMs, pair-programming for engineers. Stripe is also extremely patient with critical roles, sometimes building relationships for months or years before the right moment comes.
- Meticulous craft does not mean slow shipping. It means choosing where to be meticulous based on what matters most to users -- like making API error messages link to relevant documentation, which creates delight at a high-stakes moment.
- Product development at Stripe is a much more cross-functional team sport than at most companies. Legal, partnerships, risk, and compliance teams participate in early product development because financial infrastructure requires their creative thinking.
