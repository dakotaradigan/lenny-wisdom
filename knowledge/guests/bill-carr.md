# Bill Carr

Co-author of *Working Backwards* and former VP of Digital Media at Amazon where he spent 15 years launching Amazon Music, Prime Video, and Amazon Studios. Now runs Working Backwards LLC helping companies implement Amazon's management practices.

## Key Frameworks

### Working Backwards PR/FAQ
Start with customer needs unconstrained by resources, finances, or engineering. Write a press release and FAQ for the product before building it. If you serve customers well, revenue and growth follow as an article of faith.

### Single-Threaded Leadership
Move from project orientation to program orientation. One leader owns a domain permanently with dedicated cross-functional resources. This creates clear ownership, speed, and agility while freeing senior management from refereeing every roadmap item.

### Disagree and Commit
Your obligation is to voice disagreement with supporting data. Once the decision-maker shows they understand your point and still decides differently, commit fully. Find the kernel of why they chose that direction and run with it.

## Core Ideas

- Amazon's product and process innovation all happened in one narrow window (2003-2007) as the company transitioned from hypergrowth to managing complexity
- Never create compound metrics; they obfuscate the relationship between actions and outcomes
- Leaders are "right a lot" through accumulated judgment from experience, especially from being wrong
- Single-threaded teams require countermeasures for functional excellence: promotion panels, cross-org code reviews, and engineering standards set by central technical leaders
- The key question for single-threaded teams: does this team have the resources within their control to effectively manage their domain?
