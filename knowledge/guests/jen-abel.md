# Jen Abel

Co-founder of Jellyfish, where she helps early-stage founders learn zero-to-one enterprise sales. GM of Enterprise at State Affairs. Previously enterprise sales director at The Muse and General Assembly. Obsessed with helping founders in the zero-to-one stage.

## Key Frameworks

### The Founder Is the Product
In early sales, the founder IS the product. The actual product may be abstract or MVP-stage, but the founder's novel insight and vision is what gets the market excited. Nobody else can see the budding moments in conversations that reveal where true product-market fit lives. Day one vision is never the same as the vision that achieves PMF.

### Four-Component Cold Outreach
Keep cold emails to three or four sentences that don't require scrolling on mobile. The four components: (1) Relevancy -- why this matters to them specifically, (2) Counterintuitive or shock value statement, (3) Focus on the problem, not the solution, and (4) Concision -- leave them wanting more. Example: "Zero to one sales talent doesn't exist. That's why I want to have a conversation with you."

### The Five-Call Sales Cycle
Intro call, demo/second call, proposal walkthrough, co-authored scope refinement, procurement introduction, then signature. Each call has a specific purpose. Founder-led sales is not about revenue on day one -- it's about learning as fast as humanly possible to earn the right to sell.

## Core Ideas

- Be vulnerable on sales calls. "Hey, I'm an early-stage startup. We have a lot to learn. Can we gain your insight into how this problem is manifesting on your side?" This openness creates trust and surfaces real market intelligence.
- Interest rate is driven by the founder's insight quality, not email technique. Two simultaneous engagements using identical methods can produce 2% vs. 12% interest rates -- the only difference is the founder's insight on the market.
- Cold calling has surprisingly high interest rates, often higher than email. Don't dismiss the phone.
- Win rate matters more than conversion rate. If you close 30-40% of people who enter your funnel, you need far less top-of-funnel volume. Focus on funnel throughput, not just opening volume.
