# Maya Prohovnik

Head of Product for Podcasting at Spotify, overseeing product, design, and engineering for podcast creators and listeners. Employee number one at Anchor, which Spotify acquired and which now powers 75%+ of all new podcasts. Has four podcasts of her own covering Stephen King, Big Brother, parenting, and science fiction.

## Key Frameworks

### Dogfooding as Non-Negotiable
You cannot build great creator tools without being a creator yourself. Every PM on Maya's team is encouraged to create their own podcast. Those who finally do consistently report a qualitative shift: "Something clicked and now I really understand what they need." Without dogfooding, teams default to feature request lists rather than deeply felt problem prioritization.

### Manual-First Friction Removal
When Anchor wanted to let users distribute podcasts to Apple with one button, they did not have a backdoor API deal. They hired college interns who manually created Apple Podcast accounts and submitted hundreds of thousands of podcasts through those accounts. To users, it felt magical and automatic. This "do things that don't scale" approach drove massive hosting market share.

## Core Ideas

- Community is the key retention mechanism for podcasters; without knowing who is listening and what they like, creators lose motivation to continue
- Start a podcast about what you can talk about endlessly; passion sustains production through the inevitable difficulty
- Customer support is a powerful entry point into product careers because it builds deep user empathy from day one
- Balance strategic two-year bets with solving users' immediate pain; if you only work on big strategy, users leave for competitors in the meantime
- A mobile-first, low-production podcast strategy proves that professional audio quality is not required for audience engagement
