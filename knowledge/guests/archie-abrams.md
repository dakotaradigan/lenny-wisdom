# Archie Abrams

Archie Abrams is VP of Product and Head of Growth at Shopify, leading 600+ people across product, design, engineering, data ops, and growth marketing. Shopify powers 10% of US e-commerce and did $235 billion in GMV in 2023 (roughly the size of Finland's economy).

## Key Frameworks

### Optimize for Churn (Not Against It)
Shopify lowers barriers to starting a business because most businesses fail anyway. The business model works because revenue comes primarily from payments tied to merchant success. A few big winners in each cohort make the entire cohort profitable, even with high churn.

### Ban KPIs for Core Product Teams
Core product teams at Shopify don't have metrics or KPIs. Decisions are driven by taste, intuition, and building toward the CEO's 100-year vision. This prevents local optimization that hurts long-term product quality.

### Long-Term Holdouts
Every experiment keeps a holdout group, and impact is automatically reviewed at 1, 2, and 3 years. This reveals that many short-term wins don't hold up and some initially neutral experiments become significant long-term winners.

## Core Ideas

- Optimizing for conversion rates at each funnel stage is seductive but wrong; it's often easier to hurt the step before yours than to actually improve your step
- The best way to get more people to any step is to get more people in the door, which always hurts conversion rate but may increase absolute numbers
- Shopify's mission is to increase entrepreneurship on the internet, not to retain individual merchants
- Taste and intuition can outperform metrics-driven decisions when building toward a long-term vision

## Notable Perspectives

> "It's actually almost always easier to just make it harder to do the thing right before your step in the funnel to increase your conversion rate."

> "Core product teams don't have metrics or KPIs. They're essentially banned. Decisions are made based on taste, and intuition, and building towards this long-term vision."
