# Paul Millerd

Author of The Pathless Path, a self-published book that sold over 40,000 copies exploring alternatives to the default career script. Challenges the assumption that continuous full-time employment is the only way to structure a working life. His work resonates deeply with tech professionals questioning whether their career trajectory is truly theirs.

## Key Frameworks

### The Default Path vs The Pathless Path
The default path is the unconscious script about what you should do: college, good job, good salary, house, continuous employment until retirement. The pathless path is a conscious shift away from this toward embracing uncertainty, experimenting with work, and finding what you actually want to keep doing -- without treating not-knowing as a problem to solve.

### Three-Hour Workday Experiment
Block three hours during a workday, sneak out, go for a walk without a destination or revisit a childhood activity. Pay attention to what emerges. Do you feel guilty? Where does that guilt come from? This surfaces unconscious beliefs about work and identity that may be constraining your choices.

### Four Priorities Inventory
List the four things you most value getting from your current work situation. Assess how many you are actually getting. If you are getting three of four, that is a strong position -- but knowing the gap helps you plan your next move deliberately rather than reactively.

## Core Ideas

- We are all on a pathless path whether we admit it or not; even at stable companies, the organization reinvents itself every couple of years
- Leaving a full-time job feels shameful not because it is objectively bad but because it violates deep cultural scripts that equate working with being a good person
- When people quit jobs, they forget to fire the manager in their head -- they carry implicit contracts about work hours and productivity that no longer serve them
- The goal is not to find a job or build a business but to "actively and consciously search for the work that you want to keep doing"
