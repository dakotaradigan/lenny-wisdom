## Key Frameworks

### Three Brain Systems: Safety, Reward, Purpose
Every moment, one of three systems dominates your thinking. Safety: when you feel threatened, your brain's objective shifts from productive contribution to restoring safety. Reward: transactional motivation ("that's not my job" is the reward system speaking). Purpose: activated when you understand both the impact of your work and care about the people affected. Purpose can be activated at the smallest grain -- writing a single email -- not just grand missions. Teams operating from purpose dramatically outperform those stuck in safety or reward.

### The Campus Metaphor
The brain is like a college campus with departments: history (lowest energy, default), science (experimental, open-minded), art (creative, boundless), humanities (compassion). Most people over-rely on their history department. Deliberately routing thoughts to the science or art department produces dramatically better answers, especially for product work.

### Self-Awareness as Culinary School
Understanding your own personality preferences (Big Five traits) is the starting point for effective collaboration. Like a chef who must know their own taste preferences to cook for diverse palates, you must understand your natural tendencies -- directness, introversion, abstract thinking -- before you can work effectively with people wired differently. The growth mentality version asks: "Would these actions increase or reduce the probability of the outcome I want?"

## Core Ideas

- The biggest propaganda in business is "we're more similar than we are different"; build the muscle to work effectively with people who are extremely different from you
- Before optimizing hand-to-hand interpersonal dynamics, check if the habitat (team environment) is predisposed to high-functioning thinking
- Ask yourself "What kind of experience am I?" not "How good am I at my job?" -- if you are a miserable experience to work with, fix that first
- Personality assessments like Big Five are imperfect but useful, especially with a growth mentality rather than a justification mentality
- The best product and team environments (like Figma and Canva) show obvious investment in creating habitats that predispose people to high-function thinking
