# Shreyas Doshi (Live Session)

Note: This profile is drawn from a live session recorded at the Lenny and Friends Summit in front of 1,000 people. Shreyas is a former PM leader at Stripe, Twitter, Google, and Yahoo with 20 years of product experience. His first Lenny's Podcast episode is the third most popular of all time.

## Key Frameworks

### Questions I Wish I'd Asked Myself Sooner
A set of reflective questions for PM leaders based on Shreyas's career suffering: (1) Why am I so busy? -- Most PMs are chronically busy for 15+ years before fixing it, and the stress manifests as physical symptoms. (2) Am I building good taste? -- Taste is central to product leadership but rarely developed deliberately. (3) Am I actually listening? -- PM leaders often think they listen well but are really just waiting to speak.

### The Busyness Trap
PMs are busy because they say yes to everything, fail to distinguish high-leverage from low-leverage work, and confuse activity with progress. Shreyas spent 16-17 years stressed before fixing this in his final 3-4 years as a PM leader. The fix requires honest self-assessment about where time actually goes versus where it should go.

## Core Ideas

- Physical symptoms (back pain, aches) often stem from work stress rather than physical causes -- the body keeps score of PM leadership pressure
- Taste is not just about aesthetics; it is the ability to make good product decisions under uncertainty, and it must be actively cultivated through exposure and practice
- True listening requires vulnerability and willingness to have your mind changed, not just collecting information to support existing views
- If there is no conflict or contention in your product discussions, something is missing -- you are probably not challenging assumptions enough

## Notable Perspectives

> "There were about 16 or 17 years where I was just incredibly busy, and because I was incredibly busy, I was extremely stressed, and even though I was doing a good job, I was not feeling very good inside."
