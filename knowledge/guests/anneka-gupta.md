# Anneka Gupta

Anneka Gupta is Chief Product Officer at Rubrik, a lecturer on product management at Stanford GSB, and a board member at Tinuiti. She spent 11 years at LiveRamp, joining as one of the earliest employees and rising to President and GM.

## Key Frameworks

### What It Means to Be Strategic
Two components, both required: (1) articulate a compelling and simple "why" behind decisions and direction, and (2) champion big, hard ideas that serve the long-term interest of the product/company. Having one without the other means you won't be seen as strategic.

### Hypothesis Seeding
Instead of telling teams they're wrong, share a hypothesis based on customer conversations: "I was talking to security leaders and heard this feedback, which made me think X about our strategy - what do you think?" This opens discussion without shutting it down.

### Having Fun as a Leadership Mindset
Reframe scary situations (firing direct reports, massive organizational change) by asking "What can I learn from this?" and bringing humor into meetings. This shifts you from scarcity mindset to abundance mindset.

## Core Ideas

- Leverage founder mode as a head of product: activate the CEO to push initiatives you believe in, using their unique power as a resource
- Collect deep information about what's happening in the organization, then choose strategically where to make course corrections
- Manage your energy levels by scheduling difficult work during peak hours and protecting your fundamentals (lunch, sleep)
- When a founder pushes a pet project, decide whether this is a hill to die on - sometimes letting it go preserves your ability to fight for what truly matters

## Notable Perspectives

> "When people say 'I want someone that's strategic,' what they're really saying is 'I want someone that can come up with and articulate a compelling and simple why behind the decisions.'"

> "If you have one without the other, ultimately people are not going to see you as strategic."
