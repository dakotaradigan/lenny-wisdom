# Marc Benioff

Co-founder and CEO of Salesforce, the second-largest B2B SaaS company in the world ($350B+ valuation, $35B+ annual revenue). Previously spent 10 years at Oracle under Larry Ellison. Pioneered the "end of software" movement and cloud-based enterprise software. Close mentee of Steve Jobs.

## Key Frameworks

### Throwing Everything Against the Wall
When launching, try every possible tactic to break through noise. Benioff hired actors to protest outside Siebel's conference with "End of Software" signs, then deployed fake news crews to interview the protesters. The lesson: in a noisy world, find creative, memorable ways to differentiate and get attention, then turn the winning tactic into a winning strategy.

### Beginner's Mind as Operating System
Approach every new wave of technology as if you know nothing. When AI agents emerged, Benioff's mindset was not "I just got used to AI" but "I can't wait for the next thing." The mistake is treating new paradigms as burdens; the opportunity is embracing them with fresh enthusiasm and directing the entire company's energy toward them.

## Core Ideas

- Steve Jobs' three pieces of advice to Benioff: grow 10x in 24 months, sign a huge customer, and build an application economy -- advice that led to AppExchange and eventually the App Store concept
- The stock is not the goal and is very distracting; focus on building, not on share price
- Generosity in professional relationships compounds: Benioff gave Jobs the appstore.com domain and trademark; Jobs provided invaluable mentorship over decades
- When you have a breakthrough product, focus the entire company on it and find more fuel to feed the fire
- Relentless persistence in recruiting and closing: when convinced of a direction, never let momentum drop

## Notable Perspectives
