## Key Frameworks

### PLG Is Fundamentally DLG (Data-Led Growth)
When you give away a free product, you want two things in exchange: broader reach (lower barrier to entry means the product spreads itself) and usage behavior data (which features correlate with higher conversion and retention). If you do not have a data foundation to analyze free user behavior, you are giving away a free product for nothing.

### PLG + Sales Is the Endgame
You do not need to be a PLG purist. Most successful companies eventually run both motions. PLG provides volume and broad reach through low-barrier entry. Sales provides targeted pursuit of large accounts with big contract values. Starting with PLG is easier than adding it later to a sales-led organization, but both directions are possible.

### Common PLG Pitfalls
The biggest failures happen when companies (1) add a free tier without instrumenting data to learn from it, (2) treat PLG as a complete replacement for sales rather than a complement, or (3) try to add PLG to a sales-led company without rethinking the product experience for self-serve users.

## Core Ideas

- The users of B2B software are the same humans using B2C software -- they are trained to try before they buy, and they demand that in B2B too
- Key properties of PLG products: very low barrier to entry, a free version, no boss approval needed to start, self-service checkout, and the product spreads on its own
- If your competitors add PLG, they gain the advantage of attracting end users who become advocates to their employers; if you only have PLG, competitors may close big deals faster through sales
- Activation is the highest-leverage area to optimize in a PLG motion because it determines whether free users ever experience enough value to convert
- Audit your existing funnel for where users drop off, what features correlate with conversion, and what the natural expansion triggers are before designing your PLG strategy
