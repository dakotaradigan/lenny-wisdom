Previously led product launches at Meta focused on commerce and creators. Founded Design Nation, a national nonprofit democratizing design education.

## Key Frameworks

### Vision as Anchor
Craft a singular vision so the entire team stays oriented through the chaos of product development. The vision answers: if your team does its job correctly, what does the world look like? Every pivot then feels like forward progress rather than failure, because the vision remains constant even as the path shifts.

### Pain-Solution-Proof Point Cadence
Structure vision pitches as interleaved sequences of pain point, solution, and proof point rather than the traditional linear flow of research readout then design crit then product review. Use real user testimonials layered on prototypes to create emotional pull.

### Confidence Signaling
Explicitly communicate your confidence level on every opinion: "I feel medium confidence on this -- I defer to you" versus "I feel really strongly about this." This prevents teams from assuming every PM opinion is a directive and preserves psychological safety for dissent.

## Core Ideas

- Strong conviction early creates momentum that is more valuable than waiting for perfect information; putting out an A-minus idea to get reactions is far better than starting from zero
- Hackathons are not just for engineers -- anyone can pitch an idea, recruit collaborators by literally walking around the office asking, and use that to build momentum for a new product
- A single shared artifact built collaboratively by the whole team (not separate research, design, and product deliverables) creates unified ownership and more compelling vision pitches
- Directness only works if it is two-way; start feedback exchanges by asking others for feedback on yourself first to create a culture of constant, flowing candor
