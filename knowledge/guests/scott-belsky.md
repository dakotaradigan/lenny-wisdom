# Scott Belsky

Former CPO and Chief Strategy Officer at Adobe. Founded Behance (sold to Adobe). Author of The Messy Middle. Angel investor in Pinterest, Uber, Airtable, Warby Parker. One of the most insightful product thinkers on building consumer experiences and developing product sense.

## Key Frameworks

### The First Mile
In the first 30 seconds of using a new product, users are lazy, vain, and selfish -- they want it done quickly, want to look good, and want to feel successful immediately. Most teams spend the final mile of building considering the first mile of the customer's experience. Invest disproportionately in onboarding, orientation, and defaults. The first mile must be redesigned for each new cohort of users because later adopters are less forgiving than early ones.

### The Conviction Test
When a founder is struggling and asks "should I continue?", ask: "Knowing everything you know now, do you have more or less conviction in the problem and solution?" More conviction means you are in the messy middle -- stick with it. Less conviction means quit, pivot, do something different. Life is too short to work on something you have lost conviction in.

## Core Ideas

- Product sense is built through empathy, not passion for solutions; sit shoulder to shoulder with customers to understand the full context of their day, not just how they use your product
- People do not talk about a product doing what they expected; they talk about what they did not expect -- optimize for surprise and delight to drive word of mouth
- Every great consumer product pulls a "magic trick" -- an element of mystery, intrigue, or surprise that creates emotional connection
- AI will enable products to meet users where they are, personalizing onboarding based on user type rather than generalizing for everyone
