# Krithika Shankarraman

First marketing hire and VP of Marketing at OpenAI. First marketing hire at Stripe (sole marketer for three years). Early marketing leader at Retool and Dropbox. Android marketing at Google. Currently executive in residence at Thrive Capital supporting portfolio founders on marketing. Engineer turned marketer who brings a diagnostic framework to a discipline often driven by playbooks.

## Key Frameworks

### Anti-Playbook Playbook (Four-Step Diagnostic)
(1) Diagnose the actual problem: is it top-of-funnel awareness or bottom-of-funnel conversion? Hiring demand gen when you have a leaky funnel is the worst move. (2) Analyze competitors' approaches for gaps and niches. (3) Intentionally take a different path -- differentiation is core to marketing. (4) Look outside your vertical for ideas to cross-apply.

### Use Case Epiphany
At OpenAI, awareness was not the problem -- everyone knew ChatGPT. The problem was "I don't know what to use it for." Marketing became about creating epiphanies: "I had no idea ChatGPT could do that." This reframes marketing from demand generation to education and imagination expansion.

### Vanity Metrics Are Bullshit
Clicks, views, and impressions are not the goal. The question is: what experience do you want customers to have when they interact with your brand? Measure the outcome of that experience, not the volume of exposure.

## Core Ideas

- Do not copy what worked at other companies; the combination of context, competitive landscape, and timing that made a strategy work is non-transferable -- understand the inputs, not the outputs
- Product management and product marketing should be a three-legged race from the beginning of product development, not a handoff at the end of the conveyor belt
- When ChatGPT Enterprise's contact sales form launched, lead volume 40x-ed overnight; Krithika coded the first lead qualification model herself in Python using ChatGPT
- Consumer marketing tactics (storytelling, emotional resonance, brand moments) work for enterprise products because enterprise buyers are humans too
