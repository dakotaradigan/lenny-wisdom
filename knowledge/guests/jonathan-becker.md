## Key Frameworks

### Channel Diversification as Risk Management
Treat marketing spend like an investment portfolio -- never concentrate all capital in a single channel. The crash-and-burn scenarios come from brands built entirely on one platform loophole. Diversify across paid search, paid social, SEO, email, and offline to reduce volatility.

### Creative as the Biggest Lever
As ad platforms automate targeting and bidding, creative is the primary remaining differentiator. Organize testing around the funnel: top generates intent, bottom captures it. Isolate single variables across nearly identical creatives. User-generated content consistently outperforms polished brand assets due to perceived authenticity.

### Marketing Economics First
Understand LTV-to-CAC ratio, payback period, and lead scoring before scaling spend. Companies succeeding at performance marketing understand their own unit economics deeply and focus on bottom-line profitability, not gross growth.

## Core Ideas

- Product-market fit is the prerequisite for paid growth; if organic channels work, paid will amplify; if the product does not convert organically, paid will not fix it
- AI is displacing tactical implementation, pushing human roles toward strategy, modeling, and creative direction
- Paid growth and SEO are complementary, not mutually exclusive
- Nobody should expect overnight turnaround from performance marketing; it requires patience, testing, and constant adaptation
