# April Dunford

April Dunford is the best-selling author of Obviously Awesome, considered the industry bible on product positioning. She spent 25 years leading marketing, product, and sales teams at seven successful B2B startups (six acquired), and has worked with over 200 companies on positioning. She has almost certainly done more positioning work than any human alive.

## Key Frameworks

### Positioning Methodology
A systematic approach to positioning that starts with understanding your competitive alternatives, then identifying your unique differentiated capabilities, the value those capabilities enable for customers, the customer segments that care most about that value, and finally the market category that makes your value obvious.

### Repositioning as Growth Lever
When a product isn't selling, the problem is often positioning, not the product itself. Repositioning the same product into a different market category or for a different use case can dramatically change results without changing a single feature.

## Core Ideas

- Positioning is the foundational marketing concept, yet there was no methodology for doing it - most companies just guess or copy competitors
- When positioning is wrong, everything downstream (sales, marketing, product direction) struggles; when it's right, everything clicks
- Most products can be positioned in multiple market categories; the right category makes your value obvious to the right customers
- Good positioning flows directly into your sales narrative, marketing messaging, and product roadmap priorities
