# Melissa Perri

CEO and founder of The Product Institute. Author of Escaping the Build Trap and Product Operations. Has trained PMs at nearly every Fortune 500 company. A leading voice on the distinction between product ownership (Scrum-derived) and true product management, and on helping large non-tech companies adopt real product thinking.

## Key Frameworks

### The Build Trap
Organizations fall into the build trap when they measure success by shipping features rather than by outcomes for customers and the business. Teams become feature factories where the backlog is dictated by stakeholders and the PM role devolves into ticket prioritization. Escaping requires reorienting the entire organization around outcomes, experimentation, and customer discovery.

### Product Owner vs. Product Manager
The product owner role emerged from Scrum to help developers prioritize what to work on. It was never designed to encompass the full scope of product management (market research, customer discovery, strategy, go-to-market). Many large companies introduced "product owners" through agile transformations but never added the strategic layer, leaving a critical gap. Capital One is a positive example of layering product management onto an agile transformation successfully.

## Core Ideas

- SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework) is not recommended; every success story involves ripping it up and rebuilding something custom
- Agile principles are sound (move quickly, deliver value) but the dogmatic application of frameworks often misses the point
- Large non-tech companies are 10 years behind Silicon Valley in product management maturity and still at the beginning of the journey
- Product operations as a discipline helps organizations scale product management practices, share learnings, and maintain consistency
- The product owner role is the third-fastest growing role in tech, yet most holders lack training in strategy, customer discovery, and outcome orientation
