## Key Frameworks

### Fuel and Engine
Forget traditional marketing org charts (product marketing, content, partner, demand gen). Instead, think of marketing as needing two things: fuel (everything you create -- content, copy, design, messaging that adds value) and engine (how you get it to the right people -- distribution channels, tracking, ops). Every company needs an engine. The strategic question is: where is your biggest challenge right now, fuel or engine?

### Marketing Hire Archetypes by Business Model
Different business models need fundamentally different first marketing hires. A self-serve PLG product needs a growth marketer who understands funnels and experimentation. A sales-led enterprise product needs a demand gen or field marketer who understands pipeline. A consumer product needs a brand marketer. Hiring the wrong archetype for your model is the most common marketing hiring mistake.

## Core Ideas

- The first marketing hire is one of the most consequential hiring decisions a startup makes, and most founders get it wrong by defaulting to "marketing generalist" rather than identifying what specific marketing capability their business model demands.
- Red flags that your marketing team is not performing: marketing and product are not aligned on messaging, the team cannot explain the funnel from awareness to revenue, or marketing activity feels disconnected from business metrics.
- Product teams should work with marketing much earlier than they typically do. The best marketing-product relationships involve marketing in the problem-definition stage, not just the launch stage.
- Content marketing is fuel, not an engine. Creating great content without a distribution engine is like building a car with no wheels. Conversely, a great engine with no fuel (just running ads to a bad landing page) wastes money.
