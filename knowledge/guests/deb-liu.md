# Deb Liu

CEO of Ancestry, formerly VP of Product at Facebook where she spent 11+ years creating Facebook Marketplace (1B+ monthly users) and the company's first mobile direct-response ad product. Also built Facebook's games business and payments platform. Board member at Intuit. Known for building multiple billion-dollar zero-to-one businesses within large companies.

## Key Frameworks

### PM Your Career Like Your Product
The best PMs are often terrible PMs for their own careers -- they drift from job to job without a spec. Write a spec for your career: what does success look like, how will you get there? Balance learning and impact: maximum impact comes from the job you know best, but then you stop learning. Ladder back and forth between mastery (impact) and being a newbie (learning).

### Resilience as Career Differentiator
The most successful product leaders are not those with charmed careers but those who turned stumbling blocks into stepping stones. Trees grow strong because they bend in the wind. Hard feedback and product failures build the resilience that separates great leaders from good ones.

## Core Ideas

- Always be learning. Someone who is always learning will always exceed someone who is the expert today. There is no 100% score in careers -- someone is always better at something, so perpetual learning is the only sustainable advantage.
- To build zero-to-one within a large company, zig when others zag. At Facebook, Deb took on payments, games, and mobile ads -- areas the core product teams were not fighting over. She leveraged existing relationships (game companies from payments work) to build the first direct-response ad product.
- When Mark Zuckerberg told her she would "never" get the role she wanted, she took the job she had and turned it into the job she wanted. The raw materials of what you have can be transformed into what you want -- it is a choice.
- Introverts succeed by reframing self-promotion as "educating people about the great work your team has been doing." Sharing work is not self-promotion; it is ensuring your team gets the resources it deserves.
