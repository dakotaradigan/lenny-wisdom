# Shweta Shrivastava

Senior Director of Product Management at Waymo, leading teams responsible for on-board autonomous driving software, simulation tools, and commercial ride-hailing scaling. Previously CPO at Nauto (driver safety AI), Head of PM at AWS (databases/analytics), and at Cisco.

## Key Frameworks

### Safety-First MVP
The concept of MVP has a different meaning when safety is paramount. The minimum bar for safety must be extremely high before any deployment. You cannot cut corners on safety to ship faster. The MVP bar itself is the safety benchmark -- then iterate on everything else.

### Trust Through Naturalness
Self-driving cars use human driving data to train deep models so behavior feels natural, not robotic. The system understands pedestrian body orientation, gestures, and even social norms (jaywalking patterns vary by city intersection). After 5 minutes in a Waymo, riders report it feels completely natural -- this is by design, not happenstance.

## Core Ideas

- Two metric categories for autonomous vehicles: commercial/operational (trips, DAU, cost to operate) and system behavior (safety vs. human benchmark, stops and strands, compliance with road rules)
- The car must be both safe AND make adequate progress -- being ultra-cautious to the point of not moving serves no one; balancing assertiveness with safety is key
- Subtle behavioral details matter enormously: the car slows down going downhill in San Francisco even though it could safely maintain speed, because human drivers instinctively slow and riders expect it
- PMs in autonomous vehicles must go technically deeper than in pure software, be comfortable with extreme ambiguity, and have tenacity for the long game
