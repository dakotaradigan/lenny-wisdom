# Brendan Foody

CEO and co-founder of Mercor, the fastest-growing company in history to go from $1 to $500M in revenue (17 months). Youngest unicorn founder ever. Mercor helps AI labs hire experts to train models using AI-powered hiring and assessment.

## Key Frameworks

### Evals as PRDs
If the model is the product, the eval is the product requirement document. Labs' primary bottleneck is measuring what success looks like for models. Evals serve as both the test (benchmark) and the sales collateral demonstrating capabilities.

### Elastic vs Inelastic Demand
Focus on domains where 10x productivity increase creates 10x more demand, not domains with fixed demand. Software development is highly elastic (unlimited demand for more features). Accounting is relatively inelastic (we don't need 100x more accounting).

## Core Ideas

- The AI training market shifted from crowdsourced low-skill labeling to sourcing and vetting elite professionals (lawyers, doctors, engineers) who can evaluate model capabilities
- Reinforcement learning from AI feedback (RLAIF) is replacing RLHF; humans define success criteria, then AI scales the training process
- The entire economy may become a reinforcement learning environment, creating a massive new category of jobs alongside displacement
- Skills that matter most: leveraging AI tools ambitiously, being willing to learn new tools, and operating in elastic-demand industries
- In hiring, assess candidates by what they can build with AI tools in an hour, not by fighting against tool usage

## Notable Perspectives

> "The wealthiest companies in the world are willing to spend whatever it takes to improve model capabilities."

> "The narrative in AI over the last three years has almost entirely been one of job displacement, but very few companies and people have talked about this new category of jobs that's being created."
