# Camille Ricketts

First marketing hire at Notion (employee #11) and longtime head of marketing. Previously head of content at First Round Capital where she launched First Round Review. Early career at Tesla sitting next to Elon Musk doing communications.

## Key Frameworks

### Content-Market Fit
Just like product-market fit, you need content-market fit. Identify your audience, understand what they need to get promoted, avoid failure, or reduce anxiety. Create content that addresses those needs, not just content adjacent to your product.

## Core Ideas

- Notion's early culture was like a home: shoes off, beautiful furnishings, tea on couches, working late with headlamps when the lights were bad
- At First Round Capital, the Review became a powerful content engine by deeply serving the startup community with practical knowledge
- Working with Elon taught the importance of being incredibly well-versed in every possible topic that could come up, and painting an emotional picture of vision
- Building community around a product requires genuine passion for serving that community, not just marketing tactics
- Early-stage marketing is about creating genuine connection and letting the product's quality drive organic word of mouth

## Notable Perspectives

> "The way that you think about product market fit, you have to think about content market fit. What is it that they need in their lives? What causes them a great deal of anxiety?"
