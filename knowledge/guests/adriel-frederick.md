# Adriel Frederick

Adriel Frederick is VP of Product at Reddit, where he focuses on incubating new products. He was previously director of product at Lyft leading marketplace and pricing teams, and an early PM at Facebook where he was the first Black product manager and led user acquisition.

## Key Frameworks

### R&D Team Integration
Three principles for innovation teams within larger organizations: (1) the work must be seen as core and critical to the mission, not a side project; (2) success must be shared across the organization; (3) other teams must still feel empowered to innovate, so the R&D team isn't seen as hoarding all the fun work.

### Navigating Product Controversy
As a PM, provide a damping effect on your team through both highs and lows. Separate valid criticism from power-structure complaints by staying close to users. Go use your own product in the field to understand real pain points.

## Core Ideas

- Diverse teams are dramatically more efficient at building global products because they can resolve design debates in 15 minutes that would otherwise require weeks of user research panels
- The assumption that one phone number equals one person equals one device is false in much of the world, and this shaped Facebook's registration design
- When criticism hits, "plan the work, work the plan, go back to your job" - focus on solving for customers rather than responding to press
- Retain diverse talent first; when people see the environment values their perspective and rewards it, recruiting becomes much easier

## Notable Perspectives

> "The algorithms don't understand long term effects often, nor do they understand how people might respond to it, nor do they understand your intent for the product. That is our job."

> "Some controversy is necessary. You're not going to have any meaningful influence on the world without changing some pattern of behavior."
