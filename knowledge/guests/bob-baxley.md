# Bob Baxley

Designer, executive, and advisor who built and led design teams at Apple, Pinterest, Yahoo, and ThoughtSpot over three decades. Played a pivotal role in designing the Apple Online Store, Apple App Store, and Pinterest.

## Key Frameworks

### Design as Mindset, Not Function
Design is "clear thinking made visible" and "trying to imagine the future you want to live in and take the steps to make it real." A company being design-led does not mean designer-led. Design is a holistic mindset any role can hold.

### Values vs. Behaviors in Culture Transitions
When moving between companies, hold onto the values of your previous culture (attention to detail, product excellence) but recalibrate the behaviors to fit the new environment. Give yourself time to go through the "car wash" between cultures.

### Scenius and Small Teams
Brian Eno's concept of "scenius" (collective genius) works best with small groups. The original Mac had 20 people on the patent, the iPhone had 24. You get The Beatles with four people, not with eight or twenty-four.

## Core Ideas

- Design culture cannot be grafted onto a company after founding; it must be in the root DNA from the beginning
- Design should potentially report to engineering as "phase zero of the engineering process" rather than sitting under product
- Keep new product teams small; scale headcount only after you have clarity of vision
- Career failure at senior levels is the common case, not the exception; the branch gets flimsier the higher you climb
- A startup is still a startup until the founder moves aside; you only discover if the culture sustains itself once founders are gone

## Notable Perspectives

> "Almost everyone living in a modern economy now is going to have hundreds of interactions with a phone or with a computer. We have an obligation as product people to put that emotional energy back into people's lives."

> "Saying a company is design-led does not mean it's designer-led. I've never seen somebody graft it on after the fact. It's there at the beginning in the root DNA or doesn't exist."
