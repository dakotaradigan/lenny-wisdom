# Matt MacInnis

Chief Product Officer and former longtime COO at Rippling (valued at $16B+, 5000+ employees). Previously spent seven years at Apple under Steve Jobs and nine years as founder/CEO of Inkling. Transitioned from COO to CPO at Rippling to fix product organization challenges firsthand.

## Key Frameworks

### Alpha vs. Beta in Organizations
Borrowed from finance: alpha is outperformance, beta is volatility. Processes exist solely to lower beta (reduce volatility in output) but they suppress alpha (innovation). Apply process judiciously: high process for payroll (low beta needed), light process for zero-to-one products (alpha needed). The Product Quality List (PQL) at Rippling is a lightweight checklist that lowers beta with minimal alpha suppression.

### Deliberate Understaffing
When you cannot get staffing exactly right, it is always better to understaff than overstaff. Overstaffing creates politics, work on low-priority items, and cruft. Understaffing forces ruthless prioritization. The wisdom is knowing not to under-understaff -- finding the line between productive constraint and failure.

## Core Ideas

- Extraordinary results require extraordinary efforts; if you find yourself in the comfort zone at work, something is wrong
- Good teams get tired and that is when great teams kick their asses; the organizational engine must run at the red line because any crack invites competition
- Withholding feedback is fundamentally selfish -- you are optimizing for your own comfort at the expense of someone else's growth
- You learn far more from success than from failure; join winning teams to compound learning
- When someone uses a buzzword like "strategy," ask them to explain what they mean without using that word; if they fumble, they have not thought it through
- Every concentric circle of management beyond the founder CEO is a potential order-of-magnitude drop in intensity; the executive's job is to preserve that intensity
- Create idiosyncratic terms (PQL, not "quality checklist") to build culture and make concepts stick
