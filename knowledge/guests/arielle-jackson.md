# Arielle Jackson

Arielle Jackson spent nine years at Google helping grow Gmail in its early days, then was one of the first marketers at Square where she launched the Square Stand. She's worked with over 100 early-stage companies on brand and marketing as a marketer in residence at First Round Capital, including Patreon, Loom, Front, and Eero.

## Key Frameworks

### Brand Names Over Time
A name doesn't need to be perfect - it just needs to not be actively harmful. Over time, a word comes to mean something beyond its literal meaning (Disney = magic, Volvo = safety). A bad name with great execution will be fine; a good name won't save a bad company.

### Product Launch as Movement
Launching physical products requires orchestrating supply chain, retail partnerships, and experiential marketing simultaneously. Seed products with the coolest venues in key markets to create aspiration and social proof before broad distribution.

## Core Ideas

- Gmail's growth from side project to hundreds of millions of users required brand-building at every stage
- Square's shift from free card readers to the premium Square Stand required repositioning the entire company as an up-market point-of-sale solution
- For early-stage companies, the marketing function often doesn't exist yet; the first marketer needs to be willing to do everything from positioning to event logistics
- The most fun and memorable launches combine physical presence, aspirational placement, and compelling storytelling

## Notable Perspectives

> "A bad name with a really great company with great strategy, great marketing is going to be great over time. A good name is just going to help you, but a bad name is not going to kill a good company."
