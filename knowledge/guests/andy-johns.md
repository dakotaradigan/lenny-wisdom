# Andy Johns

Andy Johns was a legendary product and growth leader at Facebook, Twitter, Quora, and Wealthfront (VP of Growth, VP of Product, President). At the peak of his career, making seven figures as a VC partner, he walked away to focus on mental health advocacy after severe burnout, panic attacks, and a heart scare. He now helps burned-out high achievers and veterans.

## Key Frameworks

### Four Steps of Deep Personal Transformation
(1) Suffering - deep transitions rarely happen without it; (2) Seeking truth - understanding why you suffer by digging through your subconscious and history; (3) Self-compassion - realizing your suffering isn't your fault and old adaptations have gone too far; (4) Compassion for others - once you understand yourself, you see the same patterns in everyone.

### Flashing Red Alarms
When fundamental functions get disrupted - sleep, relationships, physical health, socialization - it's an undeniable sign something detrimental to your wellbeing is happening. These are the body's signals to stop and change.

## Core Ideas

- At least 50-60% of tech employees with 5-7+ years experience are experiencing some form of psychological and emotional distress
- Achievement addiction often stems from childhood adaptations that were initially beneficial but eventually go too far and become detrimental
- The slow accumulation of career pressure is like the frog boiling in the pot - you don't realize how bad things are until it happens quickly
- Early life adaptations that save you (tying self-worth to achievement) can reverse course and become the source of your suffering
- Walking away from a peak career can be an act of self-love, not running from something but running back toward yourself

## Notable Perspectives

> "When their core behaviors of diet, exercise, playfulness, socialization, sleep get disrupted, it's a sign that there is something going on here that you need to take a look at."

> "I wasn't running from something, I was running back towards myself. That was an act of kindness towards myself."
