# Brian Tolkin

Head of Product and Design at Opendoor. Previously spent nearly five years at Uber as employee #100, starting in ops, moving to product, leading uberPOOL launch and global expansion, and creating the product operations function.

## Key Frameworks

### Twin Turbine Jet Plane
Product and operations are like twin turbines: you can fly on one engine briefly, but the plane operates most efficiently when both work together. Ops teams iterate faster, scale customer conversations, and provide qualitative insights that remote PMs cannot.

### Do Things That Don't Scale, Then Scale Them
Uber onboarding evolved from 90-minute individual sessions to small classrooms to videos to fully automated OCR driver verification. Each operational stage validates what to automate next, freeing ops teams to tackle the next frontier.

## Core Ideas

- Starting in operations gives deep understanding of how a business actually works; it's a strong foundation for product leadership
- Surge pricing was initially manual with human-controlled parameters per city; GMs toggled it for known events like baseball games
- UberX was a placeholder name (X for unknown) that stuck because the company moved too fast to rename it
- Product reviews should aim to help teams think better, not feel like a firing squad; two goals are accountability and making the product better
- Staying calm under pressure produces better outcomes; reflecting stress onto teams counterintuitively degrades performance

## Notable Perspectives
