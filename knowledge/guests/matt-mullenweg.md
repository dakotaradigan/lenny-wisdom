# Matt Mullenweg

Co-creator of WordPress (powers 40%+ of websites globally) and CEO of Automattic ($7B+ valuation, 1700+ employees across 90 countries). Has been building WordPress since age 19, now over 20 years. Angel investor through Audrey Capital with investments including Stripe and SpaceX. Co-owner of Keys jazz club in San Francisco.

## Key Frameworks

### Open Source Flywheel
When an open source project reaches critical mass, it creates positive feedback loops similar to Linux or Wikipedia. WordPress grew from blogging software to powering 40% of the web through community contributions, plugin ecosystems, and the compounding effect of being the default platform. The number two platform (Shopify) has roughly 4% market share.

### Distributed-First as Superpower
Automattic has been fully distributed and asynchronous from day one (not a pandemic adaptation). Operating across 90 countries forces written communication, explicit documentation, and true flexibility -- which Mullenweg considers a structural advantage over office-centric competitors.

## Core Ideas

- Products like Meta's Llama are "fake open source" because they restrict commercial use and do not meet true open source criteria
- Running a social network (Tumblr) is the hardest thing Automattic has attempted; content moderation on social platforms is an entirely different challenge than managing WordPress
- When acquiring companies, look for products that align with open web and open source values and can benefit from Automattic's distributed infrastructure
- AI models are trained on open source code, creating interesting questions about reciprocity and the future of the open source ecosystem
- Philanthropy should take a barbell approach: work on fundamental needs at the bottom of Maslow's hierarchy and also on things that raise the soul, like public art
