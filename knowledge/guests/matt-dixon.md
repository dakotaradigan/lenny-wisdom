## Key Frameworks

### FOMU Over FOMO
56% of no-decision losses come from customers who want to buy but cannot because of indecision rooted in fear of failure. They are not afraid of missing out (FOMO); they are afraid of messing up (FOMU). Dialing up urgency and scare tactics backfires 87% of the time with indecisive customers because you are trying to scare someone who is already afraid -- just afraid of the wrong thing.

### The JOLT Method
(J) Judge their level of indecision. (O) Offer a recommendation -- reduce choice overload by narrowing options. (L) Limit the exploration -- build trust so the customer stops relitigating concerns. (T) Take risk off the table -- de-risk the deal with guarantees, phased rollouts, or success milestones.

### The Challenger Approach
Most salespeople try to discover what keeps the customer up at night. Challengers show the customer what should be keeping them up at night -- risks they do not know about but you do. Teach customers something new about their business, tailor insights to their specific situation, and take control of the conversation.

## Core Ideas

- 40-60% of qualified pipeline is lost to no decision, not to competitors; this is rising in SaaS
- Status quo bias and indecision are two different problems requiring two different sales approaches
- Three sources of customer fear: have I made the right choice, will I get the outcome I expect, and will it be too painful to implement
- Even large incumbents are vulnerable to customer indecision; brand strength does not eliminate FOMU
- Instill confidence that the customer will look like a hero, not a fool, for choosing you
