# Barbra Gago

Barbra Gago was CMO at Miro where she led the rebrand and helped create a new software category. She previously brought Greenhouse to market as VP of Marketing and was head of marketing at Culture Amp, closing their first 50 customers. She's now building Pando, an employee progression platform.

## Key Frameworks

### Category Creation Decision Framework
When creating a new category: (1) Validate it with analysts and directory sites; (2) Build thought leadership around why the category exists and what problems it solves; (3) Educate buyers that there's a new budget category they should allocate for. This requires sustained investment in content and market education.

### Opinionated Software
Build software that takes a strong point of view on how work should be done rather than trying to be everything to everyone. An opinionated product attracts passionate users who become evangelists because the product aligns with their beliefs.

## Core Ideas

- Category creation requires validation, thought leadership, and buyer education simultaneously - it's not just about having a new product
- Rebranding (Miro was formerly RealtimeBoard) can reposition a company from niche tool to category leader when timed with a strategic expansion
- The first 50 customers often come from the founders' and marketing leaders' personal networks and intensive outbound effort
- When creating a category, you're not just selling a product - you're selling the idea that this type of solution should exist and deserves budget
- Building in people/HR tech means solving both a technology problem and a behavior change problem
