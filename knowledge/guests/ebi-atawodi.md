# Ebi Atawodi

Product leader with experience at YouTube, Netflix, and Uber. Known for her distinctive framework for crafting compelling product visions and her philosophy that effective leadership requires choosing love over likability -- extending yourself for others' growth even when that means having hard conversations.

## Key Frameworks

### Three Elements of Vision
A product vision must be: (1) Lofty -- it should scare you in an exciting way, something worth getting up for every morning. (2) Realistic and attainable -- not so pie-in-the-sky that it feels out of reach. (3) Created in a vacuum from today's limitations -- the whole point is to time-travel to the future and come back to say what needs to change now. These elements must be grounded in a problem people are excited about.

### Love Over Like in Leadership
Being liked is fundamentally different from being loved. Love is the choice to extend yourself for the spiritual growth of oneself or another. This means having raw, hard conversations because you genuinely care about the person behind the role. When people know you care, they receive even the hardest feedback as being in their best interest.

## Core Ideas

- Every PM at every level needs a vision -- even junior PMs. If a pilot gets on the intercom and says they do not know where the plane is going, passengers would panic. Vision is the destination that gives everyone confidence.
- Vision is not mission. Mission is "we want to fly people where they are going safely." Vision is "our destination is Miami and it will be 24 degrees when we get there." Vision paints a specific, vivid picture of the future.
- The vision should feel like time travel: go five years out, see what the world looks like if everything went right, then come back and report on what needs to happen now to get there.
- Do not confuse vision with strategy, goals, or mission. Each serves a different purpose, and conflating them weakens all of them.
