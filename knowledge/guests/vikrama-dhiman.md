# Vikrama Dhiman

Product leader at Gojek, one of the most recommended product leaders in Asia. Known for building exceptionally strong product and design talent, and helping people transition into product management from other roles. Created a PM career growth framework used widely in Southeast Asian tech.

## Key Frameworks

### Three W's of PM Career Growth
What you produce (your artifacts and output), what you bring to the table (your skills and perspectives), and what is your operating model (how you work). Strong PMs excel at two of three; PMs on a rapid rise perform well on all three simultaneously. Working on a cool product does not guarantee growth -- these three dimensions do.

### Artifact-Based Impact
Can you show your last PRD? Your last product note? Your product strategy doc? Impact must be demonstrated through tangible artifacts. Early in your career, focus on getting stuff out and done -- producing visible, high-quality work product that speaks for itself.

### Three Blockers to Career Growth
How you view change (resistance vs. embrace), whether you focus on things you control (vs. things you cannot), and how you see yourself (self-limiting narratives). Correcting these three stories can put someone back on the growth path immediately.

## Core Ideas

- Working on a high-profile product area does not guarantee career growth; some PMs on unglamorous products grow faster because they master the three W's
- Early-career PMs should optimize for output and getting things done; strategy and influence come later
- Transitioning into product management from other roles is viable if you can demonstrate the core skills through artifacts and operating model
- The best product leaders in Asia combine deep customer empathy with rigorous execution in fast-moving, complex market environments
- Product management is ultimately about the artifacts you produce, the skills you bring, and how effectively you operate day to day

## Notable Perspectives
