She also oversaw Instagram ads introduction and led product and design for WhatsApp.

## Key Frameworks

### Dinosaur Brain
Executives have "little brontosaurus brains" that can only hold three facts at once. Your job as a PM is to make the recommendation, not present all the information. The exec provides breadth, pattern matching, and cross-company context; you provide depth and the recommendation. These roles are complementary.

### Curiosity as Disagreement Resolution
When you viscerally disagree, reinterpret that reaction as a chance to learn rather than a threat to shut down. Take a pause, then respond with genuine curiosity: "Fascinating, tell me more why you think that." This tears down walls between opposing views.

### Put on the Coat of the Job
When choosing a career move, don't use spreadsheets - imagine wearing the job. What would you think about on your commute? Who would you have lunch with? The emotional response is more telling than analytical comparison.

## Core Ideas

- "My manager owns context, I own the recommendation" - this division of labor is the most productive relationship between a PM and their leader
- It's more important to get to the right outcome than to be right; sublimate your ego and you'll learn more and get better results
- Being messy and imperfect is compatible with being very successful - nobody has it fully figured out
- Trust is the biggest unlock for career performance; seek environments where you feel at home and can take risks
