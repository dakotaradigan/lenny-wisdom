# Upasna Gautam

Product Manager at CNN Digital leading the team responsible for the content management system that journalists use to write and publish stories. Longtime meditation and mindfulness teacher. Active in the News Product Alliance, elevating PM practice within newsrooms. Specializes in building product in chaotic, breaking-news environments.

## Key Frameworks

### Four Touch Points for Editorial Collaboration
A structured system for managing intake from journalist stakeholders: weekly demo days (show-and-tell of features), working sessions (collaborative workflow recreation replacing "user testing"), breaking news dress rehearsals (scripted simulations to stress-test the platform), and office hours (open blocks for troubleshooting and feedback).

### Equanimity as PM Superpower
Mental calmness and composure under pressure, especially the ability to pause before reacting. Reactivity is the root cause of many workplace frustrations. In news product work, plans get blown up by breaking news constantly -- equanimity lets you pivot without emotional derailment and serve as a compass in the chaos for your team.

### Buffer-Based Planning
Build systematic buffers into every plan because your stakeholders (journalists) will be pulled away by breaking news unpredictably. Sometimes the buffer is a day, sometimes a month. Quick assessment of whether to use the buffer or accelerate is a daily practice.

## Core Ideas

- To be a successful PM in news, you must thrive in chaos, not just ambiguity; breaking news can derail any planned session at any moment and your users owe you nothing -- they have a different job to do
- Earning editorial trust through collaborative product discovery means that when you say "not viable," journalists accept it because they have been along for the ride
- Legacy platform migration in news organizations is a years-long effort requiring extensive training, testing, and feedback loops before actual onboarding
- Smart repetition across different formats (demos, sessions, office hours) is critical for evangelizing a new platform within a change-resistant organization
- Real power comes from managing your emotional reactions, not from trying to control others
