# Matt Mochary

Full-time CEO coach to founders of OpenAI, Coinbase, Reddit, Rippling, Notion, and partners at Sequoia, YC, and Benchmark. Author of The Great CEO Within. Creator of the Mochary Method Curriculum. Founded Totality during the dot-com era, then spent years doing social good before returning to tech through coaching.

## Key Frameworks

### Fear Gives Bad Advice
When gripped by fear, the brain makes exaggerated predictions. The solution: check with someone not in fear, who can see clearly. Mochary bets CEOs that acting against their fear will produce the opposite outcome from what they predict -- and has never lost. The most common fear pattern: not telling the board about problems. Every time a CEO shares transparently, the board says "this is one of the only companies that is actually honest with us" and trust increases.

### Firing as the Most Important Leadership Skill
Most managers fire too late and never discover the true performance bar. Until you have let someone go and regretted it, you do not know where the line is. Fire with massive compassion: deliver the news one-on-one (never in groups or email), then become their agent helping them find their dream job. Fewer people in the organization means less overhead, faster information flow, and better morale.

## Core Ideas

- Anger is not a base emotion; it is a cover for pain. The real answer is to feel the pain rather than externalizing it onto others
- Use the phrase "I perceive you to be in anger/fear" rather than "you're angry" -- it is an I-statement that punches through defensiveness
- The ideal organization is the smallest possible team; every additional person creates geometrically more overhead in context-sharing and morale management
- Test radical ideas in your own organization before prescribing them to others; about half will be wildly successful and half complete duds
- The best layoff marker: did the person hear it from their manager in a one-on-one? If yes, it will be okay. If via email or group, expect backlash.
