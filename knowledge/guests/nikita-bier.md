# Nikita Bier

Has built, launched, and helped more apps reach the top of the App Store than almost anyone. Sold TBH to Facebook for $30M+ and Gas to Discord. Built 15+ consumer apps over five years before finding breakthrough success. Now advises companies on viral growth, design, and product development.

## Key Frameworks

### Latent Demand Discovery
Search for situations where people are going through a distortive process to obtain a particular value. The strongest signal for TBH was an Arabic-language app (Sarahah) being #1 in the US App Store -- proof of overwhelming unmet demand. If you can crystallize what users are actually trying to do, you unlock explosive adoption.

### Every Tap Is a Miracle
On mobile, users switch between apps at high frequency. Every single tap a user makes in your app is precious and should be optimized. This ultra-high-bar design philosophy forces ruthless prioritization of what matters in each screen and interaction.

### Reproducible Testing Process
Develop a systematic way to test consumer product ideas at high density. Seed apps into specific communities (schools, affinity groups) to eliminate confounding variables around whether users had enough friends or reached the aha moment. The testing process influences success probability more than any individual idea.

## Core Ideas

- For every year of age from 13 to 18, the number of invitations sent per user drops 20%; if you build for adults, expect to acquire every user with ads
- TBH's breakthrough was constraining anonymous messaging to positive-only polls, solving the core desire to hear good things about yourself while eliminating the bullying that kills every anonymous app
- Geofencing a viral app to control growth is counterintuitive but essential for survival -- if you cannot keep the servers online, the opportunity is wasted
- Put live chat customer support in your app 24/7 not because it scales, but because it is the best vehicle for real-time user research and makes users feel heard enough to promote your app positively
