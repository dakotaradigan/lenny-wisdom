## Key Frameworks

### Build for Where Models Are Going
The models will eat your scaffolding for breakfast. Do not over-invest in workarounds for current model limitations -- build for future capabilities. "This is the worst the models will ever be" (Kevin Weil). Listening to current customer pain points can mislead because the field and models change faster than customer feedback cycles.

### Engineers as Fleet Managers
Engineers are becoming tech leads managing fleets of AI agents rather than writing code directly. "It literally feels like we're wizards casting spells that go out and do things for you." Engineers who use Codex more open 70% more PRs, and the gap is widening as they learn to leverage the tool.

## Core Ideas

- At OpenAI, 95% of engineers use Codex daily; 100% of PRs are reviewed by Codex; engineers who adopt most heavily open 70% more PRs
- One team at OpenAI maintains a 100% Codex-written codebase as an experiment -- they cannot roll up their sleeves and debug manually, forcing them to solve the problem of agent-generated code quality
- The second and third order effects of the one-person billion-dollar startup: it may spawn hundreds of small startups building bespoke software, potentially creating a golden age of B2B SaaS
- There is emerging "agent anxiety" -- stress people feel when their agents are not working, similar to how people feel when their internet goes down
