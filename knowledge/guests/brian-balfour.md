# Brian Balfour

Founder and CEO of Reforge. Previously led growth at HubSpot. Has seen the rise and fall of every major distribution channel including Facebook, Google, and the App Store. Predicts ChatGPT as the next major distribution platform.

## Key Frameworks

### Four-Step Platform Cycle
Step 0: Market conditions met (5-7 competitors, no clear winner). Step 1: Someone identifies the moat and opens a third-party platform to gather it faster. Step 2: Ecosystem grows with generous distribution. Step 3: Platform closes for monetization and control. This cycle repeated with Facebook, Google, iOS, and will repeat with AI.

### Distribution Before Copying
Startups is a game of getting distribution before the incumbent can copy. Building a great product is necessary but not sufficient. The separation between winners and losers is great distribution.

## Core Ideas

- AI has been a technology shift without a distribution shift so far; distribution shifts lag technology shifts but are the most impactful
- The cycles are getting shorter, giving companies less time to exploit new platforms; there is no opting out of the game
- Late-stage companies should place multiple distribution bets; startups must choose one and go all in
- Every platform eventually closes: suppressing organic reach, absorbing first-party use cases, or pushing toward paid mechanisms
- It's not that platforms are evil; competitive and capitalistic dynamics make this cycle inevitable

## Notable Perspectives

> "Building a great product is one of those things that's necessary, but not sufficient. And actually the separation is between those that build really great distribution."

> "If you don't do it, your competitors are going to go to the new platform and your customer expectations change. There is no opting out of the game."
