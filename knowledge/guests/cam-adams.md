# Cameron Adams

Co-founder and Chief Product Officer at Canva, a $2.3B ARR company that's larger than Figma, Miro, and Webflow combined. Previously a designer on Google Wave. Canva has been profitable for 7 years while growing 60%+ year over year.

## Key Frameworks

### Coaches Not Managers
Canva replaced traditional managers with coaches. Every employee has a coach from the same specialty who helps develop skills, identifies growth opportunities, and pushes people beyond comfort zones. Nearly 800-1000 coaches across the company.

### Giving Away Your Legos
As a startup scales, individuals must hand off work they love to focus on higher-leverage activities. The first email writer can't write every email for 100M users in 100 languages. Find joy in building teams and passing on experience.

## Core Ideas

- Canva prioritized profitability from early on so they'd never be in a position where they have to raise money on someone else's terms
- Almost all Canva leaders are homegrown; external hires who succeed listen for months before trying to change processes
- The type of product you build influences how you think: Spotify thinks in audio (discussions), Canva thinks in visuals (mockups and prototypes)
- Product managers at Canva are "connectives" linking teams, ideas, data, and constraints; the title wasn't used until year 6 or 7
- Word-of-mouth from delightful experiences was Canva's biggest growth driver for years; they held off launching until the experience created genuine joy
