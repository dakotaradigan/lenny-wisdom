## Key Frameworks

### AEO vs SEO: Head and Tail Differences
Everything that works in SEO works in AEO, but AEO has additional dimensions. The head is different: in Google, the top blue link wins; in LLMs, the answer summarizes many citations, so you need to be mentioned as many times as possible across citations, not just rank first. The tail is larger: average queries are 25 words in chat vs 6 in search, creating more long-tail opportunities.

### Citation Frequency Over Citation Rank
To win head terms in LLMs (e.g., "What's the best website builder?"), the first answer is typically the one mentioned most frequently across all citations. Get mentioned in YouTube videos, Reddit threads, blog posts, and affiliate content -- breadth of citation matters more than ranking position of any single citation.

### Early-Stage AEO Advantage
Unlike traditional SEO which requires domain authority built over years (usually Series A+), AEO allows early-stage companies to win immediately. A Reddit thread, a YouTube video, or blog mentions can surface a brand-new company in ChatGPT answers tomorrow. This fundamentally changes the timing of when startups should invest in search visibility.

## Core Ideas

- AEO is the second-biggest change in SEO history, after Google's anti-spam algorithm updates; it is significant but not the end of traditional SEO
- Google's slice of the pie stays the same while the overall pie gets bigger with AI search
- LLM-driven traffic converts at significantly higher rates than Google search traffic; Webflow saw a 6X conversion rate difference
- Anything can be optimized if you understand the underlying systems; but spamming AI answers is not a durable strategy -- labs will detect and penalize it
- The three most effective AEO tactics are traditional SEO content, YouTube videos, and Reddit optimization
