# Tamar Yehoshua

President of Product and Technology at Glean. Former CPO at Slack where she led product, design, and research through IPO and Salesforce acquisition. Led Google Search product and engineering teams for years. Director of Engineering and VP at Amazon's A9.com. Venture partner at IVP. Board member at ServiceNow, Snyk, and others.

## Key Frameworks

### Follow People, Not Domains
Career decisions should optimize for learning from exceptional individuals rather than chasing specific domains or financial returns. Find people who are the best at what they do and work alongside them. Skills acquired this way cannot be taken away even if a company fails. Evaluate opportunities by asking whether joining will make your hires' careers, not just yours.

### Engineering Partnership Alignment
The most important factor for a product leader's success is the quality of their engineering counterpart. Evaluate this before joining. Establish clear ownership boundaries, do joint reviews, never let teams play "ask Mom, ask Dad." At Slack, she and CTO Cal Henderson did all OKR reviews together, used async video updates when team scale made live reviews impractical, and had weekly four-person meetings with their chiefs of staff.

### Design for Tomorrow's Users
Do not over-index on the vocal minority of existing users when evolving a product. The number of future users almost always exceeds current users. Design for the larger audience while being transparent and respectful with those affected by change. A personal phone call from a senior leader can transform a critic into an advocate.

## Core Ideas

- Do a great job at the job you currently have before chasing the next role; impact on the business is what matters, not merely completing assigned tasks
- Understanding people's motivations is a core leadership skill applicable to both building products and managing organizations; read the room, ask questions, observe reactions
- Companies do not need to be well run to win; strong product-market fit covers many operational sins, especially during hyper-growth, though chaos is not an excuse to stop striving for a functioning organization
- AI will blur the lines between PMs, designers, and engineers; the creative and strategic PMs will thrive while purely execution-oriented roles face displacement
- When building AI products, do not build compensations for current LLM limitations as your differentiator; ensure your product improves as models improve
