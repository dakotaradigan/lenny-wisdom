# Nikhyl Singhal

Product leader at Meta overseeing groups, stories, messaging, and feed for the Facebook app. Previously CPO at Credit Karma and held leadership roles at Google (Hangouts, Photos). Co-founded three startups. Prolific mentor to hundreds of PMs through his newsletter and podcast The Skip, focused on helping product people navigate career transitions.

## Key Frameworks

### The Skip (Job After Next)
Think not about your next job but the one after it. Work backwards from that end state to determine whether your current role and next move serve that trajectory. This prevents lateral moves disguised as forward progress and short-term thinking driven by boss frustration or promotion anxiety.

### Five Types of Product Ambiguity
PMs should develop world-class expertise in at least one: (1) Craft excellence, (2) Market ambiguity, (3) Organizational ambiguity, (4) Domain expertise, (5) Team leadership, or (6) Growth. Pick a lane early and build a story around it for future employers.

### Four Promotion Blockers
(1) No advocate who sees your magic -- change your setting. (2) The next role does not exist -- especially common post-hypergrowth. (3) Impatience -- leadership promotions are slower by design. (4) A real development gap that you or your manager cannot identify clearly.

## Core Ideas

- Ex-growth companies (high valuations but still searching for product-market fit) are career traps: if the sucking sound of customer pull is missing and the valuation is hundreds of millions, employees should leave
- Collecting fancy company logos on your resume is shallow; the real value is getting a diverse set of experiences across different company stages and problem types
- Fear of finding another job is the worst reason to stay at a company, yet it is one of the most common
- The best way to pull honest feedback is to repeat it back better than the person said it, demonstrating you not only heard but deeply internalized it
