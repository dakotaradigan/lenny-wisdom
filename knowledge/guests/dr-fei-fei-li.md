# Dr. Fei-Fei Li

Known as the Godmother of AI. Created ImageNet, the dataset and challenge that sparked the deep learning revolution in 2012. Former Chief AI Scientist at Google Cloud, former Director of Stanford's AI Lab (SAIL), co-creator of Stanford's Human-Centered AI Institute, and co-founder of World Labs. Named one of Time's 100 Most Influential People in AI.

## Key Frameworks

### The Golden Recipe of Modern AI
Three ingredients combined to create the modern AI revolution: big data (ImageNet's 15 million labeled images), neural networks (deep learning architectures), and GPU computing. These same three ingredients, scaled up enormously, are still at the core of today's AI systems including ChatGPT -- internet-scale text data, more complex neural networks, and vastly more GPUs.

### Human-Centered AI
Technology is a double-edged sword. AI is a net positive for humanity based on the long arc of civilization, but it requires responsible individuals at every point of development and deployment. There is nothing artificial about AI -- it is inspired by people, created by people, and most importantly impacts people.

## Core Ideas

- The breakthrough insight behind ImageNet was recognizing that the field was obsessed with models but overlooked data. Human learning and evolution are fundamentally big-data learning processes -- animals evolve by experiencing the world constantly. AI needed the same volume of experience.
- As recently as 2015-2016, some tech companies avoided using the word "AI" because it was considered a dirty word. By 2017, companies began calling themselves AI companies. The cultural shift happened in just 1-2 years.
- Visual intelligence is foundational to general intelligence because humans are deeply visual animals. We interact with the world at the object level, and understanding objects requires learning from millions of examples of infinite visual possibilities.
- World models -- AI systems that understand spatial, physical, and three-dimensional reality -- represent the next frontier beyond language models. This is the focus of World Labs and the Marble large world model.
