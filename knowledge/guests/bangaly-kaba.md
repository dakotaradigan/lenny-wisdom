# Bangaly Kaba

Director of Product Management at YouTube. Previously Head of Growth at Instagram (scaled to 1B+ users), early growth PM at Facebook, and VP of Product at Instacart. Background in education before tech, which heavily influences his coaching-oriented leadership style.

## Key Frameworks

### Impact = Environment x Skills
A career decision framework where Impact is the output to optimize for (not compensation, which is a derivative). Environment includes six variables scored 0-2: manager, resources, scope, team, compensation, and company culture. Skills include communication, influence, leadership, strategic thinking, and execution. Score each annually to identify what is limiting your impact and whether you can change it.

### Understand Work (Understand, Identify, Execute)
The anti-pattern is "Identify, Justify, Execute" where someone says "let's build this," pulls data to justify it, then executes. Instead, dedicate intentional time to understand what is actually going on before building. Start at 40% understand / 60% execute when entering a new problem space, then shift to 15-20% understand / 80-85% execute as knowledge grows. This creates a velocity multiplier where each subsequent sprint has higher win rates.

### Adjacent User Theory
Growth comes from users just outside your current user base. As a product scales, new cohorts behave differently from early adopters -- they may be less tech-savvy, from different geographies, or have different use cases. Use the product as them (fresh account, their device, their context) to discover what is broken. At Instagram, cohort curves declining was the signal that adjacent users were arriving and the product was not serving them.

### Managing Complex Change
Five components required for organizational change: Vision, Skills, Incentives, Resources, and Action Plan. Missing any one produces a specific dysfunction: no vision leads to confusion, no skills leads to anxiety, no incentives leads to resistance, no resources leads to frustration, no action plan leads to false starts.

## Core Ideas

- Communication is the most important PM skill, disproportionately so; great communicators who recap decisions, name contributors, and crystallize action items build trust in every interaction
- Build a stable of 3-4 mentors meeting on different weeks, not just one mentor; find them through specific challenge-based referrals rather than cold "be my mentor" requests
- Growth is not overnight success; it requires compounding short-term wins toward long-term outcomes, and teams that slow down to understand before executing end up moving faster
- Instagram's early growth was driven not just by virality but by an underappreciated combination of celebrity partnerships, web presence for SEO, and layered growth loops reinforcing each other
- Use Bloom's Taxonomy to diagnose where PMs are stuck: do they lack knowledge, comprehension, application ability, or the capacity to synthesize and evaluate across business contexts?
