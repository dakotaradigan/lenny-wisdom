# Laura Modi

CEO and founder of Bobbie, the only female-founded organic infant formula company in the US. Previously director of hospitality at Airbnb where she led global host community and operations. Irish-born, she joined Airbnb as the company's only Irish employee and led consolidation of European offices in Dublin.

## Key Frameworks

### Slowth (Slow Growth)
When Bobbie's inventory was being depleted faster than it could be replenished during the 2022 formula crisis, the team turned off their website for six months and rebranded the growth team as the "slowth team." The principle: protect your existing customers even at the expense of new growth, because trust compounds more powerfully than acquisition.

### Brand the Mundane
Internal processes and frameworks should be branded with memorable names to keep teams motivated and create recall. At Airbnb it was "Air Dives" for customer service analysis; at Bobbie it is "Project Shamrock," "Secret Shopping Program," and similar names that make the work feel purposeful rather than bureaucratic.

## Core Ideas

- Your brand must connect with one of the three things keeping your customer up at night; if you are not solving for one of those, you are just noise
- Never position your brand by pointing fingers at competitors or alternatives; only talk about what you do and why you exist
- Culture requires personal connections, not just professional ones; cross-functional teams should know each other's lives
- Energy is the currency of high-performing startups; storytelling is the mechanism that sustains it across the organization
- Taking big career leaps requires deep conviction built through deliberate research, not impulse
