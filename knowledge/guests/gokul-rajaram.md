# Gokul Rajaram

Product leader at DoorDash overseeing a significant part of the product and business. Board member at Coinbase, Pinterest, and The Trade Desk (all public companies). Prolific angel investor. Previously at Square, Facebook, and Google where he accidentally became the PM for Google AdSense by wandering into an engineer's office after hours and volunteering to be their PM "nights and weekends."

## Key Frameworks

### Serendipity as Career Strategy
Great careers are not built linearly through promotions. They are built by knowing a lot of people, doing great work so they want you on their teams, and waiting for serendipity then seizing it. Paying it forward creates a reservoir of goodwill that returns in unexpected ways. Maintain curiosity about what others at your company are working on.

### Choose the Winner in a Large Space
When selecting a company to join, prioritize being at the market leader in a large category over having a senior title at a second-tier player. Being an IC PM at Google beats being VP of Product at Yahoo. Winners attract better talent, create unfair brand halo for your career, and compound network effects.

## Core Ideas

- Get energy from the customer segment you serve, not just the problem domain; Gokul consistently thrives serving consumers and small businesses because he can relate to them personally
- The best founders are authentically mission-driven; if they constantly talk about revenue or getting wealthy, they probably will not build a truly large company
- New career entrants should join mid-stage companies (hundreds, not thousands of people) for the right balance of mentorship and real impact
- Product development at startups should be designed around small teams with clear ownership, rapid iteration, and direct customer feedback
- The Google AdSense origin story illustrates that the biggest career opportunities often come from volunteering for unglamorous side projects
