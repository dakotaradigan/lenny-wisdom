# Bret Taylor

Co-created Google Maps, invented the like button and real-time newsfeed at FriendFeed, served as CTO of Facebook, co-CEO of Salesforce, chairman of the board at OpenAI, and now co-founder/CEO of Sierra (AI agents for customer service).

## Key Frameworks

### What's the Most Impactful Thing Today?
Wake up every morning asking what is the most impactful thing you could do today. Don't conform the job to what you like doing; transform yourself into what the company needs. Sheryl Sandberg taught Bret this lesson at Facebook, and it changed his entire career trajectory.

### Don't Digitize, Reinvent
Google Local failed because it was a digital Yellow Pages. Google Maps succeeded because it created an entirely new experience native to the platform. When new technologies emerge, disassemble the Lego set and reassemble it into something new rather than digitizing what came before.

### Beware Single-Issue Voting
Founders naturally gravitate toward solutions in their area of expertise. Engineers think engineering solves everything; designers think a redesign will fix everything. Default to questioning solutions that align with your comfort zone.

## Core Ideas

- The whole AI market is going toward agents and outcomes-based pricing; it's so obviously the correct way to build and sell software
- FriendFeed lost to Twitter despite a better product because they focused exclusively on product while Twitter focused on distribution (getting celebrities on the platform)
- Computer science is still worth studying because operating code-generating machines requires systems thinking; the act of coding will change but the discipline remains vital
- Good judgment is the ultimate hiring criterion; there's not a strong correlation between confidence of opinion and quality of opinion
- When getting advice, don't just ask what to do, ask why; most advice is extrapolated from a single experience
