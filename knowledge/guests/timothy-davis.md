## Key Frameworks

### Signs of Life Test
Before committing significant budget to a new ad platform, run a small test with your own customer data. Upload your existing customer list to build lookalike audiences, starting at 1% similarity (most correlated) and expanding to 2-4%, 5-7%, and 8%+. If the 1% lookalike shows positive signals, you have validation to build a proper campaign.

### Platform-Specific Creative
Creative that works on one platform rarely translates directly to another because user behavior and context differ. Meta users are browsing photos of friends; YouTube users are watching long-form content; TikTok users expect raw, authentic video. Adapt creative to match the mindset of users on each platform rather than reusing assets across channels.

### Data-First Channel Selection
Start with your analytics to identify where users already find you organically. If a platform already drives traffic, paid investment amplifies an existing signal rather than creating demand from scratch. This is the highest-confidence way to choose where to invest.

## Core Ideas

- Paid search is for everyone because it is user-driven; the person types a relevant keyword, making it fundamentally different from disruptive media like social ads
- The biggest mistake agencies make is ego-driven bidding to be position one; instead optimize for showing to the right person as often as possible
- Focus groups remain valuable for understanding why creative does or does not resonate; platform metrics tell you what happened but not why
- Your first three hires in performance marketing should be a generalist who can run multiple platforms, a creative specialist, and an analyst
- It is okay to fail on a platform test; the only thing worse is not giving it an appropriate shot and prematurely concluding it does not work
