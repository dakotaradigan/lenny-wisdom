He spent 13 years at Google (Search, Chrome, Maps AR), was head of corporate strategy at Stripe, and is now founding a startup reimagining the web for the AI era. Known for his 600-page "Bits and Bobs" reflection document and his viral "slime mold" deck on organizational dynamics.

## Key Frameworks

### Gardener vs. Builder Mindset
Builder mindset: have a plan, manipulate things to match it. Can't create more value than effort invested. Gardener mindset: find things that can grow on their own, direct and curate them. When done properly, results look like magic and can create disproportionate value.

### Organizational Kayfabe
Borrowed from professional wrestling - things everyone knows are fake but acts like are real. Small amounts are valuable (optimism, enthusiasm), but at scale they compound into systemic dishonesty where status reports get whitewashed through layers of management.

### LLMs as Magical Duct Tape
LLMs are squishy computers that do roughly what you meant (not exactly what you said). They undermine assumptions that software is expensive to write and cheap to run. Design products assuming the tool is imperfect rather than trying to make it perfect.

## Core Ideas

- In the AI era, taste is the most important skill - differentiate from what an LLM would produce given the same prompt
- We're in the "community gardening" phase of AI, not the "factory farming" phase; curiosity and play matter more than execution playbooks
- Create dedicated time for reflection (Friday deep thinking) - the mundane will take every square inch you give it
- LLMs are "electric bikes for idea spaces" - they let you cover vastly more intellectual ground more quickly
