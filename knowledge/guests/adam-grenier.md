# Adam Grenier

Adam Grenier was head of Growth Marketing and Innovation at Uber, where he built their growth marketing infrastructure from the ground up. He then served as VP of Product and Marketing at Lambda School and VP of Marketing at MasterClass. He now advises companies on growth and marketing strategy.

## Key Frameworks

### Emerging Channel Evaluation Framework
Three-part framework for deciding whether to invest in a new acquisition channel: (1) overlap between customer needs, company goals, and channel strengths; (2) channel DNA including trajectory maturity, risk of change, and monetization strategy; (3) alignment with the channel's own business goals to unlock partnerships.

### Improv Principles for Business
"Yes, and..." builds collaboration instead of shutting down progress. The "gift of details" - providing specific context rather than vague agreement - gives teams more to build on and opens up richer problem-solving.

## Core Ideas

- When economic conditions change, start by assuming you no longer have product-market fit rather than just launching new channels
- Understand a channel's monetization strategy - if your goals align with theirs, you get preferential access and partnerships
- Being specific in communication (the "gift of details" from improv) is more valuable than vague agreement in cross-functional work
- Don't chase hot channels just because they're hot; evaluate whether the medium's strengths match your product and customer
