# Kayvon Beykpour

Longest-tenured and beloved head of product at Twitter, also GM of the consumer business. Arrived through acquisition of Periscope (world's largest live-streaming platform, inspiring Instagram Live, TikTok Live, Facebook Live). Under his leadership, Twitter shipped Twitter Blue, Spaces, Super Follows, Communities, newsletters, topics, Fleets, Community Notes, and extensive UX improvements -- transforming a notoriously stagnant product org.

## Key Frameworks

### Sacred Cows as Roadmap
When inheriting a stagnant product culture, ask: what are all the things everyone thinks we are not allowed to change? Start there. These perceived immovable constraints are often the highest-leverage opportunities for innovation and cultural transformation.

### Acqui-hires for Culture Injection
Use acquisitions of small, hungry teams to inject new energy into an established organization. These teams arrive with startup velocity and ambition, and can lead new initiatives that break through organizational inertia that existing teams cannot overcome.

### Functional vs. GM Structure
A functional organization (separate heads of product, engineering, design) under a leader who does not aggressively tiebreak creates consensus deadlock among opinionated peers. Shifting to a GM structure where one leader owns product, engineering, and design for a domain enables faster execution and clearer accountability.

## Core Ideas

- Changing product culture from within a functional org is possible but politically exhausting; it requires patience, consensus building, and willingness to invest a full year in political groundwork before execution accelerates
- Community Notes (originally Birdwatch) was the most promising approach to scalable content moderation because centralized editorial decisions were fundamentally unscalable
- When a strong-minded founder acquires a company, the product leader role becomes untenable -- if someone else has the canvas, it is their turn
- Getting fired on paternity leave was devastating but led to the silver lining of a year with his newborn daughter and missing subsequent organizational drama
