# Jason Shah

Product leader at Alchemy (blockchain infrastructure). Previously at Airbnb, Amazon (via acquisition of do.com), and Microsoft/Yammer. Founded a test prep company at 15 and ran it for seven years. Guest authored one of Lenny's most popular newsletter posts on Web3 product management.

## Key Frameworks

### Pushback as Alignment (Not Opposition)
The word "pushback" creates a combative mindset. Instead, reframe disagreement as shifting direction or helping the business succeed. The two most effective approaches: (1) understand the other person's actual goal, then align your concerns with that goal, and (2) present alternatives that achieve the same outcome through a different path.

### Amazon's PRFAQ Discipline
The press release and FAQ forces concrete, specific thinking. Amazon gives new employees a business writing card with five tips about concision and specificity. Rule: never write the word "great" -- instead write "user friendly in X way, saving customers 20 minutes daily." When people move from slides to docs, they often just import the same fluffy thinking with more words.

## Core Ideas

- Amazon was the most formative company despite being the shortest tenure (one year) because it forced you to blend product and business thinking inseparably. You couldn't be a PM without thinking about revenue, go-to-market, and M&A.
- The only way to maintain morale through market downturns (crypto winter, Airbnb COVID) is making visible progress. No speech or extrinsic motivator works as well as shipping and seeing results.
- Stage and team size labels are misleading. A 500-person company can feel like a seed-stage startup if the culture is tight. Don't over-index on company stage when choosing where to work.
- Web3 is maturing toward traditional product management. As products become more complex and competitive, the PM role becomes essential for strategy, prioritization, and team coordination -- even in engineering-dominated cultures.
