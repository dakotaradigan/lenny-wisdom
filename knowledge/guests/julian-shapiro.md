## Key Frameworks

### The Creativity Faucet
Good ideas arrive after the bad ideas are emptied. When you exhaust bad ideas, your brain reflexively identifies what causes badness and becomes better at pattern-matching novel solutions. Most creators resist this process -- they scribble a few thoughts, are not struck with gold, and walk away. You must run the faucet until the water runs clear.

### Mind Followers vs. Labor Followers
People who follow you for the quality of your brain (original takes, like Paul Graham) versus those who follow you for curation work (meme accounts, fortune-cookie threads). Mind followers have higher affinity, more loyalty, pay closer attention, and are more likely to act on your recommendations. Optimize for quality of follower, not volume.

### Evergreen Handbooks
Create forcing functions for thorough learning by writing comprehensive handbooks that serve your own reference needs first. The extra 30 hours to make them public is worth it for audience building and SEO. Continuously update them -- spend as much time revising old posts as writing new ones.

## Core Ideas

- Threads are the primary way to get Twitter followers because they give enough surface area for readers to confirm consistency of quality
- Product-led acquisition works when the product itself generates awareness and distribution through natural usage, not just marketing spend
- Retention is the most important growth metric; focus on giving users repeated reasons to return rather than optimizing top-of-funnel
- Writing is a way to discover what you think, not just to communicate what you already know; the best content is a letter to yourself
