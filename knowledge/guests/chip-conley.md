# Chip Conley

Brian Chesky's mentor and Airbnb's head of global hospitality and strategy from 2013-2017. Previously founded Joie de Vivre, the second-largest boutique hotel chain in the US. Now runs Modern Elder Academy (MEA), the world's first midlife wisdom school.

## Key Frameworks

### Wise and Curious
To succeed as an older person in a younger environment, be both wise and curious. Often the dumbest person in the room on technical matters while bringing decades of pattern recognition. Show respect and humility, and don't pretend to know what you don't know.

### Midlife as Chrysalis
Midlife is not a crisis but a chrysalis: the transformation stage between caterpillar and butterfly. The Modern Elder Academy helps people navigate this transition by combining wisdom with beginner's mind.

## Core Ideas

- At 52, Chip didn't know what "product" meant in tech; he thought Airbnb's product was the homes, which turned out to be a valuable perspective for representing older hosts
- Chip advocated against mobile-only Airbnb by bringing older hosts in to test; he was a voice for users the young team couldn't represent
- Brian Chesky assumed everyone would work at his pace (10pm meetings); founder mode is great for the founder but exhausting for everyone else
- When older brains connect dots and younger team members move fast and focused, it's a brilliant combination
- People won't notice your wrinkles as much as they'll notice your energy

## Notable Perspectives

> "I was mentoring Brian, but he was also my boss. I was 52, the average age was 26. I had to be both wise and curious, and often the dumbest person in the room."

> "When you have older brains connecting the dots, younger team members being really fast and focused, it's brilliant."
