# Jessica Lachs

VP of Analytics and Data Science at DoorDash for 10+ years. Built one of the largest and most respected data teams in tech. Was DoorDash's first GM, responsible for launching new markets. Previously founded GiftSimple and worked in investment banking at Lehman Brothers.

## Key Frameworks

### Central Data Org (Center of Excellence)
Analytics should report through a central org, not be embedded in business units. Central teams maintain consistent methodology, share talent, cross-pollinate insights, and build career paths. To solve the "embedded feel" problem, divide the central team into pods that map perfectly to product/engineering/ops structure, sharing the same goals as their partner teams.

### Analytics as Business Impact, Not Service
Analytics is not a service function answering Jira tickets and building dashboards. It's a business-impact function that finds opportunities, has a point of view on decisions, and answers the "so what?" -- "What do we do now that we know this?" Analysts must earn their seat at the table by proactively bringing opportunities.

### Short-Term Metrics That Drive Long-Term Outputs
Retention is a terrible goal metric -- it's nearly impossible to drive meaningfully in the short term. Instead, find short-term metrics that predictably drive long-term outputs. This is where the real analytical craft lies.

## Core Ideas

- Extreme ownership defined the early DoorDash data team. If understanding the problem meant picking up the phone and calling customers, that's what you did -- regardless of your title or job description.
- Incentive alignment is critical: if your analytics team shares the same goals as partner teams (marketing, product, ops), the central model works seamlessly. Their success is your success and vice versa.
- The biggest risk of embedded data teams: business leaders control the roadmap, dictate the work, and analysts become order-takers. Central teams preserve analytical independence and ensure insights flow across the organization.
- Building great data teams requires hiring for ownership mentality, curiosity, and willingness to roll up sleeves -- not just technical SQL or Python skills.
