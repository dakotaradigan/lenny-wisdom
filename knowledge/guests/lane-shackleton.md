# Lane Shackleton

Chief Product Officer at Coda. Previously led product at YouTube under Shishir Mehrotra. Former mountain guide in Alaska. Known for writing principles of great product management on his Substack and for Coda's distinctive five-level role stage system that replaces traditional career ladders.

## Key Frameworks

### Ambiguity to Clarity
The core job of a product person is to turn ambiguity into clarity. Everything in a PM's world is ambiguous -- role, target customer, problem definition, solution -- and the best PMs systematically spot and resolve that ambiguity. The principles Lane writes about are personal methods for executing this conversion.

### Systems Not Goals
Inspired by Jerry Seinfeld throwing away all his material and rebuilding through a daily writing system: be obsessed with the system that produces outcomes, not the goal itself. Teams that have a default-on system for talking to customers develop superior instincts compared to teams that set a quarterly OKR to talk to ten customers and may or may not hit it.

### Cathedrals Not Bricks
Adapted from a story Shishir Mehrotra shared: ensure every team member sees the cathedral they are building, not just the bricks they are laying. Show the vision through multiple facets -- writeups, metrics, directional mocks, billboard mockups -- because different people need different windows into the broader arc.

## Core Ideas

- Seek "oh shit" moments where you feel underqualified; the absence of stretch over six months signals stagnation
- Coda uses five role stages (Apprentice through Principal) that are consistent across design, product, and engineering, with compensation set by a centralized committee rather than individual managers
- Preparation and checklists from mountain guiding translate directly to product: stay calm, assess, prioritize, act
- The walkthrough ritual uses beginner's mind: walk through a product with five to ten people watching and fix every problem you see as if you know nothing about the topic
- Great principles should transcend career level and apply equally to an IC PM starting out and a head of product
