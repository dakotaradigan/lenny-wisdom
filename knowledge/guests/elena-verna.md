# Elena Verna

Former CMO at Miro, SVP of Growth at SurveyMonkey, interim Head of Growth at Amplitude. One of the most prominent voices in B2B growth strategy with 15+ years of operating experience across product-led and sales-led companies.

## Key Frameworks

### Growth Tactics That Never Work (Anti-Patterns)
A curated list of 10 common growth mistakes: hiring a head of growth too early, hiring growth to fix a declining business, expecting rebrands to drive growth, blindly copying competitors, assuming your problem is unique, over-relying on paid channels, not overlaying multiple growth models, not hiring advisors, testing everything instead of trusting intuition, and obsessing over friction removal without understanding the underlying problem.

### Earned vs Rented Channels
Growth teams should prioritize building earned acquisition channels (virality, user-generated content, word-of-mouth) over rented channels (paid search, paid social). Earned channels are defensible and compound over time; rented channels are vulnerable to algorithm changes and rising costs. At Dropbox, 50%+ of acquisition came from the sharing loop -- an earned channel no competitor could replicate.

### Growth Model Layering
No single growth loop lasts forever. Every 18 months, teams should introduce a new growth mechanism. Every 5 years, a major new channel or loop must be producing meaningful volume. Dedicate 20-25% of growth team capacity annually to exploring new loops, even without immediate metric expectations.

## Core Ideas

- Founders must drive growth themselves before hiring a growth team. Growth teams cannot create product-market fit or reverse a declining business -- they can only amplify what is already working by 10-15% at best.
- Rebrands and homepage redesigns never drive growth at launch. The best case is net-neutral performance followed by 3-6 months of optimization work to reach the prior baseline. Teams consistently underestimate this cost.
- Copying competitors is the fastest path to mediocrity. Use competitive teardowns as inspiration and pattern recognition, but never skip ideation, user research, and experimentation. What you see in a competitor's product may be an untested legacy page, not a best practice.
- Growth experimentation should not be paralyzed by statistical rigor. If achieving sample size takes more than a month, skip the A/B test and use pre/post analysis instead. Trust intuition more -- progress over perfection.
- Career optionality should be your professional north star, not titles. Full-time roles are one package among many (advising, fractional, interim, freelancing). Diversify how you monetize your skills.
