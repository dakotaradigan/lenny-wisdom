# Todd Jackson

Partner at First Round Capital. Former product lead for Gmail, PM on Facebook's News Feed and Groups, Director of PM at Twitter, VP of Product and Design at Dropbox. Founded Cover (acquired by Twitter). Created the Product-Market Fit Method, a structured program helping early B2B founders increase their odds of finding PMF.

## Key Frameworks

### Four Levels of Product-Market Fit
Nascent (L1), Developing (L2), Strong (L3), Extreme (L4). Roughly 60% of startups never get past L2. Three dimensions matter across all levels: demand (do people want this?), satisfaction (are they deeply happy with it?), and efficiency (can you deliver it profitably?). You do not pursue all three from the start -- they unlock sequentially.

### The Four Ps of Pivoting
When stuck at an early PMF level, change one or more of: Persona (who you serve), Problem (what you solve), Promise (how you position it), Product (what you build). Lattice kept persona but changed the other three. Vanta changed all four. The framework helps founders systematically explore what to change rather than wandering.

### Dollar-Driven Discovery
Go beyond standard customer discovery to specifically validate willingness to pay significant money. Design partner agreements, conversion from free to paid, and pricing signals are more reliable indicators of PMF than enthusiasm or stated interest alone.

## Core Ideas

- Finding extreme product-market fit is the single most important thing a startup does in its first three years; everything else becomes easier once you find it
- Most advice on PMF is vague ("you'll know it when you see it"); founders need specific, tactical frameworks to increase their odds
- The grind of product iteration between L2 and L3 is where most founders get stuck; this is where the science matters most
- Bottom-up B2B is closer to consumer product development; this framework is specifically designed for sales-led B2B
- Design partners are critical but tricky; structure agreements carefully and have a clear plan to convert them to paying customers
