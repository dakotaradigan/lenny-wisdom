# Gina Gotthilf

Former VP of Growth and Marketing at Duolingo, where she helped scale from 3 million to over 200 million users primarily through organic and non-paid growth channels. Led growth and community for Tumblr in Latin America. Oversaw a historic digital ad budget on the Mike Bloomberg presidential campaign. Currently co-founder and COO of Latitud, building the next generation of tech startups in Latin America.

## Key Frameworks

### The A-Side / B-Side Career
Every career has an impressive highlight reel (A-side) and a hidden messy reality (B-side). Gina was fired, laid off, lost her visa multiple times, dropped out of college from depression, and went unpaid for six months at Tumblr -- all between the highlights of working at Duolingo and meeting President Obama. Recognizing B-moments as temporary and universal is essential for resilience.

### Brand Voice as Growth Engine
At Duolingo, growth came from developing a unique voice that was unexpected, quirky, and never took itself too seriously. Communication is not just conveying a message -- it is conveying it in a way the listener receives, understands, and remembers. Making people feel something (a giggle, a "wait, what?") drives organic sharing and word-of-mouth.

## Core Ideas

- Communication is constantly underrated; it is not about conveying information but about making people feel something and remember it
- PR and brand are massively underappreciated by most startups as growth channels; they compound over time in ways paid channels cannot
- Consumer subscription businesses are rare successes; Duolingo is one of the few that cracked it through a combination of voice, habit-forming design, and organic channels
- You can figure things out even without expertise in a specific market; when Duolingo asked Gina to expand to Chile and Argentina, she said yes despite knowing nothing about those markets
- Careers are longer than you think; at 26, Gina thought her career was over after being fired multiple times, but in retrospect it was barely beginning

## Notable Perspectives

> "Communication isn't about being able to convey a message, it's about being able to convey a message in a way that the listener receives it, and understands it, and remembers it."

> "Most of us have a lot of B-moments every day, every week, every month. It's easy to think things aren't going to work out if we don't recognize them as moments."
