## Key Frameworks

### State Over Story
Most people try to calm anxiety through cognitive reframes (top-down). Bottom-up approaches using breath are far more effective -- there are four times more neurons sending body-to-brain signals than the reverse. Change your physiological state first; thoughts and feelings follow.

### Feather, Brick, Dump Truck
Early burnout signals are subtle (feather: waking up tired). Ignored, they escalate (brick: losing your cool). Continued neglect leads to a dump truck: health crisis, breakdown. Interoceptive awareness lets you catch the feather and adjust before catastrophe.

### Emotional Debt
Like technical debt, unreleased stress accumulates as allostatic load. Signs: disproportionate reactivity, poor sleep, inability to downshift without substances. Pay it off through somatic practices, NSDR, and allowing incomplete stress cycles to complete.

## Core Ideas

- The 4-4-8 breath (inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 8) rapidly calms the nervous system; the key is exhale twice as long as inhale
- Espresso breath (rapid nasal pumping) provides caffeine-free activation
- APE check-in: Awareness, Posture, Emotion -- a quick body scan for interrupting anxiety
- NSDR for 15-20 minutes daily is the most impactful ongoing practice for building nervous system capacity
- The nervous system of an organization reflects the nervous system of the CEO; burnout costs a median $100K per startup event
