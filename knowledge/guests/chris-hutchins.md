# Chris Hutchins

Full-time creator running All the Hacks podcast (one of the biggest business podcasts). Previously led new product strategy at Wealthfront under Andy Rachleff, PM at Google, and co-founder of multiple startups.

## Key Frameworks

### Persistent Vision Communication
Repeat your vision and mission at every all-hands, just like founders do. As a PM, telling people once in an email or PRD is not enough. People miss things, forget, or weren't paying attention. Constant repetition creates cohesive understanding.

### Product-Market Fit Through Prototypes
At Wealthfront, they tested clickable prototypes and even pretended the product already existed in interviews. When someone said "Can I go get my husband to show him this?" they knew they had something. The strongest signal is people reaching over the table wanting the product.

## Core Ideas

- Only 150,000 podcasts have published 10+ episodes in the last 10 days; just sticking with it for 10 weeks puts you in the top 3-4% of all podcasts
- As a PM, building internal excitement and getting buy-in is as important as customer insights; creating a compelling vision is essential
- Self-Driving Money at Wealthfront was a great product for existing users but didn't become the top-of-funnel growth channel they hoped
- The biggest lesson: your ability to speak publicly, persuade people, and build influence within a company is as important as identifying user needs
- Start your show/product/pitch with exactly what it is so people can explain it to others succinctly

## Notable Perspectives

> "You think that customer research is all you need to build a product at a company, but figuring out how to create excitement internally and get buy-in from other teams is essential."

> "There are four million podcasts. However, there are only about 150,000 podcasts that have had 10 episodes and have published in the last 10 days."
