# Jeetu Patel

Chief Product Officer and President at Cisco, managing 30,000+ people. Led the transformation of Cisco from a traditional networking company into an AI-first platform company. Organized a summit featuring Jensen Huang, Sam Altman, Marc Andreessen, and other AI leaders.

## Key Frameworks

### Megatrend vs. Hype Cycle
Know the difference. When there's a megatrend, don't fight it -- go all in. AI is a megatrend, one of the most foundational movements in human history. Large companies often experiment plenty but fail to double down when experiments succeed. Cisco chose not to hedge on AI.

### Platform Over Holding Company
Transform from a collection of 251 acquisitions and thousands of products into a tightly integrated, loosely coupled platform. The customer should feel the same emotion regardless of which product they use. You don't have to buy everything at once, but when you buy two things together, they work like magic.

### Treat AI as a Teammate, Not a Tool
The breakthrough came when a former OpenAI deployed engineer told Cisco: "Stop thinking of this as a tool. Think of this as a teammate." The framing change transformed how teams actually used the technology and what they expected from it.

## Core Ideas

- Innovation is a choice, not a function of company size. Every hour of every day, you can choose to be creative or not. The constraint isn't being a large company -- it's not doubling down when experiments work.
- Survival of humanity may depend on successful AI. Birth rates are declining globally. If 60% of the population needs care and there aren't enough younger people to provide it, AI becomes essential, not optional.
- Stamina trumps intellect. You can become smart through curiosity, hunger, and persistence. You can't teach hunger.
- Critique and debate in public, not in private. The standard management advice of "praise in public, criticize in private" is wrong. Build enough trust that the team is comfortable with open debate -- that's where the best decisions come from.
