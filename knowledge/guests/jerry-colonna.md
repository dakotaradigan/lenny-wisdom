# Jerry Colonna

Co-founder and CEO of Reboot, an executive coaching firm. Previously co-founded Flatiron Partners with Fred Wilson, one of the most successful early-stage investment funds. Author of Reboot and Reunion. Known for combining therapeutic depth with practical leadership development.

## Key Frameworks

### The Complicity Question
"How have I been complicit in creating the conditions I say I don't want?" The word complicit (not responsible) is deliberate -- you're the accomplice driving the getaway car, not robbing the bank. The purpose is to evoke agency, not blame. Example: "I say I don't want to feel busy all the time, but I feel unnerved when my agenda isn't jam-packed."

### The Leadership Equation
Practical skills + radical self-inquiry + shared experiences = enhanced leadership + greater resilience. Most people come to coaching for practical skills (the "how"), but the real work is in radical self-inquiry -- asking questions about your relationship to money, self-worth, and why you chose this path in the first place.

### Radical Self-Inquiry
Ask questions you're socialized not to ask. "What will being successful do for you? Where does that definition of success come from? How does your relationship to money shape your career choices?" These questions raise consciousness so you can be in the driver's seat of your life, not acting out learned childhood behaviors.

## Core Ideas

- We're socialized to bullshit ourselves and everyone else, especially in entrepreneurship. "All our companies are moving up and to the right" is just a lie. Cutting through self-delusion is the foundation of leadership growth.
- The big lie: achieving external success (money, title, house) will make you feel safe and happy. Jerry's own story of outward VC success coinciding with suicidal depression proves this is not the case.
- If you want to create a high-functioning team, do your own work first. It starts with the person who has the most power. A leader's unexamined patterns cascade through the entire organization.
- You don't have to be an asshole to be successful, and you don't have to feel miserable to build a career. The hack is consciousness -- understanding why you do what you do.
