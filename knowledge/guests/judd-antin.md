# Judd Antin

Built user research at Facebook and was longtime head of research at Airbnb. Direct reports now lead research at Figma, Notion, Slack, Robinhood, Duolingo, and more. Author of "The UX Research Reckoning Is Here," which argues the discipline must evolve from reactive service function to integrated strategic partner.

## Key Frameworks

### Macro / Middle-Range / Micro Research
Macro: strategic, forward-looking, competitive landscape, TAM studies. Micro: laser-focused usability, AB test interpretation. Middle-range: the blob where most research lives -- interesting but not pointed enough for business impact. The discipline does too much middle-range and not enough macro or micro.

### User-Centered Performance
Customer obsession that is symbolic rather than learning-focused. Every "quick study to validate assumptions" at the end of a product process is user-centered performance. The mantra: "We don't validate, we falsify. We are looking to be wrong."

### Five Tools of a Great Researcher
Formative/generative research, evaluative usability testing, rigorous survey design, applied statistics, and technical skills (SQL/dashboarding/prompt engineering). Multi-method researchers are exponentially more valuable than single-method specialists.

## Core Ideas

- Research was hit hardest by layoffs because it was set up as a reactive service function, not an integrated strategic partner
- Researchers must learn business language: read quarterly reports, understand conversion funnels, know OKRs
- Consistent researcher engagement from beginning to end of the product process is the short-circuit to the vicious cycle of low impact and sidelining
- Design has as much performance problem as research; both disciplines need genuine growth learning mindset, not box-checking

## Notable Perspectives
