# Matt LeMay

Product consultant and author of Product Management in Practice (one of the most popular PM books) and Impact First Product Teams. Has worked with hundreds of product teams across industries as a consultant. Former music critic at Pitchfork for 13 years and touring musician.

## Key Frameworks

### The Low-Impact PM Death Spiral
A dynamic where product teams add incremental features and cosmetic improvements without connecting to business-critical outcomes, until the next round of layoffs eliminates them. It starts when teams are given "work around the work" and assume it must be important because they were hired to do it. Daniel Ek's Spotify layoff message -- "too many teams doing work around the work" -- is the warning sign.

### The CEO Funding Test
Ask yourself: if you were the CEO of this company, would you fully fund your own team? Most PMs cannot answer immediately, which reveals a dangerous disconnect between their work and the business's most critical outcomes.

### One Step From Company Goals
Team goals should be no more than one step away from company goals. Do not let goals get cascaded through multiple layers into oblivion where the connection to business outcomes becomes invisible.

## Core Ideas

- Following all best practices does not protect you if your company goes out of business; your paycheck depends on business outcomes, not OKR scores
- The magic of great product work lies in how people with different perspectives build something greater than the sum of parts
- Say what you did, show the impact, put the number on it -- even if you prefer collaborative credit, your career depends on articulating your contribution
- Product teams are the foundational unit of impact; frame team accomplishments rather than purely individual ones
- In the AI era, the ability to learn from and build with other humans becomes more valuable and precious, not less
