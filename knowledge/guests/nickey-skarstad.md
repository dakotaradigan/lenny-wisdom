## Key Frameworks

### Vision-Mission-Strategy-Objectives Pyramid
Work top down: (1) Vision -- where you want to be in 10 years, (2) Mission -- how you make the vision come alive, (3) Strategy -- what needs to happen to execute, (4) Objectives -- the specific notes to hit in the next 3-6 months. Each layer gets more concrete and time-bound as you descend.

### Quality as Balancing Metric
Use quality metrics (like review rate) alongside growth metrics to prevent growth at the expense of user experience. At Airbnb Experiences, the review rate was the top-line goal, not bookings. When these metrics are in equilibrium, long-term growth follows naturally.

### Calendar Energy Audit
Color every meeting on your calendar red, yellow, or green after it happens based on how much energy it gave you. If your calendar is mostly red and yellow, you are in the wrong role. Use this data to optimize your next career move for what genuinely energizes you.

## Core Ideas

- Adding friction to Etsy's seller onboarding actually improved outcomes: slowing sellers down to be more thoughtful about listings got them to a first sale faster, which was the strongest predictor of long-term success
- Strategy should never be built in a vacuum -- bring the team along in crafting it, but do not make it democratic; one person must be responsible for the final call
- Dogfooding is critical for quality -- at Airbnb Experiences the team constantly took experiences worldwide and brought back firsthand quality observations
- Functional org structures work best for smaller or earlier-stage companies; GM structures work for distinct new business opportunities that need autonomy within larger companies
