# Ray Cao

Global Head of Monetization Product Strategy and Operations at TikTok, where he has spent over four years building out the advertising business. Previously spent six years at Google scaling Google Shopping globally. Offers a rare inside look at how TikTok operates and builds product at extraordinary speed.

## Key Frameworks

### Context, Not Control
TikTok's core operating principle: give team members full context about the business, then encourage proactive thinking rather than prescriptive task assignment. People should see themselves as business owners, not cogs. The complementary principle is "proactive thinking, reactive doing" -- think broadly about problems on your own, but collaborate when taking action to avoid stepping on toes.

### Always Day One
Maintaining startup mentality regardless of scale. TikTok combines Amazon-style document reading meetings (180+ people reading and commenting on one doc together), OKR systems learned from Google, and a culture of open communication where anyone can message leadership directly. The goal is keeping the energy of a young company even as headcount grows.

## Core Ideas

- The algorithm helps TikTok go global, but local talent and cultural understanding are essential to fine-tune it -- technology is a tool, not a replacement for market knowledge
- Engineering, product, and sales teams at TikTok collaborate unusually closely, with PMs and engineers regularly joining client meetings to "feel the heat" of real market feedback
- When hiring fast, never compromise quality standards -- wrong hires slow you down more than empty seats do
- Different markets demand different content strategies; TikTok seeds each market with culturally relevant use cases rather than assuming one format works everywhere
