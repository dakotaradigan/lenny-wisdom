# Christine Itwaru

Longtime product operations leader at Pendo who helped define and evangelize the product ops role. Transitioned from product management into product ops during the function's emergence around 2019.

## Key Frameworks

### Product Ops as System and Role
Product operations is two things: 1) A practice any PM leader does to create systems that help product teams thrive. 2) An emerging dedicated role where specialists partner with PMs on data, voice of customer, tooling, and process to free PMs to focus on customers and engineers.

### Voice of Customer as Cross-Functional Intelligence
Product ops synthesizes qualitative and quantitative customer signals from sales, success, support, and product analytics, then validates or disputes findings with user research. This creates transparency across the revenue org and surfaces insights no single team could see alone.

## Core Ideas

- Product ops emerged around 2019 driven by growth, increased PM scope, product-led growth trends, and the natural evolution of maturing product functions
- The role handles: voice of customer synthesis, PM tooling stack optimization, content strategy within the product, and sometimes product development process facilitation
- PMs should never give up talking to customers directly; product ops supports and amplifies that, not replaces it
- Bringing sales, success, and product teams together around customer data reveals that they're often screaming about the same problems from different angles
- Not everything customers ask for requires a product change; sometimes it's an enablement or education gap

## Notable Perspectives
