# Josh Miller

CEO and co-founder of The Browser Company, makers of Arc browser. Previously acquired by Facebook where he first learned PM. Built the company around the philosophy that the team is the product. Known for optimizing for user feelings rather than metrics, shipping frequently, and attracting world-class talent who join as ICs despite VP-level experience elsewhere.

## Key Frameworks

### Optimize for Feelings, Not Metrics
Instead of "how many times per week do people share?", ask "do people feel closer to their friends and family?" Metrics keep you honest, but at the moment of creation, think about the human at the other end. When you pick the right feeling (surprise, joy, speed), it tracks closely with business metrics anyway.

### Heartfelt Intensity
The most important company value. Hire people with fire in their belly and something to prove. Combined with "assume you don't know" (beginner's mind) and "start by asking what could be" (ambition), this creates a default-to-action culture that ships remarkable work without heavy process.

### D5/D7 as One Metric
How many people use Arc at least five days out of seven? Captures retention, engagement, and growth in one ungameable number. Focus on week-over-week growth rate, not absolutes.

## Core Ideas

- The goal of interviews is to convince candidates not to join, creating a self-selecting filter for genuine conviction
- Celebrate every hire publicly from day one -- if the team is your product, each hire is a product launch
- Company values should emerge organically from how the team already works; interview the whole team and mirror their language back
- Great people join when you offer ambitious prompts, genuine empowerment, and a kind culture

## Notable Perspectives
