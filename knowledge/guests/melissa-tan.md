# Melissa Tan

Growth advisor and former Head of Growth at Webflow. Was the first growth PM and longtime head of growth for Dropbox's B2B business. Full-time advisor to Canva, Grammarly, Miro, and Ro. Known for building teams that people follow from company to company.

## Key Frameworks

### First-Principles Hiring for Innovation
Dropbox hired smart people who had never done sales and had them do sales. The disadvantage is lack of experience; the advantage is they come in thinking "I don't know anything, let me figure this out" and innovate wildly. This approach generated Dropbox's innovative go-to-market motions because those people later moved into other functions carrying deep user context.

### The Four Lessons of Dropbox
(1) Hiring for first-principles thinkers and humble collaborators drives innovation. (2) The devil is in execution details, not best practices. (3) Clarify the point of view on the overall go-to-market motion early -- especially the consumer-to-B2B journey. (4) Engage the entire company in thinking about growth and revenue from day one rather than treating it as a dirty word.

## Core Ideas

- People follow leaders who deeply care about their development and invest in their careers; the core is genuinely caring, and all actions follow from there
- Growth was a dirty word at Dropbox in 2013; companies that delay growth thinking until self-serve slows down lose valuable time to competitors
- Signal for when to add sales to a PLG product: larger companies in your self-serve base knocking on your door asking for enterprise features
- Caring about people and driving impact are not opposites; both can coexist and reinforce each other
- Start product-led when the product is intuitive and viral; layer in sales when enterprise customers demand SSO, security, and custom features

## Notable Perspectives
