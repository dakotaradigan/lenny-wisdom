# Jeanne DeWitt Grosser

COO at Vercel overseeing marketing, sales, customer success, revenue ops, and field engineering. Previously built Stripe's early sales team from the ground up. Built world-class GTM teams at multiple unicorns.

## Key Frameworks

### Go-to-Market as a Product
Think of the entire customer lifecycle -- from awareness to high LTV -- as a product you design. Map the jobs to be done at each stage, orchestrate across functions, and iterate like you would with your actual product. The buying experience itself increasingly differentiates companies when products converge.

### The GTM Engineer
A technical role that sits at the intersection of engineering and go-to-market. They shadow top-performing salespeople, document their workflows, then build AI agents to automate repetitive parts. At Vercel, one GTM engineer spent six weeks building an inbound lead agent that replaced 9 of 10 SDRs on inbound, maintaining the same lead-to-opportunity conversion rate while reducing response time.

### Salespeople That Pass for PMs
The litmus test: "If I put you in front of 10 engineers at our company, it should take them 10 minutes to figure out you aren't a product manager." Sales teams for technical products must be deeply consultative, not transactional.

## Core Ideas

- Go-to-market encompasses everything that touches a customer or makes a dollar: marketing, sales, technical sales, customer success, support, and partnerships. The hyper-specialization era (17+ GTM roles) is collapsing back toward integrated, AI-augmented workflows.
- 80% of customers buy to avoid pain or reduce risk, not for increased upside. Startups love talking about the art of the possible, but enterprises buy to avoid missing their revenue target next quarter.
- Forward-deployed engineering is professional services rebranded -- but genuinely different because learnings flow back to product and engineering to determine what's generalizable vs. what remains a service.
- Sales process documentation is now a prerequisite for AI automation. You can't apply GTM engineering unless you have a documented playbook of what best practice looks like. This forces rigor earlier than most startups naturally achieve.
