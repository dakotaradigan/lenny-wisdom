# Ben Williams

VP of Product at Snyk, the developer security company valued at $8.6B. Led growth organization and developer experience initiatives, helping scale Snyk's PLG motion from early community-driven adoption to enterprise sales.

## Key Frameworks

### Depth-First Then Breadth
Start with a narrow persona, single context, and single use case. Snyk focused exclusively on Node.js developers using open source dependencies before expanding to other languages. Nail the narrow use case before casting a wider net.

### Growth Loop Architecture
Identify and design acquisition, engagement, and retention loops into the product from day one. Snyk's GitHub fix PR loop was both an acquisition and engagement loop: scan code, create branded PRs, other devs see them, follow links, create accounts.

## Core Ideas

- PLG and security were never spoken in the same sentence before Snyk; the founders saw developer-first security as a complete industry disruption
- Community engagement with a specific developer community (Node.js) was the foundation of the first 100 users and early growth
- First self-serve monetization failed because enterprise security buyers needed governance features and multi-language support before purchasing
- Programmatic SEO assets like Snyk Advisor (package health scores) created powerful content loops driving sustained organic growth
- Being intentional about growth loops during early product development gives you a massive advantage when product-market fit hits
