# Marc Andreessen

Co-founder of Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) and co-creator of Netscape, the first widely used web browser. One of the most influential technology investors and thinkers. Known for prescient calls including "software is eating the world" and the smartphone adoption curve.

## Key Frameworks

### AI as the Philosopher's Stone
AI transmutes sand (silicon) into thought -- the most common thing in the world into the most rare. This is the alchemical transformation Newton spent decades seeking. The technology now exists to convert ubiquitous raw materials into reasoning capability, making this a historically unprecedented moment.

### The Mexican Standoff of Roles
Every coder now believes they can be a PM and designer because of AI. Every PM thinks they can code and design. Every designer thinks they can do both. They are all partially correct. The additive effect of competence in two domains is more than double; in three domains, more than triple. The future belongs to specialists in the combination of domains.

### Task Loss vs. Job Loss
Jobs persist longer than individual tasks. AI will substitute tasks within jobs rather than eliminating entire jobs wholesale. Even if AI triples productivity growth, it returns us to the job churn levels of 1870-1930 -- a period people considered a watch with opportunity, not a crisis.

## Core Ideas

- Productivity growth has been very low for 50 years; AI is arriving precisely when demographic decline demands it -- the timing is miraculously good
- The super-empowered individual: great practitioners using AI become not twice as good but ten times as good
- Teach children agency and full use of AI tools; one-on-one AI tutoring can democratize the Bloom's 2 Sigma effect that was previously only available to the wealthy
- AI-native founders are asking whether a single founder can run an entire company with AI doing everything
- Price deflation from AI productivity gains is the equivalent of giving everyone a massive raise
