# Matthew Dicks

59-time Moth Story Slam winner and nine-time Grand Slam champion. Author of Storyworthy (widely considered the best practical book on storytelling). Elementary school teacher for 25 years. Author of nine other books including fiction and rock operas.

## Key Frameworks

### The Five-Second Moment
Every story worth telling is about a singular moment of transformation or realization. The moment is the flip -- one second you thought one thing, the next second you think something new. 98% of the story is context to bring that moment into maximum clarity. Start at the end: know your five-second moment, then construct the story backward from there.

### Beginning and End in Opposition
The beginning of a story should be the opposite of the end. If the end is "I realized Eileen has confidence," the beginning is "Eileen does not have confidence." Star Wars: a boy with no religion gains religion. When Harry Met Sally: two people who hate each other fall in love. Knowing the end tells you the beginning.

### The Dinner Test
Any story you tell formally -- on stage, in a keynote, in a pitch -- should closely resemble how you would tell it at dinner. No performance art, no unattributed dialogue openers, no starting with sound effects. If you would not say it across a dinner table, cut it.

## Core Ideas

- If you are not telling stories, you will be forgotten; the mind is not designed to remember pie charts, statistics, or platitudes without imagery
- The shortest version of every story is the best version; start as close to the end as possible
- Never tell vacation stories unless something changed in you during the vacation; the location is almost always irrelevant
- Stories must be about you, not about someone else; telling someone else's story requires no vulnerability and feels like fiction
- PowerPoint presentations are never watched twice; stories are experienced again and again because human minds are wired for narrative
- To be a storyteller in business means separating yourself from the herd, which risks getting picked off -- but the alternative is being forgettable
