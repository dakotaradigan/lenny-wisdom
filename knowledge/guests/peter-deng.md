# Peter Deng

Perhaps the most under-the-radar impactful product leader in tech. VP Product at OpenAI (shipped ChatGPT Enterprise, voice, memory, desktop, custom GPTs, built first growth team). First Head of Product at Instagram. Led Rider product at Uber. Facebook's 4th ever PM (built News Feed, standalone Messenger, photos, groups). Now GP at Felicis. First-ever podcast appearance.

## Key Frameworks

### Five Types of Product Managers
A taxonomy for understanding PM archetypes and matching the right type to the right stage and challenge. Each type has distinct strengths suited to different product situations, from zero-to-one creation to scaling established products.

### Chess-Move Planning
Think before you act and plan several moves ahead. Build systems that let you go sustainably faster rather than sprinting unsustainably. This applies to both product architecture decisions and organizational design.

### Six-Month Autonomy Test
In six months, if you are still telling a hire what to do, you hired the wrong person. The meta-goal of onboarding is calibrating together until the hire is the one telling you what needs to be done. This reframes the manager-report relationship as a collaboration toward the report's independence.

## Core Ideas

- Sometimes your product literally does not matter as much as you think -- at Uber, the price and ETA were the product; pixel-level app improvements had far less impact than those core variables
- Humans consume the entirety of the product holistically; fixing a bug may matter less than improving something more fundamental to the experience
- Education will be one of the biggest areas transformed by AI; his nine-year-old son built a custom GPT, demonstrating how young people are already rewiring their thinking through AI tools
- The best product people often have no public presence because they are too busy doing the work
