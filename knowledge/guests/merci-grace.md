# Merci Grace

Former Head of Growth at Slack, previously a game designer and VC at Lightspeed Ventures, now founder of Panobi. Co-founder of Women in Product. Brings a game-design sensibility to growth and onboarding, shaped by her background designing interactive experiences before entering product management.

## Key Frameworks

### Onboarding as Game Design
Design your product from the onboarding experience outward rather than treating onboarding as an afterthought bolted onto a finished product. The best onboarding uses the product itself to teach someone how to use it -- like a to-do app that has you complete your first task as part of setup. Avoid plug-and-play onboarding templates; what worked for Slack will not work for you.

### Social Inviter Segmentation
Within any user base, a subset of people are natural connectors who will invite others unprompted. It is far easier to get these social users to send more invites than to convince shy users to send even one. Place invite prompts early and often so connectors find them; non-connectors will simply skip past.

### Slack's Activation Metric
Through regression analysis Slack identified that three real humans exchanging fifty real messages was the activation threshold. Below three people, a simpler tool like email suffices; the third person is where collaboration tools start to prove their value over one-on-one channels.

## Core Ideas

- Start with curiosity rather than prescribed metrics when building a growth function at a new-category product; cookie-cutter playbooks are dangerous because competitors copy your experiments without knowing which ones actually failed
- Founders who are great storytellers start pitches with their unique insight and drop the audience into the action immediately, not with market sizing slides -- attention is the P0 of any interaction
- Business outcomes are driven more by interpersonal dynamics and subjective founder perception than by the fundamentals people attribute in hindsight; venture is more personal than most outsiders realize
- Longer free trials capture more users because conversion timing is dictated by the customer's schedule, not yours
