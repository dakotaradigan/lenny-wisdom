# Kevin Aluwi

Co-founder and former CEO of Gojek, Southeast Asia's biggest startup (2.7M drivers, 3B annual orders, $27B+ IPO). Started as a motorcycle taxi service in Indonesia, evolved into a super app with ~30 services. Built the company from $2M in funding against a competitor with $250M, survived motorcycle taxi mafias, and grew through brand building and operational tenacity.

## Key Frameworks

### Super App Reality Check
Super apps sound great on strategy decks (lower CAC, higher retention, cross-selling). In practice, most benefits do not pan out unless services share a unifying concept in the customer's mind. At Gojek, services connected to "the driver" cross-sold naturally. Services without that link (mobile top-up, massage) had poor discovery despite being on the homepage -- only 40% of customers even knew the top-up feature existed despite it being on one of six homepage buttons.

### Brand as Survival Strategy
When outfunded 125:1, brand was the reason Gojek survived. Great brands create associations that transcend transactional relationships and become part of customer identity. In consumer businesses, the two most important things are product and brand, in that order. Brand is most critical when you lack the capital to compete on subsidies.

### Operational Scrappiness as Moat
Gojek hired private security to protect drivers from motorcycle taxi mafias who physically assaulted them. Rather than treating drivers as expendable contractors, they invested in their safety. This operational commitment -- doing things that do not scale and that competitors will not match -- built driver loyalty and supply-side competitive advantage.

## Core Ideas

- A unifying concept across super app services is essential; if customers think of you as "the driver app" and you launch massages, they will ask if the driver is giving the massage
- Indonesia's scale is underappreciated: Gojek has more drivers than all of America and processes order volumes comparable to Uber globally
- Design constraints of super apps are unsolved: displaying 30 unrelated services creates a confusing grid that limits UX quality
- Product and brand are the two most important things in a consumer business; everything else is secondary
