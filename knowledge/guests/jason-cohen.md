# Jason Cohen

Four-time founder including two unicorns (WP Engine, Smart Bear). Prolific writer at asmartbear.com for 20+ years and author of *Hidden Multipliers*. Angel investor in ~60 startups.

## Key Frameworks

### Five Questions for Stalled Growth
A sequential diagnostic framework for why product growth slows: (1) Is logo churn too high? (2) Is pricing correct? (3) Are existing customers growing (NRR)? (4) Are marketing channels saturated? (5) Do you even need to grow? The order matters because fixing a later step is pointless if an earlier one is broken.

### The Churn Ceiling Formula
Divide new customers per month by the monthly cancellation rate to find the maximum number of customers you can ever have. For example, 100 new customers/month at 5% churn means a ceiling of 2,000 customers. This makes churn viscerally real rather than abstract.

### The Elephant Curve
Marketing channels follow an S-curve that then sags downward over time as audiences saturate, channels decline, and competitors crowd in. This means you cannot rely on existing channels indefinitely and must proactively explore new ones before the current ones peak.

## Core Ideas

- Logo churn is the most critical growth problem because cancellations scale automatically with your customer base while marketing growth requires constant manual effort, creating an inevitable ceiling on company size.
- When customers say they cancelled because of price, that is almost never the real reason. They already evaluated and accepted the price. You must dig into root-er causes, not just the proximate excuse, using open-ended questions like "what made you cancel" instead of "why did you cancel."
- Pricing selects your market. Raising prices often increases signups because higher prices signal quality to larger companies. Positioning a product around growth rather than cost savings can justify dramatically higher prices for the same product.
- NRR above 100% is effectively mandatory for building a large SaaS business. Among 100+ public SaaS companies, only about two have NRR below 100%, and the median at IPO is 119%.
- The common thread across all growth levers is whether you are genuinely creating value for customers in ways they recognize and can articulate. Price, retention, and expansion all flow from that foundation.
