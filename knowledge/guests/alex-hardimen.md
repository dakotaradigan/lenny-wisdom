# Alex Hardiman

Alex Hardiman is Chief Product Officer at the New York Times, leading teams that build news, cooking, games, audio, and advertising products. She previously was CPO at The Atlantic and spent two years at Facebook leading their news product post-2016 election.

## Key Frameworks

### Solar System Product Strategy
News is the "sun" - the core that gives the company brand, trust, and the largest audience. Satellite "planets" (cooking, games, sports, audio) share the same DNA of great journalism and product experience, creating a connected family of products that form an essential subscription bundle.

### Cross-Functional Missions
Organize around missions (consumer, monetization, platform) rather than just functions. Consumer missions embed editors alongside PMs, engineers, designers, and data scientists, marrying journalistic expertise with product signal.

## Core Ideas

- Product managers thrive in chaos - the ability to take crazy inputs, create structured models, prioritize, and rally teams around shared context is where PMs shine
- Rebundling disaggregated journalism on your own destination is key to building a subscription business; you need direct customer relationships
- Embedding editors in product teams creates a differentiated advantage - their subject expertise combined with data and research leads to better consumer solutions
- Impact at mission-driven companies means both growing subscribers AND triggering policy changes through deeply reported stories

## Notable Perspectives

> "Our impact and our business goals are in service of our mission, which is to seek the truth and help people understand the world, not the other way around."

> "There are really three things that we need to do: have the best news destination in the world, build beyond-news products, and create a connected family of products."
