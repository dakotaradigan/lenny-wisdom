## Key Frameworks

### Agency-Control Tradeoff
Every time you give an AI system more agency (decision-making ability), you lose control. Build incrementally: start with high control/low agency (AI suggests, human decides), then gradually increase agency as trust is earned through demonstrated reliability.

### Non-Determinism in AI Products
Unlike traditional software with predictable inputs/outputs, AI products have non-deterministic inputs (natural language) AND outputs (probabilistic models). This fundamentally changes how you should build, test, and monitor products.

### Success Triangle
Three dimensions for AI product success: great leaders who rebuild their intuitions, an empowering culture that doesn't threaten employees, and technical prowess that obsesses over understanding workflows deeply.

## Core Ideas

- Start with "minimum viable agency" - V1 suggests, V2 acts with review, V3 operates autonomously - rather than trying to build fully autonomous agents from day one
- It's not about being the first company with an agent; it's about building the right flywheels to improve over time
- If someone is selling you "one-click agents," it's pure marketing - replacing critical workflows takes 4-6 months minimum
- Leaders must rebuild their intuitions for AI; the CEO's engagement with AI is the number one predictor of company success
