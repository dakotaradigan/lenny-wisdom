## Key Frameworks

### Radical Candor (2x2)
Care Personally + Challenge Directly = Radical Candor. Missing challenge = Ruinous Empathy (90% of mistakes, by far the most common). Missing care = Obnoxious Aggression. Missing both = Manipulative Insincerity. Use this as a compass for specific conversations, not a label for people. Most of us make mistakes in all four quadrants daily.

### Soliciting Feedback First
Always start with soliciting feedback before giving it. Ask: "What could I do or stop doing that would make it easier to work with me?" Do not use Kim's exact words -- write your own question that sounds authentic to you. The question must be specific enough that "everything's fine" is not an adequate response.

### Praise Matters More Than Criticism
Radical Candor applies equally to praise and criticism, but praise is even more important. Specific, sincere praise that shows you genuinely noticed what someone did well builds the trust bank that makes critical feedback receivable. Never use the "feedback sandwich" -- it undermines trust in praise.

## Core Ideas

- Ruinous empathy is the most common and most damaging failure mode: being too worried about hurting feelings to share information someone needs to hear
- The impact of radical candor: better relationships, better work, more success, more happiness -- the famous story of Sheryl Sandberg telling Kim "when you say um every third word, it makes you sound stupid" transformed her public speaking career
- Omniscience is not required: enter feedback conversations with humility, state your intention to be helpful, give feedback immediately (delay usually means avoidance), and keep it in private for criticism
- Feedback is a dialogue, not a monologue -- use "candor" not "truth" because truth implies you have a pipeline to God and the other person knows nothing
