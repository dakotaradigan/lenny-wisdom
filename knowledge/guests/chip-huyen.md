# Chip Huyen

AI engineering author, educator, and practitioner. Core developer on NVIDIA's NeMo platform, AI researcher at Netflix, Stanford ML instructor, two-time founder. Author of *AI Engineering*, the most-read book on the O'Reilly platform since launch.

## Key Frameworks

### What Actually Improves AI Apps
What people think helps: staying up to date with AI news, adopting newest frameworks, agonizing over vector DBs, constantly comparing models, fine-tuning. What actually helps: talking to users, building reliable platforms, preparing better data, optimizing end-to-end workflows, writing better prompts.

### Pre-Training vs Post-Training
Pre-training encodes statistical information about language across massive data. Post-training (supervised fine-tuning + reinforcement learning) is where frontier labs differentiate. Everyone has similar pre-training data; post-training is the competitive edge.

## Core Ideas

- Most companies don't need to keep up with every AI development; if choosing between two technologies doesn't materially change performance, don't agonize over it
- Reinforcement learning is everywhere in post-training and is far more data-efficient than supervised fine-tuning
- AI productivity is hard to measure; managers want headcount, VPs want AI assistants, because they operate at different abstraction levels
- We're in an "idea crisis" where people have all the tools but don't know what to build; talking to users solves this
- Sampling strategy (how you pick from the probability distribution) is extremely important and very underrated for improving model output quality
