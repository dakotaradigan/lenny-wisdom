# Brandon Chu

VP of Product at Shopify for 7+ years, having joined the week of their IPO. Previously co-founded Tunezy and held PM roles at FreshBooks. Author of highly influential Medium posts on product management craft that remain widely referenced.

## Key Frameworks

### Ship the Right Thing at the Right Time in the Right Way
Shopify's PM job description. "Help teams" emphasizes servant leadership. PMs aren't dictators but exist to help the team arrive at the right thing, which they are ultimately accountable for.

### Writing as Crystallization
The writing process itself solidifies mental models and frameworks. Brandon figured out everything in his posts at the exact moment he wrote them. 40 hours per post, mostly editing, with fearless early sharing for feedback.

## Core Ideas

- To have the highest career trajectory, lean into founder skills: storytelling, fostering creative teams, making high-conviction leap-of-faith decisions, and leading by example
- At Shopify, everyone is responsible for product thinking, not just PMs; 30-40% of the PM team are ex-founders
- Shopify is excellent at avoiding sunk-cost fallacy, willing to throw away 3-6 months of work when the world changes
- During COVID, Shopify went from 40 planned projects to 3, shipping gift cards and curbside pickup in weeks to save merchant livelihoods
- Remote work success requires intentional in-person "bursts" with built tooling for one-click team travel booking
