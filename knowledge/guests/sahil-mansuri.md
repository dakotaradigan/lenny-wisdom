## Key Frameworks

### Milestone-Based Forecasting
In uncertain markets, set conservative annual targets but create short-term milestones that unlock additional spend. Pre-agree on what actions to take at each checkpoint to avoid optimism bias. If you hit Q1 targets, revise up and unlock budget. If you miss, revise down immediately. Make these decisions in advance to combat the founder bias toward optimism.

### Retention-Weighted Comp Plans
Traditional sales comp rewards new logo acquisition with no accountability for customer quality. Rep A closes 15 deals, 10 churn -- gets President's Club. Rep B closes 12, all renew and upsell -- gets less recognition. Reform: tie compensation to renewal rates, customer quality, and references generated, not just new ARR. Aligns rep incentives with what the business actually needs.

## Core Ideas

- In Q3 2022, 63% of sales reps missed quota (up from 46% in Q1); 76% of companies missed targets -- these are structural shifts, not individual performance issues
- Sales strategy must fundamentally change in downturns: shift from cold prospecting to cross-selling and upselling existing customers
- The 50-50 base/variable comp split for salespeople creates mercenary behavior; consider higher base with longer-horizon variable tied to customer outcomes
- Cap table as a growth strategy: getting founders and operators as angel investors creates early customers who advocate for the product from genuine usage
