# Megan Cook

Head of Product for Jira at Atlassian, which serves 75% of Fortune 500 companies and 125,000+ customers globally. Has been at Atlassian for 11 years. Previously an analyst, developer, and agile coach. Leads a fully distributed product team.

## Key Frameworks

### Fight Club for Leadership Triads
A weekly 30-minute meeting between the product, engineering, and design lead specifically for having conflicts. By scheduling time for disagreement, teams address problems early rather than letting them fester. The mindset of knowing you are going in to solve a hard problem makes difficult conversations normal rather than avoided.

### Peer Feedback Groups
Divide PMs into small groups that meet every two weeks. One person brings rough-draft work; everyone gives feedback. This builds psychological safety because people practice showing unfinished work and receiving constructive input in a low-stakes setting. It also models good feedback behavior across seniority levels.

## Core Ideas

- The opposite of play is not work, it is fear; when teams lose psychological safety, ideas become incremental and work gets "painfully polished" before reaching leadership
- Signs of declining safety include anonymous feedback increasing, junior people stopping participation, and work arriving overly polished
- Bring distributed teams together in person roughly three to four times per year; research shows connection and productivity both boost by about 30% and the effect lasts months
- The $10 game applied to individual priorities: divide $10 across all your work to visualize where time is actually going and identify misalignment
- Have senior leaders share stories of failure at offsites to normalize risk-taking and celebrate learning
