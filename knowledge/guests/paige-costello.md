# Paige Costello

Product leader at Asana responsible for desktop, web, and mobile apps. Oversees the core product experience helping teams answer "who is doing what, by when, and why." Known for helping younger PMs project confidence and win over skeptics through deep customer and market knowledge.

## Key Frameworks

### Double Diamond Process
Alternate between going broad and going narrow: broad on customer selection, narrow to pick one; broad on their problems, narrow to the key problem; broad on solutions, narrow to the best starting point. This forces curiosity over opinion and maps naturally to Asana's review artifacts at each inflection point.

### Nested Planning Structure
R&D metrics cascade through pillars, areas, and teams. Each area has a specific target customer, problem space, and metrics. Planning happens every six months for a rolling twelve-month horizon -- higher confidence near-term, lower confidence further out. This reduces churn versus annual planning that gets disrupted halfway through.

### Pivot-or-Cut Permission
Explicitly build into the product process the expectation that roadmap items might be pivoted or cut. Without this, once something is on the roadmap it feels mandatory, leading to poor scope decisions. Adding this permission makes teams more honest about what is working.

## Core Ideas

- Bring the insight to win over skeptics: know your customer, market, competitors, numbers, and product more deeply than anyone else in the room -- credentials matter less than demonstrated expertise
- The scarcity mindset holds back new PMs; asking "how might the opposite be true?" breaks the frame and reveals options that anxiety obscures
- Prototyping should be encouraged early and frequently to test assumptions before overcommitting to a direction
- Planning at too fine a granularity (specific quarters, specific weeks) causes strange scope decisions; align on what success looks like and let teams ship as quickly and iteratively as possible
