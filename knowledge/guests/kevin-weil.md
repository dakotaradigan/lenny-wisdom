# Kevin Weil

Chief Product Officer at OpenAI, leading product for what may be the most important company in the world right now. Previously head of product at Instagram (led Stories launch) and Twitter. Co-created Libra cryptocurrency at Facebook. Board member at Planet, Strava, Black Product Managers Network, and Nature Conservancy.

## Key Frameworks

### The Worst Model You Will Ever Use
The AI model you are using today is the worst AI model you will ever use for the rest of your life. Unlike any previous technology platform, the ground shifts every two months. If your product is on the edge of current model capabilities, keep going -- the next model will make it sing. Build for what is coming, not just what exists today.

### Evals as the New Core Skill
Defining what success looks like for AI model behavior is becoming the most important product skill. Evals are how you measure progress, specify desired behaviors, and iterate on model quality. Product managers and builders who master eval writing will have outsized impact in the AI era.

### Internal Dogfooding as Launch Signal
When a new feature generates explosive internal usage -- people using it all weekend, spontaneously discussing what they saw -- that is a strong signal it will resonate externally. This was true for Instagram Stories and for OpenAI's image generation. If a social or creative feature does not take off internally among a tight-knit team, question the direction.

## Core Ideas

- AI follows a pattern: the new thing is "AI," once it works it is "machine learning," once it is ubiquitous it is "just an algorithm" -- we are always recalibrating what counts as AI
- OpenAI's general mindset: in two months there will be a better model that blows away current limitations; build on the edge of capabilities
- Libra (Facebook cryptocurrency) is the biggest disappointment of Kevin's career -- a basket-of-currencies stablecoin integrated into WhatsApp that would have enabled free global money transfer; the world would be better if it existed
- The skills that matter most in an AI era: creative thinking, judgment, taste, and the ability to frame problems clearly for AI systems
