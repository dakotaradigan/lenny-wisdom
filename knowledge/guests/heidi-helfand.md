# Heidi Helfand

Author of Dynamic Reteaming: The Art and Wisdom of Changing Teams. Spent 20 years in fast-growing SaaS companies, including being the 10th employee at a company that grew to 900+ people. Her book challenges the conventional wisdom that teams should be kept stable, arguing instead that team change is inevitable and should be embraced, planned for, and made transparent.

## Key Frameworks

### Five Patterns of Reteaming
Teams change in predictable patterns: one-by-one (individual moves), grow-and-split (team gets too large), merging (combining teams), isolation (creating a focused squad), and switching (wholesale reorganization). Recognizing which pattern is happening helps leaders plan rather than react.

### Whiteboard Reteaming (Transparent Reorgs)
Instead of planning reorgs in secret and dropping them on employees, visualize the future team structure on whiteboards showing team names, missions, open headcount, and current people. Roll the whiteboards out, invite feedback, and let people identify mistakes and express interest in opportunities. This creates agency and ownership rather than fear.

### RIDE Framework for Change Decisions
From Pat Wadors: Who is Requesting the change? Who can give Input? Who is the Decider? Who will Execute? Clarity on these roles prevents the confusion and anxiety that derails organizational changes.

## Core Ideas

- Forming-storming-norming-performing is aspirational but unrealistic in fast-growing or shrinking companies; teams will change, so get better at changing them
- Reorgs create the biggest career opportunities because new roles open up; people who lean into change rather than fear it advance fastest
- William Bridges' Transitions framework (ending, neutral zone, new beginning) makes any organizational change easier to navigate emotionally
- Time-box the transparent reteaming process so it does not consume everyone's energy for weeks
- Giving people choice in team placement, even partial choice, dramatically increases buy-in and reduces resistance
