# Eoghan McCabe

Co-founder and CEO of Intercom. Returned as CEO after a two-year absence to find the company approaching negative growth. Transformed Intercom from a traditional SaaS business into an AI-first company, launching Fin (their AI agent) just six weeks after GPT-3.5's release. Fin grew from zero to mid-eight-digit ARR and is on track to pass $100M ARR, making it one of the most successful SaaS-to-AI pivots at scale.

## Key Frameworks

### Wartime Founder Mode
When a company faces existential threat or stagnation, the founder must take an authoritarian, top-down approach: pick a strategic lane, rewrite company values as a sharp knife to cut out cultural dysfunction, hard-code performance formulas that remove manager subjectivity, and accept that roughly 40% turnover may be necessary. The result is a high-alignment, high-performance culture where a 98-99% employee approval rating emerges on the other side.

### Outcome-Based Pricing for AI
Price AI products on the value delivered, not the cost to serve. Intercom charges 99 cents per resolved customer ticket -- a fraction of the $20-30 industry average per human resolution. Early on, they lost money on every transaction (cost was $1.20 per resolution), but bet that inference costs would fall. Pricing should come from value, not from costs; the cost is the company's problem to solve.

### Employee-Founder-Product-Market Fit
Beyond product-market fit and founder-market fit, you need the right employees for the type of company you are building. Comfortable employees in a wartime company create friction. Rather than forcing mismatched employees to adapt, lovingly part ways and hire people who thrive in the new environment.

## Core Ideas

- When your company is stagnating, do not half-ass AI adoption; sprinkle-on AI strategies will fail against young companies that are AI-native from the ground up
- Founder-led companies outperform professional-CEO-led companies because founders have the moral authority and willingness to take risks that hired CEOs are implicitly told to avoid
- AI customer service agents can be more personal than humans because they are instantly available, consistent, expert, and always on -- versus making customers wait days for a canned response
- Simplified, fair pricing builds customer trust and long-term retention; Intercom gave away $50M in ARR by simplifying pricing, and it paid off in loyalty
- Future organizations will be a medley of humans and agents in every direction, flatter and smaller, with agents not just in IC roles but potentially at the highest levels
