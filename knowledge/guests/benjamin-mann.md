# Benjamin Mann

Co-founder of Anthropic and tech lead for product engineering. Previously one of the architects of GPT-3 at OpenAI. Focuses on aligning AI to be helpful, harmless, and honest. Left OpenAI with seven others because safety wasn't the top priority there.

## Key Frameworks

### Economic Turing Test
Instead of the loaded term AGI, use "transformative AI" measured by the Economic Turing Test: if you contract an AI agent for a job and it turns out to be a machine, it passed. Apply this across a market basket of money-weighted jobs to measure when we've hit transformation.

### Constitutional AI
A list of natural language principles (drawn from UN Declaration of Human Rights, privacy policies, and internally generated values) that guides model behavior. This produces Claude's distinctive personality and character as a direct byproduct of safety research.

## Core Ideas

- AI scaling laws continue to hold across 15+ orders of magnitude; progress is accelerating, not plateauing, but faster release cadences create an illusion of slowing
- Safety and capability are convex, not competing: working on safety directly produced Claude's beloved personality and character
- The biggest near-term AI impact is expanding what existing workers can do (10-20x productivity), not wholesale replacement
- Curiosity, creativity, and kindness are the skills to instill in children for an AI future; factual knowledge will fade into the background
- Claude Code team has 95% of code written by Claude; legal and finance teams are also getting enormous value from AI coding tools
