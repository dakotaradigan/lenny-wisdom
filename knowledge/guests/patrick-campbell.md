## Key Frameworks

### Value Metric as #1 Pricing Lever
The single most impactful pricing decision is choosing how you charge (per user, per 1000 visits, per GB). Getting this right compensates for getting everything else wrong because it naturally segments customers by size, reduces churn by 20-25%, and doubles expansion revenue compared to flat pricing.

### Quarterly Pricing Cadence
Set a recurring calendar invite every three months to do one pricing-related thing -- a price increase, packaging change, localization, discount policy review. Most companies labor over product features and then pluck pricing out of thin air. Just doing something quarterly compounds into massive revenue improvements over time.

### Most Charitable Interpretation
When there is conflict, default to assuming the most generous interpretation of the other person's intent. This is a cultural value with real teeth: people who cannot operate this way must leave. It eliminates the infantilization of teams through excessive HR processes and lets smart people resolve friction directly.

## Core Ideas

- Bootstrapping to $200M was a great outcome but likely a strategic mistake -- they could have had a $1B exit with funding; knowing when to bootstrap vs raise is about matching your ambition to your funding model
- Revenue per customer is the one KPI to track for monetization health; you want it going up and to the right every quarter
- Tempo framework is more important than org design; if a team seems smart but is always planning rather than shipping, the problem is alignment on shipping cadence
- Values are not values unless they include a clear tradeoff that excludes some people; present values upfront in the interview process rather than discovering misalignment after hiring
