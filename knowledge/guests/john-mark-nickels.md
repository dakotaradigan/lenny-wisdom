# John Mark Nickels (J.M.)

Product leader at Uber (mobility), previously at Waymo (autonomous ride-hailing), DoorDash (platform), and Uber where he built the first version of Uber Pool and led pricing and matching infrastructure. Background in engineering management and equity trading. Passionate about bringing conscious leadership practices into high-performance product teams.

## Key Frameworks

### Conscious Leadership
Leadership is having influence -- everyone is a leader. The "conscious" part is becoming aware of inherited belief systems and biases, then taking responsibility for your influence. When brilliant people are in lizard-brain mode arguing in meetings, the bottleneck is not another PhD but a shift from fear and righteousness into curiosity, trust, and collaboration.

### Life's Objective Function
Clarify what you are optimizing for by thinking from the perspective of future-you. Five years from now you will not care about a slightly better presentation, but you will care deeply about your relationships. Awareness that life ends punctuates priorities and forces rethinking what matters day to day.

### What You Resist Persists
In high-stakes meetings, trying to suppress nervousness creates a self-reinforcing cognitive-emotive loop. Instead, allow the emotion, recognize you do not need external approval to be okay, and return focus to making a great product. Paradoxically, dropping ego-driven optics focus leads to better work and more recognition.

## Core Ideas

- Fear-based organizations produce results but at enormous human cost; shifting to trust and openness unlocks more creative collaboration among experts
- As a senior leader, speak last and create space for junior voices -- power dynamics silence people unless deliberately counteracted
- The best product work comes from tapping into purpose larger than your career -- making riders, drivers, and cities better off
- Feedback is a gift when approached from a learning stance; the presentation that did not go well is practice, not a career-ending event

## Notable Perspectives

> "Get clear on your objective function... most of us aren't tuned into an awareness that our lives will come to an end."

> "What you resist will persist and what you fear will appear."
