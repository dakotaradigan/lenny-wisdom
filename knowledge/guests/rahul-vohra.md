# Rahul Vohra

Founder and CEO of Superhuman, the high-end email client. Previously founded Rapportive (first Gmail extension to reach millions of users), which he sold to LinkedIn. Known for extreme attention to detail, contrarian product thinking, and developing a quantitative framework for measuring product-market fit that has become widely adopted across the startup ecosystem.

## Key Frameworks

### The Superhuman PMF Engine
A quantitative method for measuring product-market fit by surveying users: "How would you feel if you could no longer use the product?" If 40%+ say "very disappointed," you have PMF. The critical insight is to deliberately ignore feedback from users who would not be disappointed and focus only on those who love it -- segmenting by who your product is truly for, then doubling down on what they love while addressing what holds them back.

### Solution Deepening vs. Market Widening
Two categories of product work: solution deepening makes the product better for existing users, while market widening makes it available to more users (new platforms, integrations). Market widening is essential for growth but creates perceived slowdowns in product velocity since users do not see visible improvements. Understanding this distinction helps leaders communicate why shipping appears to slow during platform expansion phases.

### The Switch Log
A CEO productivity technique: instead of following a calendar, do whatever feels right and Slack-DM your EA each time you switch tasks (e.g., "TS: preparing board deck"). This creates an organic time audit revealing where your time actually goes versus where you think it goes. Rahul discovered he was spending only 6-7% of his time on product/design -- his zone of genius -- which led him to hire a president, go from eight direct reports to two, and reclaim 60-70% of his week for high-leverage work.

## Core Ideas

- True virality does not exist; no product sustains a viral factor above 1.0 for long. The real growth secret is word of mouth -- spontaneous sharing that cannot be measured or engineered through features alone. Build remarkableness into company values to make sharing inevitable.
- Bake growth ingredients into company values: "create delight," "deliver remarkable quality," and "build the extraordinary" are Superhuman's actual values, each designed to generate organic word of mouth.
- Extreme attention to detail is a competitive advantage; Superhuman created a custom font because existing options were not good enough, and positioned entirely around one attribute: speed.
- As CEO, define the role around your zone of genius rather than accepting the default eight-direct-report structure. Hiring a president to handle operations freed Rahul to focus on product, design, and marketing.

## Notable Perspectives

> "It is word of mouth. It is the virality you can't measure that isn't a mechanic that isn't in a feature. It is when one user spontaneously tells another user about your product."

> "Your calendar says what you thought you were going to do, but it's really only your trail of work that describes what you actually did."
