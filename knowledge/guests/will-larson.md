# Will Larson

CTO at Carta. Previously engineering leadership at Stripe, Uber, and Calm. Author of An Elegant Puzzle, Staff Engineer, and The Engineering Executive's Primer. Publishes regularly at lethain.com. One of the most influential voices in engineering leadership, focused on systems thinking, organizational design, and creating career paths for senior engineers.

## Key Frameworks

### Systems Thinking with Reality Check
Build mental models to represent complex systems (hiring pipelines, incident remediation, team dynamics). But when the model conflicts with reality, reality is always right and your model is always wrong. The gap between model and reality is where learning happens. The biggest failure mode of systems thinkers is deciding reality is wrong.

### Treat Engineers Like Adults
The ZIRP era created a culture of coddling engineers to retain them, which paradoxically prevented them from growing into senior leadership roles. If you cannot hold engineers accountable, you cannot put them in senior roles. The current market correction enables treating engineers as true peers with real responsibilities and real accountability.

### Engineering Allocation as Core Competency
In the post-ZIRP era, figuring out the right sizing and allocation of engineering teams has become a core leadership skill that was previously avoidable. Teams are disappearing, consolidating, and getting cut. Engineering leaders who only knew how to hire now need to learn how to right-size, which is a fundamentally different and harder skill.

## Core Ideas

- The shift from ZIRP to the current era means engineering leaders who excelled at hiring may now appear to be low performers because their primary skill is no longer needed
- Measure twice, cut once is wise, but you cannot measure infinite times and never cut; at Stripe, incident analysis became so thorough that it crowded out actually fixing incidents
- Staff engineer career paths require organizations willing to hold senior engineers accountable for outcomes, not just code quality
- Every framework has downsides; there is no framework that can be applied consistently and universally with good results
- The best engineering cultures give engineers real, hard problems and hold them accountable -- this is what enables career growth, not sheltering them
