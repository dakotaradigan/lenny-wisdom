# Alexander Embiricos

Alexander Embiricos is the product lead for Codex at OpenAI, their coding agent that has seen 20x growth since the launch of GPT-5. Previously, he founded a startup for five years and was a product manager at Dropbox. He was brought into OpenAI when his company was acquired.

## Key Frameworks

### Teammate Evolution Model
Codex's evolution mirrors hiring a new teammate: start with interactive pairing (IDE extension), then build trust through side-by-side work, then delegate asynchronous tasks, then eventually achieve proactivity where the agent identifies and does work without being asked.

### Meet Users Where They Are
The initial cloud-only Codex was too far in the future. The breakthrough came from bringing Codex into the IDE/terminal where engineers already work, creating a tighter feedback loop that naturally on-ramps users to more autonomous delegation.

## Core Ideas

- The current underappreciated limiting factor for AI impact is human typing speed and multitasking speed, not model capability
- OpenAI is truly bottoms-up in organization - very few companies have the talent caliber to operate this way
- Dogfooding signal can differ from general market signal; OpenAI's internal users were comfortable with async AI but the broader market needed interactive pairing first
- Codex writing and reviewing its own training infrastructure code is an early glimpse of recursive AI improvement
- The Sora Android app was built in 18 days and launched in 28 days total, demonstrating AI-accelerated development
