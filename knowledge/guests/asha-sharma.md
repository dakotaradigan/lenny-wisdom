# Asha Sharma

Asha Sharma is CVP of Product for Microsoft's AI platform, overseeing AI infrastructure, foundation models, and agent tool chains. She was previously COO at Instacart and VP of Product at Meta (Messenger, Instagram Direct). She sits on the boards of Home Depot and Coupang and is a second-degree black belt in Taekwondo.

## Key Frameworks

### Product as Organism (Not Artifact)
Traditional products are static artifacts. AI products are living organisms that get better with more interactions. Models are tuned to specific outcomes, creating products that think, live, and learn. This is the new IP of every company.

### Seasons of AI
Planning in AI means thinking in "seasons" rather than traditional roadmaps. Season one was prototyping, then models and reasoning, now the advent of agents. This framing helps teams navigate when a new model release can invalidate months of planning overnight.

### The Death of Org Charts
As the marginal cost of good output approaches zero and agents multiply, the org chart becomes the work chart. Fewer layers are needed when agents can handle increasing amounts of knowledge work.

## Core Ideas

- We're approaching a world where agents will outnumber employees in companies by 2026
- Post-training is the new pre-training: fine-tuning models for specific domains and outcomes is where value is created
- GUIs are being replaced by code-native interfaces as the primary way humans interact with AI systems
- Companies with 80,000+ AI deployments show the pattern: successful ones treat products as living systems that improve with use
- The biggest leadership lesson from Satya: create the conditions for others to succeed rather than trying to succeed yourself
