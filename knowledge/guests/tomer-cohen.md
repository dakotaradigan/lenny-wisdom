# Tomer Cohen

Chief Product Officer at LinkedIn overseeing all product development, design, business development, content creation, and customer operations. Led the transformation of LinkedIn's feed from a promotional activity stream into a daily-use knowledge exchange platform. Pioneered LinkedIn's shift to AI-first product development years before AI became mainstream.

## Key Frameworks

### "We Might Be Wrong But We're Not Confused"
Clarity of thought and clarity of execution are both non-negotiable. Being attached to being right creates lingering and hedging in organizations. Instead, get attached to clarity and focus. Make a call, align everyone in the same direction, and accept that you may be wrong -- but eliminate confusion. If priorities do not match where your best engineers and resources are deployed, you are confused.

### Disagree or Misunderstand?
When someone says "I'm not exactly clear on this," push to distinguish: are they disagreeing or genuinely misunderstanding? If misunderstanding, invest in mutual articulation until each person can state the other's view. If disagreeing, stop wasting time on fake alignment and address the real conflict.

### Start from the Peak, Work Backwards
Set ambitious goals based on what the product could become, not what the numbers show today. Calculate from the total addressable opportunity -- how many professionals exist who would benefit -- then set that as your mountain peak. The middle of the climb may be blurry, but base camp and the summit should be crystal clear.

## Core Ideas

- Turnaround products (minus-one to one) are harder internally than externally because you must change entrenched flows, metrics, and mental models before you can change the product
- LinkedIn's feed transformation required redefining its purpose: not a springboard for other products or an upsell feed, but a place where people who matter talk about things you care about professionally
- AI is the ultimate matchmaker; LinkedIn unified its AI team with product teams and retrained PMs to think AI-first by asking them to let go of what they built and rethink objectives with new technology
- The best product principles have teeth -- saying "build a simple product" is useless; saying "I'm willing to sacrifice X to achieve Y" is where real clarity lives
- Decisions that are not manifested in resourcing are not real decisions
