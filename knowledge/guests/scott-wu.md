# Scott Wu

Co-founder and CEO of Cognition, which makes Devin -- the world's first autonomous AI software engineer. Previously founded Lunchclub. Background in competitive programming and math competitions. His 15-person engineering team uses up to 5 Devins each, with ~25% of PRs committed by AI (expected to exceed 50% by end of year).

## Key Frameworks

### Jagged Intelligence
AI agents are not uniformly skilled like a human at a given level -- they are dramatically better at some things and dramatically worse at others. This "jagged intelligence" means the seniority analogy (junior vs. senior engineer) is imprecise. Product design must account for where the agent excels and where it needs human oversight.

### From Coder to Architect
As AI handles more execution, engineers evolve from writing code to managing fleets of AI agents -- becoming tech leads and architects. The job shifts from "write this function" to "scope this task, review this plan, verify this output." Programming becomes more important, not less, but the form factor changes dramatically.

## Core Ideas

- Cognition pivoted 8 times within coding agents before landing on Devin's current form; even with a clear thesis (coding + agents), finding the right product took extensive iteration
- The shift from imitation learning (train on internet text) to high-compute reinforcement learning (evaluate and learn from code execution) is the paradigm shift enabling autonomous agents
- Code is ideal for AI because it has automated feedback loops -- you can run code and verify correctness, which feeds the RL training cycle
- AI will create more engineers, not fewer; as the definition of "programming" broadens to include anyone who can direct a computer, the total number of people doing engineering work will increase
