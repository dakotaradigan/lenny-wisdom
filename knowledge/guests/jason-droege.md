# Jason Droege

CEO of Scale AI, which provides foundational training data to every major AI lab. Previously co-founded Scour (peer-to-peer file sharing) with Travis Kalanick and launched Uber Eats, growing it from an idea to $20B+ in revenue. This is his first interview since taking over as CEO after the $14B Meta deal.

## Key Frameworks

### Expert Data Labeling Evolution
Data labeling has evolved from generalists rating short stories to PhD-level experts spending hours building websites, explaining cancer topics, and debugging code for models. 80% of Scale's expert network has a bachelor's degree or higher; 15% have PhDs. The frontier is now digitizing human judgment specific to individual enterprises.

### Enterprise AI Accuracy Sweet Spot
AI excels when replacing processes that are 10-20% accurate -- getting to 50-80% is transformative. But replacing 98% accurate human processes with the remaining 2% improvement is extremely hard. Pick your AI use cases based on where the baseline human process has the most room for improvement.

### Entrepreneurship as Unique Insight
The key question for starting any business: "What insight do I have? Why am I so lucky to have this insight? Why, in a world of a million smart entrepreneurs, am I in the position where I likely have an insight that others do not?" If you can't answer this, reconsider the venture.

## Core Ideas

- In business and startups, everything is negotiable. Learning this at 19 from aggressive investors was highly imprinting and influenced the culture at both Scour and later Uber.
- AI models are getting smarter primarily through humans teaching them things they don't know -- filling gaps, correcting understanding, and showing what good looks like. This is the unglamorous "dig up every road" work of the AI revolution.
- RL environments (sandboxes where AI agents practice accomplishing goals) are the next frontier of training data. The challenge is making training data generalizable enough across the endless permutations of real-world software configurations.
- Evals are simply establishing benchmarks for what good looks like. They're increasingly the bulk of enterprise AI work because someone has to define the standard before an agent can meet it.
