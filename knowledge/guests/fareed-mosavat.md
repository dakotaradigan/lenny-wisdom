# Fareed Mosavat

Chief Development Officer at Reforge. Previously Director of Product for the Lifecycle (growth) team at Slack, responsible for the entire self-service business from signup through activation, expansion, and monetization. Built one of the first growth teams at Instacart. Was GM at Zynga. Started his career with nearly seven years at Pixar Animation Studios working on visual effects for Finding Nemo, Cars, Wall-E, and Up.

## Key Frameworks

### End Experience Over Process Fidelity
From Pixar: what matters on screen is the emotion and story, not whether the physics are real. If you have to "box the numbers" to make it look right, that is the right thing to do. Applied to product: your strategy doc is just an input into the end experience. Your frameworks do not need to fit perfectly together -- they need to help people solve actual problems.

### Getting Better Requires Real Reps
You cannot do homework or exercises or fake stuff to get better at product management. You have to work on real products at real companies with real customers and real data. Training, mentorship, and reading are layers on top of actual execution. The real acceleration comes from doing it and getting more reps, with a focus on the execution-to-learning loop.

## Core Ideas

- Product management skill develops primarily through repetition on real products, not through frameworks or courses alone
- Growth at Slack required connecting the dots between self-service product usage and sales-assisted conversion for larger accounts
- Great careers are often built by connecting dots backwards; diverse backgrounds (like starting at Pixar) provide unexpected product intuition
- At Reforge, the goal is unlocking expertise locked in the heads of great operators and making it accessible and actionable
- The best growth teams blend quantitative rigor with deep empathy for end-user experience
