# Gaurav Misra

Co-founder and CEO of Captions, one of the most successful consumer AI products with over 10 million users. Previously an early employee at Snap where he created the design engineering function. Captions lets anyone generate and edit talking videos with AI, targeting users who could not create video before due to lack of tools, skills, or time.

## Key Frameworks

### Public Roadmap vs Secret Roadmap
Maintain two roadmaps. The public roadmap contains features users have asked for -- but these are known to every competitor too, so they cannot be a differentiator. The secret roadmap contains bets on capabilities that no one has asked for yet but that create magic competitors cannot copy. The secret roadmap is where competitive advantage lives.

### Ship a Marketable Feature Every Week
Every engineer should ship a marketable product feature every week. This requires ruthless scope cutting and embracing technical debt. As a startup, your job is to take on technical debt because that is how you operate faster than bigger companies. Bigger companies either pay it immediately or are paying back debt from when they were startups.

## Core Ideas

- This is a rare moment where everything you try works because AI technology is advancing faster than anyone can build on it; this window will not last forever
- User acquisition is temporarily easier because people will try anything labeled "AI," but retention still requires solving real problems
- Check for user demand by looking at what has virality; viral signals indicate genuine interest that can be channeled into product features
- Building for non-professionals (people who could not create video before) is a larger and more durable market than building professional tools
- The fundamentals have not changed: identify a user problem, apply technology to solve it, find a mechanism to reach people with that problem
