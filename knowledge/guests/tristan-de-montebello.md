# Tristan de Montebello

Co-creator of Ultraspeaking, the public speaking workshop. In 2017, became the fastest competitor to reach the finals of the World Championship of Public Speaking. Built a unique game-based course that helps people quickly become more comfortable and effective speakers, especially impromptu speaking.

## Key Frameworks

### Speaking as a Subconscious Flow Process
The best speaking happens when you are not thinking about speaking at all. Instead of adding more conscious tactics and frameworks, remove the bugs in your software. Address root causes (discomfort with pausing, lack of trust in yourself under pressure) rather than symptoms (filler words, racing, monotony). When root causes are fixed, symptoms resolve automatically.

### Speaking as a Meta-Skill
Like fitness, improving your speaking ability has compounding effects across every domain of life -- work, friendships, family, confidence. It is not a specialized skill but a foundation skill. Most people grossly underestimate how transformative speaking improvement can be.

### Enjoyment as Barometer
If you are not enjoying speaking, you are doing it wrong. Humans evolved to speak; communication naturally rewards us. Lack of enjoyment signals overthinking, pressure, or being in a "professional voice" rather than your natural conversational self. Practice must also be enjoyable or you will quit within weeks.

## Core Ideas

- The "public speaking voice" is the enemy; people enter a different, stilted version of themselves the moment a timer starts; speak conversationally instead
- Look up when gathering thoughts, not down; looking up reads as thoughtful and confident, looking down reads as uncertain and invites interruption
- Sound like you mean it by varying your delivery; monotony comes from feeling boxed in and not trusting that expressiveness will work under pressure
- Practice through games and play, not through rehearsing scripts; games create the flow state that real speaking requires
- The fastest path to speaking improvement is proving to yourself that pausing, slowing down, and being expressive all work under pressure
