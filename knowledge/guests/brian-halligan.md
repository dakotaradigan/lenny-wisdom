# Brian Halligan

Co-founder and longtime CEO of HubSpot. Now Sequoia's in-house CEO coach, gathering groups of CEOs at "kids table" (under 100 employees) and "adults table" (over 100) to learn from each other.

## Key Frameworks

### LOCK(S) Algorithm for Evaluating Founders
L = Lovable (would you crawl across broken glass to work for them?). O = Obsession (deep founder-market fit, not a 6-month-old idea). C = Chip on shoulder (most great founders have a boulder on their shoulder). K = Knowledgeable (deep domain expertise). S = Student (constantly learning, going way back in history).

### 2004 Red Sox Team Building
Mix homegrown talent with selective experienced hires. People dramatically underrate their internal talent and overrate fancy logos on resumes. 100% attrition on big-company hires at HubSpot. If it's close between internal and external, give homegrown a shot.

## Core Ideas

- Starting a company has never been easier; scaling one into a durable organization has never been harder
- Adults table CEOs spend half their time recruiting and interviewing; the skill most CEOs must learn is giving hard feedback
- Everyone overrates their ability to interview; blind references and real interactive projects matter more than gut feeling
- Hire spiky people with weaknesses over consistent 3-out-of-4 candidates; shrink interview panels from 8 to 4
- The McKinsey hire almost never works at startups because by definition they're conservative about conventional wisdom

## Notable Perspectives
