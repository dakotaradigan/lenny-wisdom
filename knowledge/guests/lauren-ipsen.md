# Lauren Ipsen

Talent specialist at General Catalyst, formerly at boutique executive search firm Daversa Partners. Has placed over 80 senior product leaders across major tech companies. Started her career in broadcast before pivoting to executive search, where she built deep expertise in consumer mobile and platform product hiring.

## Key Frameworks

### Best Talent for This Role vs. Best Talent in the World
The most common hiring mistake is conflating these two questions. The best CPO from a public company may not be the best hire for a Series A startup. Match the candidate to what the company actually needs right now -- someone who can both lead and get hands dirty -- rather than chasing prestigious logos.

### Major vs. Minor Spikes
Product leaders come in flavors: platform/infrastructure, core product/consumer, and growth/monetization specialists. Founders must determine where the hire should spike rather than seeking a unicorn who does everything. Clarity on mandate, 90-day plan, and year-one success criteria prevents hiring for brand recognition over fit.

## Core Ideas

- Avoid the shiny object trap: senior executives from huge companies may be far from the work, surrounded by executors who actually built the product
- Look for candidates with a chip on their shoulder who still have something to prove, not just resume prestige
- Always be keeping a pulse on the market even when not actively hiring; build warm relationships with benchmark candidates
- Involve spouses and families in the closing process for senior hires; the decision is personal, not just professional
- Early-stage companies should default to "Head of" titles rather than VP or C-level; preserve room for the role to evolve
- The best founders plant seeds with potential hires a year or two before they need them
