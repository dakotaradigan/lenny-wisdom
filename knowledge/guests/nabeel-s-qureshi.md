# Nabeel S. Qureshi

Founder, writer, and researcher who spent nearly eight years at Palantir as a forward-deployed engineer working on public health, defense, and enterprise data problems. Previously founding member and VP of Business Development at GoCardless. Visiting scholar at the Mercatus Center researching AI policy.

## Key Frameworks

### Forward-Deployed Engineer Model
Engineers embed directly at customer sites four days a week, gaining a desk, badge, and deep immersion in the customer's actual problems. This creates a rapid build-show-iterate cycle -- four to five feedback loops per week -- that produces both custom solutions and features that fold back into the core platform.

### Murder Board for Project Plans
Before starting a new project, write a two-page plan including vision, goals, three-month tactics, and guiding principles. Invite three or four smart people unfamiliar with the project whose sole job is to tear it apart. Principles must be genuinely controversial -- if no one can disagree with a principle, it is not useful.

### No-Titles Meritocracy
Eliminating formal titles removes the memetic totem people compete for, reducing Goodhart's Law effects where people optimize for promotions rather than impact. The tradeoff is influence becomes more about relationships than explicit authority, and people must continuously earn their role.

## Core Ideas

- 30% of PMs who leave Palantir start a company -- the forward-deployed model is an exceptional founder training ground because it compresses the full startup cycle (find problem, build, sell, iterate) into repeatable reps
- Palantir screened for independent-minded, intellectually broad, intensely competitive people and used a "bad signal" strategy that deliberately turned off candidates who would not thrive there
- The transition from services company to product company requires solving individual customer problems so well that the solutions generalize into platform capabilities others will pay for
- Getting good at managing lots of data is an underappreciated competitive moat; Palantir's data platform capabilities are what make everything else possible

## Notable Perspectives

> "Say you got five reps of this in five disparate contexts -- you actually become very good at this cycle of go into the building, gain trust, talk about problems, build something that actually solves them."
