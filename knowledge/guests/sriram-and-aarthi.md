# Sriram Krishnan and Aarthi Ramamurthy

Tech's power couple. Sriram is a partner at a16z; Aarthi is a former PM who worked at Netflix, Snap, and Clubhouse. Between them they have worked at nearly every major tech company. They host the Good Time Show (famously featured Elon Musk on Clubhouse, sparking its explosive growth). Known for contrarian hot takes and deep consumer product knowledge.

## Key Frameworks

### High-Status Underserved Users
Every successful social platform bootstraps by attracting high-status people who are underserved by existing platforms. Each new network has its own breakout stars: Snapchat had Kylie Jenner, Instagram had Cristiano Ronaldo, TikTok had Charli D'Amelio. The key is these stars could not have risen on the previous platform -- TikTok rewarded video and dance skills that Instagram did not surface.

### The JTBD Counter-Argument
Sriram argues Jobs-to-be-Done is a terrible framework for social products. Facebook's People You May Know made YOUR experience slightly worse to help a NEW user get to 10 friends in 14 days. It performed no job for you -- it was optimizing for someone else's activation. Growth features in social networks often cannot be explained through JTBD.

## Core Ideas

- Cold DMs and cold emails create career-changing opportunities; Sriram's relationship with Elon started from an unsolicited DM where Elon reached out about a product issue at Twitter
- Clubhouse's strategy of attracting famous people (Naval, Marc Andreessen) was great for top-of-funnel but insufficient for retention; what matters more is homegrown talent native to the platform
- Homegrown stars (like Lenny for Substack, Sriram and Aarthi for Clubhouse) often bring more sustained value than celebrities imported from other platforms
- Every founder considering a social platform should study Eugene Wei's "Status as a Service" essay for understanding social network dynamics

## Notable Perspectives

> "I hate Jobs-to-be-Done. No successful company has ever been built on top of JTBD."

> "A lot of people trying to get ahead should just write cold emails, cold DMs, put out content. That leads to good things."
