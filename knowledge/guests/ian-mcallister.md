# Ian McAllister

Senior Director of Product and Tech for Vehicles at Uber. Previously spent 12 years at Amazon where he created Amazon Smile and led Alexa International. Also worked at Airbnb and Microsoft. Author of the legendary "What distinguishes the top 1% of PMs" Quora post.

## Key Frameworks

### Top 1% PM Attributes
Core skills for new PMs: communicate, prioritize, execute. For senior PMs: think big, earn trust, be driven by impact. Additional skills include simplifying, forecasting/measuring, understanding technical trade-offs, understanding good design, writing effective copy, digging for data, pushing back effectively, and adapting to change.

### Working Backwards (Amazon)
Start with the customer problem, not the solution. The press release and FAQ mechanism enforces this but isn't the only way. The most common failure: teams have a solution they want to build and retrofit a problem to justify it. Warning sign: when someone describes combining existing technologies or "ingredients in the pantry" rather than articulating a customer pain point.

### Trust as Currency
Trust is built by repeatedly setting and meeting expectations. Tell the truth without fail, launch what you said you'd launch, own your mistakes. Trust directly correlates with your ability to gain resources and grow your career.

## Core Ideas

- Communication is the most important PM skill at every level -- the stakes just get higher. Grade yourself after every meeting, email, or elevator conversation. Answer first, then explain.
- Prioritization is the number one differentiating skill. Given the same intelligence and resources, a PM with great prioritization instincts generates 5X the impact.
- Be driven by impact, not promotion. Wake up every day trying to have the biggest impact possible. Promotion follows impact, not the reverse.
- Writing on the internet can transform your career. Start by crystallizing your own thinking -- the audience will follow.
