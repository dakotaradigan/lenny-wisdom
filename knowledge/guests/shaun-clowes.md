# Shaun Clowes

CPO at Confluent. Previously CPO at MuleSoft (within Salesforce), CPO at Metromile, and six years at Atlassian where he ran Jira Agile and built the first B2B growth team. Created popular Reforge courses on retention and data for PMs. Known for his philosophical and tactical approach to making PMs genuinely excellent.

## Key Frameworks

### 80% Outside the Building
The time you spend looking inside the building does not benefit you much. Spend 80% of time thinking about customers, markets, and competitors. Most PMs get dragged into internal politics and scrum management. The PMs who consistently talk from the customer's and market's perspective get better almost immediately.

### Data as AI's Real Moat
LLMs can only be as good as the data they receive, and information has a decay rate. The real competitive advantage in AI products is not the model (replaceable) or the prompts (improvable) but the quality, timeliness, and breadth of context data. B2B SaaS apps look like "forms on databases" but are really business rules engines -- and those rules are nearly impossible to replicate.

## Core Ideas

- Product management is 15-20 years old and still produces wildly inconsistent outcomes; a 10x PM creates 100x returns because PM is fundamentally about leverage over resources
- Use LLMs to find where you are wrong: upload customer interviews and ask "where does my strategy NOT fit what customers said?" -- seek the counterfactual, not confirmation
- The "Feedback River" concept (from Sachin Rekhi): surround yourself constantly with user interviews, NPS data, competitor intel, and customer requests -- LLMs can now help synthesize this river
- SaaS defensibility comes from business rules, not UIs; Jira, Salesforce, and Workday are all "forms on databases" but the encoded business logic is what makes them nearly impossible to replicate
