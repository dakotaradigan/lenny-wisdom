# Christian Idiodi

Partner at Silicon Valley Product Group alongside Marty Cagan. Described by Cagan as "the most interesting man in the world." Works closely with companies implementing and improving their product management discipline.

## Key Frameworks

### Certificate of Appreciation
The essence of product management: you wake up on behalf of someone else to solve a problem for them, and you do it well enough that they give you something back in return (revenue, engagement, loyalty, reference). If it's not fun, you're not doing it right. If it's not hard, you're not doing it right.

### Four Risks of Product
Value (will they buy/use it?), Usability (can they use it?), Feasibility (can we build it?), Viability (does it work for our business?). PMs own value and viability. Value is the most important and most overlooked risk.

## Core Ideas

- People don't hate product management; they hate poor product management they've experienced. A competent PM makes everyone want them on the team
- Build trust by learning from the most influential person in the organization: either ask them to teach you or volunteer to help them
- Great product managers come from either massive career failures or learning from great product leaders
- When teams are given roadmaps of features to deliver, they assume value, which eliminates the need for (and undermines) product management
- The single best discovery method is finding reference customers to work with and designing the product alongside them
