# Marily Nika

Product Lead at Meta focusing on Metaverse, avatars, and identity. Previously at Google for eight years working on Google Glass, computer vision, and speech recognition ML. Teaches the most popular Maven course on AI and product management.

## Key Frameworks

### The Shiny Object Trap
Do not build AI for the sake of AI. Identify a real pain point first, determine if it can be solved in a smart way with data, then bring in a research scientist to build the model. The AI PM's job is to solve the right problem, while the generalist PM's job is to build and ship the right product.

### AI PM as Uncertainty Manager
Working with research scientists requires comfort with uncertainty that traditional PMs lack. Research timelines are unpredictable -- you might invest a year and shut it down. PMs must embrace this cadence rather than expecting the linear identify-build-launch cycle of traditional product development.

## Core Ideas

- Every PM will become an AI PM because every product will need personalization, recommendation, automation, or fraud detection
- Do not use AI for your MVP; fake the AI with a Figma prototype to validate the market first, then invest in model training
- Use ChatGPT today for mission statement refinement, user persona generation, and brainstorming AI-enhanced features
- The PM decides "good enough" quality thresholds for model accuracy -- a 70% vs. 80% accuracy bar is a product decision, not an engineering one
- Learn basic coding fundamentals even if AI tools can do it for you; understanding how things work gives confidence and better collaboration with data scientists
