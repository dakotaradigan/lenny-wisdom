# Guillermo Rauch

Founder and CEO of Vercel, creator of Next.js and Socket.IO -- some of the most popular JavaScript frameworks in the world. Vercel's platform runs websites for companies like Claude, Grok, and Midjourney. Their product v0 is one of the most popular AI website building tools, aiming to expand the total addressable market of builders from 5-20 million developers to 100 million product builders.

## Key Frameworks

### TAM Expansion Through AI Building Tools
If there are 5 million React developers and 20 million JavaScript developers, there are at least 100 million product builders (approximated by Slack's MAU of people who talk about building digital products). v0 lets anyone with an idea build and ship real software. The opportunity is not to replace developers but to let more people participate in building.

### Taste as a Learnable Skill
Taste is not an inaccessible innate trait. It is a skill that develops through exposure. One of Vercel's internal operating principles is "increasing exposure hours" -- quantifying how much time you spend watching how people use your products. Try lots of products, observe users constantly, and the muscle develops.

### AI Makes Everyone More Full-Stack
Designers can ship fully baked products. Product managers can prototype and ship to production. Specializations that were translation tasks (converting intentions into code) are going away, but understanding how things work under the hood becomes more valuable because it helps you influence the model to follow your intention.

## Core Ideas

- At some point, we need to stop talking about AI and just call it software; AI will become synonymous with software, and we will use software to build software
- Programming specializations that are translation tasks are going away, but knowing how things work under the hood will be more important for influencing AI tools
- The outputs of AI building tools must live up to the standard set by the best websites on the internet; low-quality AI output is not acceptable
- The meta pattern of Guillermo's career has been taking something very difficult but compelling and making it as easy as possible, from real-time communication to web frameworks to AI building
- v0 was a natural TAM expansion play for Vercel: get more people building, and they will need hosting and deployment infrastructure

## Notable Perspectives

> "We need to stop talking about AI at some point. I just see a future where AI becomes synonymous with software."

> "Taste, sometimes I think we think of as this inaccessible thing. I see it as a skill that can develop. Try to quantify how much time you expose yourself to watching how people use your products."
