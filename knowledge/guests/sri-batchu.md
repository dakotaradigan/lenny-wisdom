# Sri Batchu

Head of Growth at Ramp, the fastest growing SaaS and FinTech business in history (hit $100M ARR in 2 years, then grew 4X the following year). Previously Head of Growth at Instacart and VP of Ops at Opendoor. Specializes in applying technology and data to make every growth channel more efficient.

## Key Frameworks

### B2B Growth Channel Progression
Start with founder-led sales, then first salespeople, then low-cost targeted marketing (content, community, small events), then PR, then paid and brand, then SEO. Channels get more expensive as you progress and more effective as you understand your customers better. Do not skip steps.

### Growth Engineering for Sales
Dedicate engineers to supporting sales efficiency rather than just product features. At Ramp, growth engineers share the same pipeline and payback goals as the sales team -- they own the quota alongside sellers. These engineers use third-party data and AI to automate workflow, prioritize prospects, draft responses, and send targeted messaging.

## Core Ideas

- Cap table as growth strategy: Ramp got founders and operators as angel investors who became early customers and advocates -- more effective than VC introductions because users who love the product are more credible than investor recommendations
- The secret sauce of Ramp's growth is not one unique channel but making every channel technology-driven and data-informed; their distribution would not look far off from benchmarks, but each channel is more efficient
- The vast majority of Ramp's growth comes from new customer acquisition, not expansion -- they are simply adding new customers at an extraordinary rate
- Innovation/skunkworks team for cross-channel experiments that do not fit neatly into any one channel's mandate

## Notable Perspectives
