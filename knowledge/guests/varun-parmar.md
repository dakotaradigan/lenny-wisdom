# Varun Parmar

Chief Product Officer at Miro, previously SVP and CPO at Box. Leads Miro's product organization across European hubs. Specializes in building collaboration tools for cross-functional teams and competing in crowded markets by focusing on team-centric rather than persona-specific solutions.

## Key Frameworks

### Chess Points Against Competitors
Every code push to production either gains or loses competitive chess points. Products never remain the same -- every release from you or your competitor changes the board. This framing drives insane clarity about the impact of each release and prevents complacency even when you are market leader.

### Team-Centric vs. Persona-Centric Design
Most competitors build tools optimized for a single persona (designers, engineers). Miro deliberately builds for the intersection of cross-functional teams because innovation happens when diverse perspectives come together. This architectural decision is hard to copy retroactively and creates durable differentiation.

### Design Sprint Validation
Use Jake Knapp's five-day Design Sprint framework extensively for zero-to-one initiatives: quickly mock a concept, create a prototype, and validate with users within a week. Follow insights from sprints with deep empathy questions about how users arrived at their conclusions and what informed their feedback.

## Core Ideas

- Practicing empathy internally across distributed teams is just as important as practicing empathy with customers; ask what insights informed someone's prioritization decision
- Community love is the fuel that keeps product teams going; Miro's template community and user base are a key moat
- Broad industry applicability (manufacturing, healthcare, architecture, government) creates a different competitive position than tools narrowly focused on digital product teams
- The questions you ask in product reviews reveal more about culture than the answers; double-click into what insight led to each decision
- Advanced collaboration capabilities like workshop facilitation, async talk tracks synchronized with board movement, and team rituals serve use cases that competitors overlook
