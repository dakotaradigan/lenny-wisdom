# Laura Schaffer

VP of Growth at Amplitude. Previously built the growth engineering and growth product team at Twilio from scratch after starting in product marketing. Early career at Bandwidth where she transitioned from sales to e-commerce by identifying patterns in customer conversations.

## Key Frameworks

### Career Growth Through Customer Proximity
Instead of relying solely on your manager for career advancement, build a brand as the person who deeply knows customers. Share insights proactively -- voice-of-customer reports, quarterly sessions -- and executives will seek you out. This creates career opportunities that transcend your current role and manager.

### Good Friction vs. Bad Friction
Adding signup questions at Twilio that asked about coding language and use case improved conversion by 5 percent, not because of personalization downstream, but because it comforted anxious new users. Users signing up for something new are looking for the bogeyman; questions that align with their organic concerns ("Do they support my language?") reassure them. Not all friction is bad -- good friction reduces anxiety.

## Core Ideas

- Your executive team loses access to customer insights as the company grows; your superpower is staying close to customers and surfacing insights that align to north star metrics
- Start sharing customer insights without asking permission; create a digest, build an audience internally, then use that trust to propose larger initiatives
- The best career moves come from identifying problems the company does not yet see and proposing solutions, not from waiting for a manager to create a path
- Ungate your knowledge: share skills and expertise broadly within the company, even outside your role, to build internal brand recognition

## Notable Perspectives
