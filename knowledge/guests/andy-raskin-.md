# Andy Raskin

Andy Raskin helps CEOs align their teams around a "strategic narrative" - a story that drives success in marketing, sales, product, fundraising, and recruiting. He developed his approach after discovering screenwriting principles while trying to fix a terrible startup pitch, and has been refining it for over two decades.

## Key Frameworks

### Old Game to New Game
Instead of the "arrogant doctor" pitch (problem, solution, why we're better), start with a fundamental shift in the world - from the old game to a new game. The archetypal example is Salesforce: "Software is over, the cloud is the new game, are you in or out?" This defines a movement rather than solving a problem.

### Strategic Narrative Structure
(1) Name the shift happening in the world; (2) Show the stakes - winners and losers of this shift; (3) Tease the "promised land" - the future for those who adapt; (4) Introduce your capabilities as the way to get there; (5) Show evidence that you can deliver. This aligns everything from sales to product roadmap.

## Core Ideas

- A strategic narrative becomes a north star for product roadmap, fundraising, recruiting, and sales - not just marketing
- Changing the story (not the product) can transform results, as his own startup proved when the same product got funded after reframing
- Zuora's "subscription economy" narrative and Gong's "goodbye opinions, hello reality" are powerful examples of movement-based positioning
- Competitors get repositioned as part of the "old game" rather than requiring feature-by-feature comparison
