# Vijay Iyengar

Head of Product at Mixpanel. Unusual career path from engineering intern at Amazon to engineer at Uber to engineering manager at Mixpanel, then pivoting to Director of Product and Head of Product. Led Mixpanel's strategic refocus from a multi-product suite back to a single core analytics product, driving retention from 60% to 90% and NPS from 16 to 50.

## Key Frameworks

### Churn-Driven Roadmap
When in crisis, take all churn reasons collected by customer success and sales, group by category, sort descending by ARR, and make the top 10 items your roadmap. Give engineers direct customer access for each bucket. This optimizes for speed through extreme clarity, though it outlives its usefulness quickly once table stakes are covered.

### Invest Profits Not People
When expanding to new product lines, never take people away from the investment in your core product. You leave yourself ripe for disruption. Instead, invest profits or venture capital into new ventures while continuing to out-invest everyone in your core. Mixpanel's expansion into messaging and data infrastructure pulled resources from core analytics, enabling competitors to close the gap.

### Design-Led Architecture Phase
After closing feature gaps through speed, shift to a design-led initiative focused on system architecture: what are the few core building blocks, how do they relate, and how do users discover them? Great products win or lose on their architecture (like Notion's pages-and-blocks model). Give designers three months of dedicated space away from tactical fires to think holistically.

## Core Ideas

- Engineers transitioning to product must unlearn the instinct to immediately say no; ideas are fragile and a hard no can kill a high-impact direction; try to make yes work first
- Shipping 100 features fast without holistic design leads to diminishing returns because reach of each feature is low and nothing connects coherently
- You cannot mow your lawn while your house is on fire; sequence crisis response (close churn gaps) before design excellence
- Consistency in visualization and UX lets every new feature and enhancement instantly apply everywhere, multiplying reach
- Context determines approach: in competitive crisis, optimize for speed; once stable, optimize for design and architecture
