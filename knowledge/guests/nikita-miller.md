# Nikita Miller

SVP and Head of Product at The Knot Worldwide, previously VP Product at Dooly and Head of Growth and Retention at Trello for five years. Specializes in building cross-functional alignment and scaling product teams, particularly in distributed and remote settings.

## Key Frameworks

### Roles and Responsibilities Contract
Have each function (product, design, engineering, data) write down their own expectations and what they expect from their counterparts. Then review together and arrive at a shared contract. This surfaces misalignment early and prevents the friction that comes from unstated assumptions about who does what.

### Outcomes-Output Balance
The industry swung too far toward pure outcomes thinking and forgot that output is an indicator of progress. If a team does all the right ideation and documentation but is not shipping frequently enough to market, the outcomes thinking is hollow. Track both and treat shipping velocity as a health signal.

### Product-Design-Engineering-Data Quartet
Expand the traditional triad to include data science as a first-class partner. Data scientists should co-own experiment briefs and analysis alongside PMs rather than being an afterthought service function.

## Core Ideas

- Building for product managers is bittersweet because defining productivity for PMs, designers, engineers, and data scientists requires fundamentally different measures -- no one tool solves all use cases
- Planning weddings and managing product development have surprising similarities: high-stakes, multi-stakeholder projects with long timelines and an enormous number of decisions
- Progressive disclosure -- starting simple and growing with user sophistication -- was Trello's key differentiator against tools that started with complexity
- The single question Nikita asks constantly to drive team performance is centered on urgency and shipping cadence
