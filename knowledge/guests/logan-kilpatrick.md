# Logan Kilpatrick

Former Head of Developer Relations at OpenAI. Previously machine learning engineer at Apple and advised NASA on open source policy. Supported developers building on OpenAI APIs and ChatGPT during the critical first years after ChatGPT's launch.

## Key Frameworks

### Context Is All You Need
The biggest barrier to getting great output from AI is insufficient context. Language models are like meeting a human-level intelligence with zero context about you, your goals, or your situation. Providing background, links, examples, and constraints dramatically improves output quality. Prompt engineering is fundamentally a human communication skill.

### Vertical vs. General AI Products
OpenAI focuses on general reasoning capabilities. Startups should build vertical, domain-specific products rather than general-purpose assistants that will compete directly with ChatGPT. Harvey (legal AI) succeeds because it builds custom models for a specific domain. Companies building general agents should expect direct competition from OpenAI.

## Core Ideas

- High agency and urgency are the top two hiring attributes at OpenAI; people who hear a customer problem and immediately push toward a solution without waiting for consensus
- OpenAI's team bounced back from the board crisis within days because mission-driven cultures recover faster from trauma
- Engineering is one of the highest-leverage use cases for AI today, likely yielding at least 50% productivity improvements on lower-hanging tasks
- GPTs enable non-developers to solve domain-specific problems by giving the model enough context through custom instructions and file uploads
- The smiley face at the end of a prompt can marginally improve model performance because training data reflects human communication patterns
