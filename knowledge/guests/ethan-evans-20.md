## Key Frameworks

### The Magic Loop
A five-step repeatable career growth process: (1) Do your current job well -- you cannot grow if your manager wishes you were different. (2) Ask your manager how you can help -- very few people do this, and it immediately sets you apart. (3) Do whatever they ask, even unglamorous work. (4) Go back and ask if there is something that helps them AND advances your goal -- this is where the magic happens, because managers help those who help them. (5) Repeat. The loop works in almost every circumstance, even with mediocre managers, because it puts career control in your hands.

### Invention Needs Only Two Hours a Month
People overestimate how much time invention requires. You need only two hours once a month to generate one good idea. But expressing that idea may take years -- Prime shipping is still being refined after 20+ years. The Kindle, decades old, is still getting better. You do not need many good ideas to be seen as tremendously inventive.

## Core Ideas

- Your career is your responsibility; what your manager "should" do and four dollars will get you a cup of coffee at Starbucks
- Managers from their side can initiate the Magic Loop by proactively asking employees about their career goals and forming an explicit partnership
- The Magic Loop works at every level, from entry-level employees to senior executives; both ends of the spectrum have reported transformative results
- People who feel exploited by the loop (doing extra work with no reciprocation) are dealing with rare bad managers and should consider changing teams, not abandoning the framework
- Great managers are busy and well-intentioned but forget to invest in your career; the loop compensates for this structural reality
