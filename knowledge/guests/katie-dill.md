# Katie Dill

Head of Design at Stripe overseeing product design, brand, research, content strategy, and design ops. Previously Head of Design at Lyft and Head of Experience Design at Airbnb. Has built and scaled design teams 10x at three hypergrowth companies, two of which (Airbnb and Stripe) are among the largest and best-designed products in the world.

## Key Frameworks

### Quality Levels (1 through 5)
Level 1: Does the thing work? Level 2: Is it error-free? Level 3+: Does it exceed expectations? The right quality bar depends on user expectations and competitive landscape. Like the first car versus modern luxury vehicles -- when competition is fierce, the stitching and door-sound details distinguish ordinary from premium.

### Quality Is Growth
Not a trade-off between growth and quality -- quality drives growth. At Stripe, improving checkout experience quality yielded a 10.5% increase in merchant revenue. Growth teams maniacally focused on better experiences see direct ties to business metrics through improved activation, usability, and conversion.

### Trust Before Change
After joining Airbnb and pushing for change in her first month, the design team staged an intervention -- reading from papers all the things she was doing wrong. The missing piece: she had not earned trust. The lesson: come in listening, not swinging. Build the trust bank before making withdrawals. A few months after adjusting, her team had the best engagement scores in the company.

## Core Ideas

- Beauty enhances functionality by increasing trust, approachability, and willingness to use a product; the declining use of the word "beauty" in culture correlates with an over-index on functionality alone
- Designers should not frame their work as "design goals" separate from "business goals" -- the conversation should be "what are we trying to build?" with quality as a non-negotiable element
- Multidisciplinary teams are checks and balances on each other: designers bring the emotional lens, PMs bring the metric lens, engineers bring the feasibility lens
- 99% of top e-commerce sites have checkout flow errors that hinder conversion -- small quality improvements compound into material revenue impact

## Notable Perspectives

> "Quality is growth. Things that are more beautiful increase trust."

> "You can inflict change on people, but if you want to do it with them, trust is the key element."
