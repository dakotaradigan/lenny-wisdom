# Gustaf Alstromer

Group Partner at Y Combinator where he has worked with over 600 companies in six years. Previously at Airbnb for over four years where he started the original growth team. His experience at Airbnb set an unreachable bar -- almost no alumni he has spoken to have found another experience as special. Also at the heart of YC's increased focus on climate tech.

## Key Frameworks

### Why Companies Fail Is Simple
If you drill down, companies fail because they do not talk to users, which means they do not find product-market fit. If they do not find product-market fit, nothing else matters. YC's headline "Make things people want" is still true and always will be.

### Founder Motivation Diversity
Motivations for starting companies vary enormously: solving a technical problem, proving themselves, changing the world, or just wanting to build something big. No single motivation predicts success better than others. The motivation will evolve over time into simply enjoying the act of running the company. The real question is: "Why will you come in and work late after four years when you have no money left and everything's going to shit?"

### What Made Airbnb Special
Three factors: (1) Hiring people who were genuinely excited about Airbnb specifically, not just any good company. (2) Culture interviews that mapped candidates to core values. (3) Diverse backgrounds -- most early PMs were former founders, not career big-company people. This set a self-reinforcing bar. Plus, Airbnb had such a strong business model that it was hard to fail at the company level, which gave teams permission to take risks.

## Core Ideas

- Starting a company comes from a positive place more often than desperation, though desperation can be a valid motivator
- The first 10-15 hires set the cultural standard that persists long after those people leave
- Having an incredible underlying business model gives teams psychological safety to take risks and experiment
- The strongest signal of future success is durable founder drive, regardless of what motivates it
- Airbnb's founders tried to recreate YC internally (demo days, doing things that do not scale) because that experience shaped their operating philosophy
