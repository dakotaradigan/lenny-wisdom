# Camille Hearst

Head of Fan Monetization at Spotify. Previously head of product for creators at Patreon, product marketing manager at YouTube, and the second PM on iTunes. Also a former founder (Kit, acquired by Patreon).

## Key Frameworks

### Direct Fan-to-Artist Monetization
Beyond streaming royalties, create ways for fans to directly support artists through merch, listening parties, exclusive access, and rewards for top listeners. The goal is connecting passionate fanbases with monetization regardless of artist size.

## Core Ideas

- Fan passion exists at every level, not just for superstars; Spotify focuses on helping smaller artists monetize their dedicated fanbases
- Merch on Spotify is more mature than people realize; the team focuses on making it prominent and offering exclusive rewards for top listeners
- Listening parties create shared experiences around music that can be monetized
- The Steve Jobs elevator test was real at Apple: you needed to be able to articulate what you do at the company at any moment
- The creator economy requires understanding both the creator side (tools, monetization) and the platform side (distribution, discovery)

## Notable Perspectives

> "One of the things that we all know is it's not just the big huge superstars who have those rabid and super engaged fan bases. People are really passionate just about supporting the artists that they love in general."
