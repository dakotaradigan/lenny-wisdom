# Luc Levesque

Chief Growth Officer at Shopify. Previously recruited personally by Mark Zuckerberg to grow Facebook Messenger, Instagram, and WhatsApp. VP of Growth and GM at TripAdvisor. Growth advisor to Twitter, Pinterest, Patreon, Thumbtack, and Canva.

## Key Frameworks

### Signs of Excellence Hiring
The best predictor of future performance is past performance. Look for three or more "signs of excellence" -- repeated demonstrations of standing above the crowd, whether professional or personal. One powerful signal: when someone's former boss leaves a company and comes back to poach them, it means the person with the most knowledge of their performance risked their own reputation to re-recruit them.

### The 10x Growth Advisor
A great growth advisor who deeply understands a channel can literally say one sentence that changes a company's trajectory. This is because it takes years to build deep channel knowledge through exposure to thousands of experiments, but only seconds to communicate the insight. Structure advisorships with equity (for incentive alignment), front-loaded vesting, and a three-month cliff so both sides can walk away if impact is not materializing.

## Core Ideas

- Impact is the only metric that matters; reward outcomes, not activities or hours worked
- Do not invest in growth before product-market fit; exposing a bad product to the market through growth levers does more damage than good because users rarely try something twice
- Most successful companies got where they are through one dominant growth loop, not many; find the one and master it
- When recruiting senior leaders, involve the entire executive team in closing, make it personal by involving the candidate's family, and be absolutely relentless over months
- Create a personal blueprint document listing your quirks and working style; share it with new hires to quickly calibrate the working relationship
