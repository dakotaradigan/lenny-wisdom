# Alisa Cohn

Alisa Cohn is an executive coach ranked the number one startup coach for four consecutive years by Global Gurus and named one of the top 50 coaches in the world by Thinkers50. She works with C-suite execs at startups and Fortune 500 companies, providing specific, actionable scripts for leadership challenges.

## Key Frameworks

### Difficult Conversation Scripts
Start by naming the topic neutrally ("I want to chat about what I'm hearing from your peers"), share specific observations, frame improvement as aligned with their goals ("we both know the most important way you can succeed"), and end with a clear call to action for what changes look like.

### Three Questions to End Every Meeting
Close every meeting with questions that ensure maximum forward progress: What did we decide? Who is doing what by when? What else needs to happen that we haven't covered?

### Founder Prenup
A set of questions potential co-founders should discuss before partnering to ensure alignment on values, working styles, conflict resolution, equity, and exit scenarios.

## Core Ideas

- Your job as a leader is not to make employees happy - it's to drive results while developing people, even when that means uncomfortable feedback
- The reason you avoid giving feedback is worth examining: is it about them being upset, or about your own discomfort with their reaction?
- Build the relationship first with positive feedback so constructive feedback arrives through a lens of "this person wants to help me"
- Practice scripts out loud before delivering them - wrapping your mouth around the words makes the conversation go dramatically better

## Notable Perspectives

> "Hope for the future is so important. 'I know this is going to be challenging for you to hear... but I want you to know it's really important to me that you're able to succeed in your career here.'"

> "This employee continuing to not really do a great job... you don't want to push them because you don't want to upset them. Ultimately, that leads to the demise of your company."
