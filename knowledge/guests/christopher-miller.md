# Christopher Miller

VP of Product for Growth and AI at HubSpot, where he helped transform the company into one of the most successful PLG businesses in history. Started as an IC PM and built HubSpot's early growth team, shifting the company from primarily sales-led to a hybrid PLG model over seven years.

## Key Frameworks

### Radical Accountability in Growth
Growth teams should adopt an aggressive ownership mentality where every problem is their problem. At HubSpot, the growth team volunteered to take over self-service subscription revenue that no other team was working on and "immediately blew it up." This hunger-driven approach of finding neglected opportunities signals value and earns expanded scope.

### Traits for Growth PMs
Four essential traits: relentless curiosity (insatiable desire to understand, unafraid to admit ignorance), resilience (70-80% of growth experiments fail, so you must avoid grasping for small wins), coachability (PLG is still fledgling and looks different at every company), and creativity (valuing simple solutions over complex ones, being ambivalent to solution complexity).

## Core Ideas

- If your growth team is hitting more than 30-40% of experiments, you are thinking too small. The failure rate is a feature, not a bug -- it means you are swinging at big opportunities.
- Talking to customers who chose NOT to use your product is often more valuable than talking to existing users, because their decisions are frequently more emotional and instinctual than calculated.
- Finding a single mentor or sponsor who invests real capital in your career development is more impactful than any course or reading. The difference between a mentor and a sponsor is that a sponsor is willing to gamble professional capital on you.
- Proactivity and being ahead of where leadership is thinking is how PMs earn a seat at the table -- the pricing and packaging story at HubSpot came from an IC PM pitching a contrarian view at a party.
