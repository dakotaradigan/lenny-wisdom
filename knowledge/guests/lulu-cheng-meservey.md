Former Head of Communications at Substack, now advises companies on communications strategy. Previously at Activision Blizzard. Known for taking bold, unconventional approaches to PR that generated outsized attention for Substack. Arguably the most innovative comms person in tech.

## Key Frameworks

### Cultural Erogenous Zones
Find what your audience already cares passionately about, then shape your message to connect to that passion. You cannot change someone's worldview; you can only build a bridge from what they care about (X) to what you offer (Y). Kamala Harris connected K-12 education to national defense by noting you need a 10th-grade reading level to enlist in the army -- that is finding the cultural erogenous zone.

### Concentric Circles Distribution
Spread messages outward from your inner circle: employees first, then investors, then power users, then broader audience. Each circle assumes the inner circle knows better and follows their lead. You cannot skip a circle -- if employees are not bought in, everything downstream is undermined. Map five to six audience circles, rank by influence and credibility, and work outward sequentially.

### Comms Math Formula
Start with a business goal (not a comms goal). Identify who needs to do what for that goal to come true. Determine what they need to believe to take that action. Then find where they reside intellectually (podcasts, subreddits, conferences) and deliver the message through those channels.

## Core Ideas

- Make ideas spread by making them repeatable: jokes, analogies, mental images, and stories beat adjectives every time
- For startups, your biggest enemy is the status quo; making mistakes of commission beats omission because you observe, learn, and adapt
- Put the pill in some cheese: wrap your hard message inside something the audience already wants to consume
- Delete cliches and common parlance from your messaging; use language a second-grader could understand
- Most comms problems can be solved with a one-page Google Doc listing audiences, their cultural erogenous zones, and where they reside intellectually
