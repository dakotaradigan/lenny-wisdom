# Anuj Rathi

Anuj Rathi is Chief Product and Marketing Officer at Jupiter Money. He was previously SVP of Revenue and Growth at Swiggy (7 years), VP of Product at Snapdeal, and the very first Product Manager at Flipkart. He was the single most requested India-based product leader by Lenny's audience.

## Key Frameworks

### Lazy, Vain, and Selfish Users
Modern internet consumers have three attributes: Lazy (blow my mind or I won't pay attention), Vain (I have existing habits, don't expect me to change easily), and Selfish (show me what's in it for me). Design onboarding and product experiences through this lens.

### Can't Do, Won't Do, Wasn't Set Up To Do
Three reasons things don't happen as a leader wants: capability issue (can't do), motivation/alignment issue (won't do), or organizational design issue (wasn't set up to do). Each requires a different response: coaching, alignment conversations, or structural change.

### Marketing-to-Product Continuity
The user journey from marketing message to app onboarding should be seamless. Continue the exact positioning and language from the ad/promotion through the splash screen, onboarding, and first experience rather than treating marketing and product as separate worlds.

## Core Ideas

- India's product ecosystem started roughly in 2010 and is still maturing; the entire surrounding ecosystem (business leaders, sales, ops) also needed to learn software thinking
- India's UPI digital payments revolution and massive smartphone adoption created entirely new product design paradigms
- Most PMs are too engrossed in their product and assume users think about it all day; the marginal user doesn't care about your product at all
- Cross-selling within multi-product apps fails when you're "greedy" about showing everything at once rather than timing introductions to adjacent products
