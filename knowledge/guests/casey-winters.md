# Casey Winters

Chief Product Officer at Eventbrite. Previously led growth at Pinterest (40M to 150M MAU), led demand growth at GrubHub from Series A to IPO, and advised Airbnb, Canva, Tinder, and Thumbtack through Greylock.

## Key Frameworks

### Chapter One Communication
When presenting to executives, start at "Chapter One" (strategy context and assumptions), not "Chapter Six" (tactical details). But also don't start at the beginning of time. Find the last obvious point and go from there. Executive communication is actually executives communication: tailor to each individual's style and concerns.

### Kindle vs Fire Strategies
Non-scalable hacks (kindle strategies) exist only to unlock scalable growth (fire strategies) that could take you to millions of users. Don't confuse the two or invest too long in kindle strategies.

## Core Ideas

- People dramatically under-communicate upward, then complain that executives are out of touch
- De-risk important meetings with pre-meetings and role-playing different executives' likely questions
- PMs who try to handle everything themselves instead of escalating constraints are not being good leaders; change the circumstances, don't just cope
- The PM career spectrum ranges from analytical (measuring, optimizing) to creative (envisioning, storytelling), and most PMs should stretch toward the creative end
- Operations is often a sign of inefficiency that should be automated over time, but the freed-up people move to the next frontier of problems
