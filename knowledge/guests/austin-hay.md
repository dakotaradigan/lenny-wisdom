# Austin Hay

Austin Hay is one of the smartest people in MarTech. He's head of marketing technology at Ramp and has advised Notion, Airbnb, Walmart, Postmates, and Robinhood on MarTech strategy. Previously VP of business operations at Runway, VP of growth at mParticle, and fourth employee at Branch Metrics. He teaches MarTech at Reforge.

## Key Frameworks

### MarTech as Product Management
MarTech is a product manager whose focus is the marketing technology platform - the collection of first-party and third-party tools that enable growth. It sits at the crossroads of product, growth, engineering, and marketing.

### Village to Central Ownership
At 30 people, everyone chips in on tools (village approach). At 100-150 people, you need someone centrally owning data flows, schemas, procurement, and legal contracts. The structure varies by B2B vs B2C and company size.

### Probabilistic vs Deterministic Data
The golden years of deterministic matching (2010-2020) are over. Marketers now must make decisions with probabilistic data - building models from 30% of the population and extrapolating to 100%.

## Core Ideas

- Tools are just meant to solve problems; the problem set for marketing technologists is the system and platform enabling growth
- In B2C, the CDP is typically the source of truth and MarTech fits with growth; in B2B, CRM (Salesforce) becomes central and MarTech fits with rev ops
- Companies face infinite liability if marketing technology contracts aren't managed properly (data privacy, vendor agreements)
- The shift from IDFA-based tracking to probabilistic attribution fundamentally changed how growth marketing operates
- MarTech roles can emerge from growth, user acquisition, or product ops backgrounds
