# Michael Truell

Co-founder and CEO of Anysphere, the company behind Cursor -- the fastest-growing AI code editor, reaching $300M ARR two years after launch. Studied CS and math at MIT with a decade of AI research. Thinks deeply about what comes after traditional code and the evolution of software creation.

## Key Frameworks

### The World After Code
Programming will evolve from writing formal code to specifying intent through a higher-level, more English-like representation of software logic. This is neither today's text editing nor a chatbot -- it is a new medium where humans retain precise control over software while working at a much higher level of abstraction.

### Foreground vs Background AI Work
The most successful users of AI coding tools chop work into small specify-build-review cycles rather than handing off large tasks. Autocomplete sits at one extreme of tight iteration; background agents sit at the other. The sweet spot shifts as models improve, but tight loops still dominate today.

### Dogfooding as Product Truth
Every magic moment in Cursor involves a custom model. The team uses the product all day, every day, and refuses to ship anything that is not useful to themselves. Dogfooding instills realism about where AI capabilities actually stand versus where demos suggest they could be.

## Core Ideas

- Taste -- having the right idea for what should be built -- will be increasingly more valuable than carefulness as AI handles more implementation details
- The most counterintuitive lesson was needing to do custom model development; they expected to just use off-the-shelf models but found every differentiating feature required custom work
- Exponential growth feels slow when the numbers are small; Cursor's growth was remarkably consistent month-over-month rather than driven by a single inflection point
- They initially tried building for mechanical engineering to avoid competition, then realized they should pursue the space they were most passionate about and where incumbents lacked sufficient ambition
