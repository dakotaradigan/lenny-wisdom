# Marty Cagan

Founder of Silicon Valley Product Group (SVPG). Former VP of Product at Netscape and SVP at eBay. Author of *Inspired* and *Empowered*, two of the most widely read books on product management. Has spent 20+ years coaching product teams at top companies worldwide.

## Key Frameworks

### Empowered Product Teams vs Feature Teams
Feature teams receive roadmaps of prioritized features from stakeholders and execute them. Empowered product teams receive problems to solve and are given the skills and autonomy to discover the best solutions. The difference is outcomes vs output. Only about 10-15% of companies operate with true empowered product teams.

### The Four Risks of Product Discovery
Every product solution must be validated across four dimensions: valuable (customers will buy/use it), usable (customers can figure it out), feasible (engineers can build it), and viable (it works for the business). Product managers own valuable and viable; designers own usable; engineers own feasible. All four must succeed or the product fails.

### Three Sacred PM Responsibilities
A product manager must have unencumbered direct access to three things: (1) users and customers, (2) engineers on their team, and (3) business stakeholders. Any role or process that mediates these relationships undermines the PM's effectiveness. Everything else can be delegated.

## Core Ideas

- The biggest gap in the industry is between how the best product companies work and how the rest operate. Most companies are feature factories where PMs are essentially project managers herding cats rather than discovering winning solutions.
- Companies lose their product mojo when product becomes less valued than sales, marketing, and finance. Good product people leave, and the company devolves into execution mode without innovation -- a disease Steve Jobs identified in 1995.
- Solution discovery is where teams succeed or fail, not problem discovery. Founders usually know the problem. PMs should spend minimal time validating known problems and maximum time crafting solutions that customers will actually choose over alternatives.
- Scaling with process (e.g., SAFe) destroys product innovation. The only effective way to scale is with strong leaders who coach their teams. The "disease of process people" replaces empowerment with control.
- User research should be evaluative, not just generative -- focused on finding reasons people will not use your product. PMs and designers must be present during testing; reports alone get ignored.
