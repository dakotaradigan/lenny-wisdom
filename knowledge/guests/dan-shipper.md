# Dan Shipper

Co-founder and CEO of Every, an AI-native media and software company with 15 employees, four shipped products, seven-figure revenue, and a daily newsletter. Engineers at Every write zero manual code -- everything is built through AI agents. Known for being at the bleeding edge of AI adoption in both editorial and product work.

## Key Frameworks

### AI Leash Length as AGI Metric
The progression of AI capability can be measured by how long you can leave it working unsupervised. Tab-complete was seconds, ChatGPT responses were minutes, Claude Code with Opus 4 is 20-30 minutes. AGI arrives when it becomes economically profitable to run agents indefinitely without human intervention -- like a teenager who just lives their life productively.

### Head of AI Operations Role
Every has a dedicated person whose entire job is building prompts and workflows to help every employee become more efficient with AI. This role constantly identifies repetitive tasks and automates them, creating organizational leverage that compounds over time.

## Core Ideas

- Claude Code is the most underrated tool for non-technical people. It can process entire folders of meeting notes, analyze patterns across documents, and work autonomously for 20-30 minutes. The only barrier is the intimidation of using a terminal.
- AI will reshore American jobs rather than eliminate them. Cheap intelligence makes expensive services affordable for small companies and individuals, while enabling US-based workers to serve far more customers with AI amplification.
- Junior employees who use AI to encode feedback into prompts and never make the same mistake twice can compress a year of progress into two months. AI is not replacing entry-level workers -- it is supercharging the best ones.
- The skill of using AI is itself a skill that develops over time. Studies comparing "doctors vs. doctors plus AI vs. AI alone" are misleading because doctors have not yet developed expertise in using AI effectively.
