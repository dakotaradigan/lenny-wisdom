Background as engineer at NYT and designer at Dropbox and Square. Uniquely straddles research, engineering, and design.

## Key Frameworks

### Model Training as Debugging
Model training is more art than science. Data quality is paramount. Debugging models resembles debugging software -- conflicting training data (e.g., teaching self-knowledge of having no body while also teaching function calls like "set alarm") creates confused behavior. The constant trade-off is helpfulness versus harmlessness across diverse scenarios.

### Synthetic Training for Product Features
Teach models product behaviors by synthetically constructing training data using stronger models (like o1). For Canvas: (1) when to trigger the canvas UI vs. regular chat, (2) how to edit specific sections of a document, (3) how to make contextual comments. Measure progress through robust evals rather than vibes. This enables rapid model iteration tied to product outcomes.

### Soft Skills as Future of Work
As models get better at coding and technical execution, creative thinking, aesthetic judgment, and the ability to generate and filter ideas become the most valuable human skills. The model struggles with being truly aesthetic, deeply creative in writing, or innovatively visual -- these remain human advantages.

## Core Ideas

- The data wall is a misnomer: pre-training may plateau, but post-training via reinforcement learning on infinite tasks has no ceiling -- that is how models become broadly capable
- Evals are the new product specs: defining what success looks like for model behavior is the core skill for AI product builders
- Researchers and engineers should collaborate from day one of product development, not be siloed into separate tracks
- The AI model you are using today is the worst AI model you will ever use for the rest of your life; build on the edge of current capabilities because the next model will make your product sing
