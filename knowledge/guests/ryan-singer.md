# Ryan Singer

Creator of Shape Up and one of the first hires at 37signals. Spent 17 years building product at Basecamp alongside Jason Fried and DHH. Wrote Shape Up based on hard-won lessons about maintaining startup shipping speed as a company grows.

## Key Frameworks

### Shape Up Method
An alternative to Scrum built on: (1) Shaping -- intense collaborative sessions where product, design, and engineering figure out the idea together before committing time. (2) Appetites not estimates -- decide maximum time worth spending, then shape ideas to fit. (3) Six-week cycles with full autonomy for the building team. Never start something unless you can see the end from the beginning.

### See the End from the Beginning
Like checking if there is electricity in a wall before designing a lamp -- identify unknowns before committing. Shaping sessions produce diagrams where engineers confirm "we understand that," reducing wasted cycles from mid-build ambiguity.

## Core Ideas

- Shape Up was born from the constraint of DHH coding only 10 hours per week -- every hour had to count
- Works for companies very different from Basecamp; VC-funded, larger companies with different pressures have adopted it
- Shaping originated as short intense sessions with a Sharpie on a big pad -- not documents or Figma files
- People do not like to talk publicly about struggles shipping, which is why Shape Up spread through word of mouth
