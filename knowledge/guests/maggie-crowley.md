# Maggie Crowley

VP of Product at Toast. Previously VP and Head of Product at Charlie Health, Senior Director of Product at Drift, and PM at TripAdvisor. Harvard MBA. Former Olympic speed skater. Prolific content creator on product management craft.

## Key Frameworks

### Three Threads of Great PMs
(1) Simplifying -- finding truly the only thing you need to do and sticking with it long enough to finish. (2) Following up on results -- proactively checking metrics weeks and months after launch and sharing results without being asked. (3) Carrying the water -- doing the unglamorous work of customer support, sales calls, project management, and copy writing because you own the outcome.

### Strategy Is 5% of the Work
A great strategy will not save you if you cannot ship. The PM who has a decent strategy but ships more, gets more reps, and actually creates impact will outperform the PM with a brilliant strategy who cannot get things out the door. Strategy matters, but execution is the multiplier.

## Core Ideas

- If you find yourself saying "that's not my job," it probably is; PMs are the emotional center of the team and must keep optimism going
- Read your writing out loud; half the time you will realize it is too complicated or does not say what you meant
- Delete the first two paragraphs of every document you write; they are almost always unnecessary warmup
- Use the Minto Pyramid principle: put the full conclusion first, supporting arguments second
- Create a small Slack workspace with two to three trusted PM peers for honest feedback on your work
- It takes about five years to truly feel confident as a PM; stay at a company long enough to see two or three product cycles
