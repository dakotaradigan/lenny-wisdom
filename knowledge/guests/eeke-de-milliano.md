# Eeke de Milliano

Head of Product at Retool, previously one of Stripe's earliest PMs where she helped build foundational products including Stripe Checkout, Connect, Radar, and Chargeback Protection. Known for her thinking on how to protect innovation as companies scale and the tension between process and creative output.

## Key Frameworks

### Process as Variance Reducer
Process is by definition variance-reducing: it brings lower performers up to the average but simultaneously brings highest performers down to the average. The cost of process is often hidden because you are suppressing your most creative thinkers and highest performers. As companies grow, process becomes more necessary because you cannot hire only people who do not need it, but the tradeoff must be intentional.

### Talent Portfolio
Build a team like an investment portfolio -- diversify across risk profiles. Some team members should be reliable, consistent executors (low risk, steady returns). Others should be high-variance, creative thinkers who might fail spectacularly but occasionally produce breakthrough results. Optimizing only for consistency produces a team that cannot innovate.

## Core Ideas

- The tension between process and innovation is the central challenge of scaling product organizations. Companies introduce process when variance feels too high, but they rarely measure the cost of suppressing the top end of that variance.
- Stripe's product culture was built on extremely product-minded engineers who could act as PMs, deep user empathy through direct relationships with customers, and a willingness to invest in long-term infrastructure even when short-term pressure existed.
- Innovation does not survive without explicit protection. Leaders must create space, time, and permission for creative thinking that does not fit neatly into quarterly planning cycles or roadmap commitments.
- The best product organizations maintain a healthy tension between rigor (metrics, processes, reviews) and freedom (exploration, creative risk-taking, unstructured time). Neither extreme works alone.

## Notable Perspectives

> "Process, by definition, is variance reducing. The cost is that while you are bringing folks up to the average, you're also bringing other folks down to the average."
