# Tim Holley

VP of Product at Etsy with over a decade at the company. Led the product team through the Josh Silverman-era transformation, the COVID face mask surge, and the shift from seller-centric to buyer-experience focus. Oversaw marketplace growth strategies including retention, conversion optimization, and category expansion.

## Key Frameworks

### Five-Legged Stool
Etsy's cross-functional team model extends the classic PM-eng-design trio to five: product, engineering, design, insights (research and analytics), and marketing. Not every team has all five dedicated, but this is the aspiration. The PM does not operate as a mini-CEO but as the person who chooses the best ideas and owns the outcome when data is inconclusive.

### Habit Loop for Retention
Apply trigger-action-reward loops to marketplace retention. A buyer favorites an item (action), the seller puts it on sale or stock runs low (trigger), a push notification brings them back (reward). Close every loop. Invest in features like updates feeds that surface changes to items users have shown intent on.

### Weekly Focus Ritual
Every Monday, team members post in a shared Slack channel what they are focused on that week and reflect on whether last week's focus areas were completed. Simple but creates accountability, reveals patterns in time estimation, and keeps people anchored on priorities rather than tasks.

## Core Ideas

- A clear north star KPI (GMS for Etsy) combined with a consistent narrative repeated six-plus times is the most powerful combination for organizational alignment
- In a marketplace with 100 million unique items, the core challenge shifts from supply quantity to helping buyers choose with confidence; reviews, buyer photos, and behavioral nudges drive outsized conversion impact
- Small copy changes can have shockingly large conversion impact; adding "Etsy offsets carbon emissions from every delivery" to the cart drove measurable uplift by aligning with buyer values
- Most experiments fail (roughly 80%), so the experimentation culture must embrace learning; but A/B testing is not the only validation method, especially for retention and cohort-based analysis
- Brand integrity is a durable competitive advantage; Etsy persists alongside Amazon and eBay because the supply policies and brand promise create a distinct mental image for buyers
