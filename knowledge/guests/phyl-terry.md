# Phyl Terry

Author of Never Search Alone, the most referenced book for job seekers in the product community. CEO of Creative Good for 15+ years (clients included Apple, Facebook, Microsoft). On the founding team of the first company Amazon acquired. Has launched 2,000+ free Job Search Councils helping people find jobs through community and a product-thinking methodology.

## Key Frameworks

### Candidate Market Fit
Apply product-market fit thinking to your job search. Define what you want and what you do not want -- most people skip this step and spray-and-pray instead. Just as products need a narrow ICP, job seekers need a focused target. Your job search needs a spear, not a net.

### Job Search Councils
Groups of 6-8 job seekers who commit to supporting each other through the process. The psychological hack: putting anxious people together and asking them to be open and ask for help flips anxiety into hope, motivation, accountability, and confidence. Over 2,000 councils have been launched, completely free.

### Play to Win, Not Not to Lose
In interviews and negotiations, most people play defensively out of fear. The council gives you courage to play offensively -- asking for what you actually want, negotiating from strength, and viewing the process as mutual evaluation rather than begging for acceptance.

## Core Ideas

- Everyone -- from entry-level PMs to CEOs of public companies -- feels insecure and anxious during job searches; doing it alone magnifies that anxiety while community transforms it
- Thinking about what you do not want is as important as what you do want, yet most people never articulate their anti-requirements
- The methodology brings a product lens to job searching: hypothesis formation, testing, iteration, and customer discovery applied to your own career
- Job Search Councils are volunteer-driven with 20,000+ hours of volunteer time invested; all book revenue and more goes to running the program
