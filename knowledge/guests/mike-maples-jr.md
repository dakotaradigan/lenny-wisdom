# Mike Maples Jr

Legendary early-stage investor and founding partner at Floodgate, one of the earliest pioneers of seed-stage investing. Made early bets on Twitter, Lyft, Twitch, Okta, and Applied Intuition. Eight-time Forbes Midas List member. Author of Pattern Breakers, distilling decades of studying what separates breakthrough startups from failures.

## Key Frameworks

### Inflections, Insights, Founder Future Fit
Breakthrough startups require three elements: (1) Inflections -- external events that create potential for radical change (technological, regulatory, or belief shifts), (2) Insights -- non-obvious truths about how inflections can be harnessed to change behavior, and (3) Founder Future Fit -- the founder being uniquely suited to build this particular future.

### Stress Testing Inflections
For any proposed inflection, ask three questions: What is the specific new thing? What specific form of empowerment does it offer, and to whom? Under what conditions might that empowerment be realized or blocked? If the empowerment conditions are unlikely to be met, the inflection will not translate into a startup opportunity.

### Backcasting vs Forecasting
Great founders backcast -- they assume the future must be radically different, envision that future, and work backwards. Corporate capitalists forecast by projecting forward from the present. Startups win by forcing a choice rather than a comparison, denying the premise of existing rules entirely.

## Core Ideas

- 80% of Floodgate's biggest returning investments came from pivots, suggesting that the right insight matters more than the right first implementation
- Startups never beat big companies by executing better; they win by proposing a radically different future that disorients incumbents
- The best startup ideas are actively disliked or dismissed by most people but trigger an "I've been waiting for this" response from a passionate subset
- Inflections are always around us but go unrecognized; the wheel was mounted horizontally for 500 years before someone mounted it vertically and changed transportation
