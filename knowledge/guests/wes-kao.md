## Key Frameworks

### The Super-Specific Who
When communicating, do not address a generic audience. Identify exactly who you are talking to and tailor your message to their specific context, concerns, and language. Specificity creates resonance; generality creates noise.

### State Change Method
Every meeting should create a state change -- participants should leave in a measurably different state than when they entered. Define the desired state change before the meeting. If you cannot articulate what changes as a result of the meeting, the meeting should not happen.

### Managing Up as Career Accelerant
Most people resent the idea of managing their boss, viewing it as the manager's job. But embracing managing up builds trust, unlocks more opportunities, and makes your manager appreciate you more. It is one of the highest-leverage career investments available to anyone at any level.

## Core Ideas

- Your boss has to manage many people; you only have to manage one boss -- the asymmetry means you can be much more thoughtful about the relationship
- Writing well is a force multiplier; every written communication is an opportunity to demonstrate clarity of thought, not just convey information
- Saying no effectively requires offering alternatives and framing the refusal in terms of the other person's priorities, not your own constraints
- Cohort-based courses are the future of professional education because they combine the accountability of peers with the expertise of instructors in a time-bound format
- The janky tech stack problem in education (toggling between six tools) was the original insight behind Maven -- when infrastructure is too hard, great teachers cannot teach
