# Manik Gupta

Corporate VP at Microsoft leading consumer efforts. Previously CPO at Uber for four years and Director of Product for Google Maps in India. Angel investor. Built Google Maps India from zero to the second-largest Maps market globally and helped Uber achieve sub-five-minute average ETAs across 75+ countries.

## Key Frameworks

### Company-Product Fit
Before seeking product-market fit, ask whether the product fits the company's portfolio and unique strengths. If a successful product would not serve the right place in the company's portfolio, do not build it. This question, asked with conviction, removes distractions and ensures organizational sponsorship for the journey to PMF.

### The Consumer Stack
Five capabilities needed for consumer product success: (1) Design-led thinking to delight users with pixel-perfect craft. (2) Strong focus and prioritization -- few features done exceptionally well. (3) Right metrics and instrumentation with codified definitions. (4) Growth and distribution strategy. (5) Strong culture of consumer empathy.

## Core Ideas

- Surround yourself with A-plus people and play the long game with them across multiple companies and decades
- Technology optimism as a career compass: choose projects where technology solves real human needs at scale
- Building consumer products is harder than people think because users must choose you; go-to-market cannot be forced
- Global consumer product patterns are more universal than people assume; do not over-index on market-specific solutions
- Give teams quick wins to sustain motivation during challenging periods; winning drives energy, which drives more winning
- Pessimism is as infectious as optimism; leaders must be genuinely motivated, not fake it
