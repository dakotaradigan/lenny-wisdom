# Jeff Weinstein

Product lead at Stripe for 6+ years. Led payment infrastructure scaling to hundreds of billions in volume, multiple zero-to-one bets, and Stripe Atlas (one in six new Delaware corporations). Known for his intense customer engagement on social media and systematic approach to craft.

## Key Frameworks

### Go, Go, Go ASAP + Long-Term Compounding
Pair urgency with patience. Inject energy to ship today, make deadlines tomorrow. But also ask: what will we never regret investing in? At Stripe, payment method expansion was flat for years despite intense effort. They slowed down, built internal platforms, sent people around the world -- and then expansion went from 10 to 100+ payment methods nonlinearly.

### Study Groups for Empathy
Four to eight people pretend to be some company with a specific outcome problem. Rule 1: you do not work at Stripe. Rule 2: you're not here to solve problems. This is about practicing empathy -- understanding what the customer faces before jumping to solutions. It builds shared context across the team.

### Metrics From the Customer's Perspective
Ask: what is the value we're trying to produce for the customer, and can we measure it from their perspective? Product-market fit signals are charts going up and to the right on one hand, and unsolicited tweets on the other. If your service goes down for 20 minutes and nobody's furious, you don't have PMF.

## Core Ideas

- Craft is a dessert you earn after the meal of solving a real problem. No amount of beauty or delight can save a product that doesn't solve a burning enough problem. The signal for PMF is that customers pause their entire day because your product went down.
- Speed of response to customer feedback is P-zero priority. Leave a meeting to send one message back. Even "Got this, can I follow up tomorrow?" creates a secret portal between a big brand and a curious human.
- Turn detractors into promoters. When Stripe Atlas had a bug (dropping a percent sign from legal docs), the founder who caught it became a long-term collaborator and document reviewer.
- Bug-finder programs, invented on a whim via a single tweet, generated 65 customer videos and dozens of discovered issues. Give people permission through a program, even if deeply arbitrary.
