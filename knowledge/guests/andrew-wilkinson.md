# Andrew Wilkinson

Andrew Wilkinson is co-founder and CEO of Tiny, a holding company often called the "Berkshire Hathaway of the internet" that owns 40+ businesses including Dribbble and AeroPress. He bootstrapped from zero to hundreds of millions in value and has been involved with 75+ businesses across his career.

## Key Frameworks

### Fish Where the Fish Are
Find business niches with lots of demand and little competition rather than crowded markets. Boring businesses (funeral homes, pest control, government form software) often have better economics than exciting ones (restaurants, cafes, project management tools).

### Gym Analogy for Entrepreneurs
Don't try to deadlift 300 pounds on day one. First-time entrepreneurs should start with simple, proven business models to build their "muscle" and narrative ("I'm good at business"), not jump straight to building the next great AI company.

### Lazy Leadership
Get away from tasks you hate as quickly as possible. Scale past being a solo operator so you can focus on your superpower (often sales or taste) while others handle operations.

## Core Ideas

- Competition equals lower margin; the more competitors, the lower your prices and the harder the business
- You can't take a brilliant management team and change a bad business model - the business model wins
- Follow your passion but find the profitable niche within it (loved movies -> bought Letterboxd, not funded films)
- Lifestyle/bootstrap businesses can get huge ($300M+ revenue) without VC; the only difference is tolerance for burning money
- Use AI (Claude, ChatGPT) to evaluate business ideas before starting - there's no excuse for starting incredibly stupid businesses anymore
