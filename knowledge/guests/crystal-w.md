## Key Frameworks

### Growth Physics Model
Start with the constraints of your market, product, model, and channels -- the "physics" of your universe. Identify underutilized levers within existing physics rather than trying to change multiple variables at once. At Gojek, they discovered that drivers were an underutilized acquisition channel and created an incentive for drivers to sell GoPay to passengers, which drove 60% of GoPay acquisition.

### Retention Benchmarks
For free products with weekly frequency, week-one retention should be at least 60% and then flatten. For paid products, 20-30%. If friends and family cannot retain at 80%, the product will not work for anyone else. Early adopters set the ceiling, not the floor, for retention.

## Core Ideas

- Run experiments even with only 30 users. The underlying trends will be the same as at larger scale; only the precision improves. Zero data points is always worse than 30.
- Do things that do not scale. Gojek rented a stadium, pre-loaded phones, and hired 60,000 drivers in two weeks. They tested subscription features by adding drivers to WhatsApp groups and manually applying vouchers in the backend -- pure Wizard of Oz.
- Super apps thrive in Southeast Asia partly because phone storage is scarce. Users delete apps to make room for photos, so an app that does many things well survives where single-purpose apps get deleted.
- If users land on your app and do nothing, it is a copy problem, not a product problem. Resonate with the pain point rather than describing the solution so users understand how to fit themselves into the use case.
