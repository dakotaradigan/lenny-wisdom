# Julia Schottenstein

Product leader at dbt Labs leading dbt Cloud. Former VC at NEA investing in dev tools and data companies. Transitioned from investing to product after becoming so convicted about dbt's potential that she offered to invest 20% of her net worth, lost the deal to Sequoia, and joined the company instead. Led dbt Labs' acquisition of Transform.

## Key Frameworks

### M&A: Inflict Pain, Stay Friendly
For any startup, only two to three buyers will find what you build truly strategic. Figure out your competitive advantage area and inflict pain on those potential acquirers -- make it impossible for them to ignore you. But maintain a friendly, partner-oriented stance so the door stays open when M&A timing is right.

### Four Dimensions of Early-Stage Evaluation
People (does the CEO have range from vision to detail?), Market (is it growing with space for a new entrant?), Product (do users talk about it with evangelical passion?), Distribution (open source flywheel, enterprise sales muscle, or bottoms-up adoption?). You will not get 10/10 on all four -- pick where you can personally de-risk.

### Competition Philosophy
Three pillars: (1) Hold true to your vision and avoid distraction from competitor noise. (2) Grow the pie with partners rather than slicing it thinly. (3) Lean into your strengths and leave space for ecosystem partners to complement you.

## Core Ideas

- The best time to think about M&A strategy is when you do not need one -- having a viable independent path gives you maximum leverage in every acquisition conversation
- dbt succeeded through power-in-simplicity (if you know SQL, you can contribute to production data) combined with commitment to being open source, which drove a flywheel of adoption, use case diversity, and ecosystem partner investment
- Starting as a consulting firm (Fishtown Analytics) for two years gave dbt founders deep firsthand understanding of customer pain before writing a line of product code
- The spark of product-market fit is when users cannot stop talking about the product and want to share it -- that evangelism does the distribution work for you
