# Bob Moesta

Co-creator of the Jobs to Be Done (JTBD) framework alongside Clay Christensen. Has worked on 3,500+ products across many industries. Eight-time founder, CEO of The Rewired Group, and adjunct lecturer at Kellogg School at Northwestern.

## Key Frameworks

### Four Forces of Progress
People switch products when Push (context dissatisfaction) + Pull (desired outcome) > Anxiety of the new + Habit of the present. More features create anxiety, not just pull. Reducing friction (F3/F4) can increase sales without changing the product at all.

### Context and Outcome, Not Pain and Gain
JTBD is not about pain points. It's about the context that makes someone value a solution. Snickers competes with protein drinks (missed meal, need energy), while Milky Way competes with wine (emotional recovery). Context reveals the true competitive set.

### Six Phases of Buying
First thought, passive looking, active looking, deciding, first use, ongoing use. Most companies design sales for how they want to sell, not how customers want to buy. Meeting customers where they are in their buying timeline can halve the sales cycle and 4x conversion.

## Core Ideas

- People hire products to make progress in their lives; understanding the struggling moment is the seed for real innovation
- Supply and demand are not as connected as people think; a struggling moment causes demand, not a product
- Interview people who recently purchased to understand causation; what people say they want differs from what they actually choose
- Most products are hired for 3-5 different jobs that are often in conflict with each other; Intercom discovered four distinct jobs and repositioned accordingly
- A "kick-ass half is better than a half-ass whole"; QuickBooks has half the features and double the price of competitors
