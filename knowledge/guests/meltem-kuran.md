# Meltem Kuran

Head of Growth at Deel, arguably the fastest-growing SaaS business of all time ($0 to $300M ARR in three years while staying EBITDA positive). Employee number 19-20 at Deel and second hire on the marketing team. Specializes in cheap growth channels, which was the original reason Deel hired her.

## Key Frameworks

### Build the Skeleton Before the Makeup
Before investing in paid acquisition, answer these foundational questions in order: (1) Do you have a website? (2) Is it fast? (3) Does Google know it exists? (4) Can people find it through organic search? (5) Do you need content to improve discoverability? Only after all of these are solid should you consider paid ads. You cannot run successful paid ads on a site that loads in four-plus seconds.

### Community Piggybacking
The cheapest growth channels are places where potential customers already ask questions: Reddit, Quora, partnerships, and existing communities. Set up keyword alerts for relevant subreddits and respond with genuine answers. One answer lives online for others to find repeatedly. The key: add value to the community rather than selling; be someone people actually want to talk to.

## Core Ideas

- People do not want to be sold to; they want their problems solved -- meet them where they are asking questions
- Reddit was one of Deel's first acquisition channels; track relevant subreddits, respond genuinely to questions, and recognize the upper limit of audience size in each community
- Do not go blindly into SEO; if people are not searching for your topic on Google, content will not get found regardless of quality
- Each cheap channel has a ceiling; understand the upper limit of the audience and stack multiple small channels together
- The early Deel growth story is fundamentally about being where the conversation already was rather than trying to create new conversations
