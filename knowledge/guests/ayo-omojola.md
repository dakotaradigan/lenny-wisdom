# Ayo Omojola

Ayo Omojola co-created and scaled Square's Cash Card alongside Cash App. He's currently CPO at Carbon Health, one of the biggest health tech companies. He's a former founder, board member at Pinwheel, and angel investor in Mercury Bank, Faire, Modern Treasury, and dozens of other startups.

## Key Frameworks

### Different AND Better
Being different is easy (just build something else). Being better is easy (just improve and charge more). The key is being different from what exists in a way that matters to the end user. Cash App's test: "Try and send me a dollar you can use right now" - only one app could do it.

### Building a Startup Within a Startup
The Cash Card was built as an internal startup within Square. Success required protecting the team's autonomy while leveraging the parent company's infrastructure, balancing independence with organizational alignment.

## Core Ideas

- Product differentiation must be both different AND better in ways the end user actually cares about, not just technically superior
- SEO and content infrastructure built for a side project (Mailform) taught foundational growth principles that transferred to larger products
- Answering 100+ Quora questions about mailing packages was an early growth tactic that worked because Quora was a dominant platform at the time
- Meet users where they already look for solutions, even if that means unglamorous work like forum answers and how-to content
- Both fintech and health tech require navigating heavy regulation while maintaining consumer product excellence

## Notable Perspectives

> "Being different is not enough... Being better is not enough... It has to be better than what exists today in a way that matters to the end user."

> "When someone says, 'Hey, why are you betting on Venmo?' I'd be like, 'Try and send me a dollar that I can use now,' and there was only one app you could do it with."
