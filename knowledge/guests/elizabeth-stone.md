# Elizabeth Stone

CTO of Netflix and likely the first economist to be named CTO at a Fortune 500 company. Previously VP of Data and Insights at Netflix, VP of Science at Lyft, COO at Nuna, and a trader at Merrill Lynch. Known for her rapid career ascent across multiple companies and her deep understanding of Netflix's unique culture.

## Key Frameworks

### High Talent Density as Foundation
Netflix's culture of candor, learning, excellence, and freedom with responsibility all depend on starting with high talent density. This reflects Reed Hastings' founding belief that a company with exceptional people operating differently would produce better outcomes and more fulfilling work. Maintaining this requires holding yourself to standards that do not feel like natural human behavior.

### Data and Research Organization Design
Netflix's approach to structuring data and user research teams is considered part of their secret to success. The organization of these functions -- how they sit relative to product and engineering teams -- materially affects the quality of decisions made across the company.

## Core Ideas

- An economics background provides unique advantages in tech leadership: comfort with uncertainty, systems thinking, understanding incentive structures, and the ability to reason about complex tradeoffs. More economists will rise in tech company ranks.
- The biggest change when moving from VP to CTO was the amount of context switching and the degree to which she felt behind. Engineering spans far more problem spaces and technical domains than data, requiring constant learning.
- Excellence at Netflix means people derive fulfillment from the quality of outcomes and talent around them, not from perks or easy work. This creates a self-reinforcing cycle where great people attract great people.
- Freedom and responsibility at Netflix is not a euphemism for chaos -- it is a structured approach where high-talent individuals are given significant autonomy precisely because the hiring bar ensures they will use it well.

## Notable Perspectives

> "We can't really have any of the other aspects of the culture, including candor, learning, seeking excellence and improvement, freedom and responsibility, if you don't start with high talent density."
