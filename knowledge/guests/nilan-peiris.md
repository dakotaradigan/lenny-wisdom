# Nilan Peiris

Chief Product Officer at Wise, which moves $12B per month and acquires 70% of new users through word of mouth. Has built one of the most systematic approaches to driving organic growth through product excellence, proving that making a product dramatically better than alternatives is the highest-leverage growth strategy.

## Key Frameworks

### NPS-to-Virality Exponential
Each NPS band roughly doubles the number of people a user tells about your product. Moving users from 6 to 7-8 doubles referrals; 8 to 9 doubles again; 9 to 10 doubles again. The ROI of moving NPS up compounds through increasing the viral coefficient of your entire user base, dwarfing the one-time impact of conversion rate optimization.

### Product Pillars for Recommendation
Identify the 2-3 product attributes that drive recommendation (for Wise: price, speed, ease of use). These emerge from NPS comments and customer conversations. Optimize relentlessly against these pillars rather than spreading effort across everything.

### 8-10x Better Threshold
Users will use a product that is marginally better, but they will only recommend one that is 8-10x better. At Wise, entering a market 5.9% cheaper than 6% alternatives generated usage but not advocacy. Advocacy only started when they were 8-10x cheaper.

## Core Ideas

- Trust is best solved indirectly -- rather than building trust through badges and certifications, deliver such a good experience that people recommend you to friends, and friend recommendations bypass trust barriers entirely
- Word of mouth requires low distribution cost and massive scalability, making it ideal for commodity businesses like money transfer where margins are thin
- The bar for recommendation is much higher than the bar for usage: most teams stop when the product works; to get NPS 9-10 you must give users an experience they did not know was previously possible
- Building NPS measurement into the product flow rather than as a separate survey produces more reliable attribution data
