# Ryan Hoover

Founder of Product Hunt, former PM, and now full-time investor at Weekend Fund. Has observed tens of thousands of product launches and offers unique insights on what makes launches successful. Known for his authentic, accessible approach to the startup world and his willingness to share vulnerably about the founder experience.

## Key Frameworks

### Launches as Multi-Purpose Events
Launches are not just about customer acquisition. They serve recruiting, fundraising, partnerships, feedback, SEO, and team morale. Understanding which goal matters most should dictate launch strategy. The overlooked benefit: team morale from celebrating what has been built and sharing it with the world.

### Startup as Experiment
Frame early work as an experiment, not a startup. Product Hunt itself started as a newsletter and was not incorporated for four to five months. The experimental framing reduces pressure, encourages learning, and avoids premature commitment to venture scale. The litmus test for raising: can you see yourself working on this for a decade?

## Core Ideas

- Speak like a human in launches, not like a PR person; describe your product the way your users describe it to friends
- The gallery in a Product Hunt launch is the first thing people see; use it like a slideshow telling a story
- Product Hunt itself was not intended to be a startup -- it was a side project that gained traction
- Many founders wear a mask of confidence while dealing with intense anxiety; vulnerability in DMs reveals struggles that public discourse rarely shows

## Notable Perspectives

> "That flutter in your stomach that you wake up with in the morning of just anxiety and stress -- being a CEO makes it slightly harder because you still have to put on this mask of confidence."
