# Seth Godin

Author of 21 books including 18 international bestsellers. Blogging daily for nearly 10,000 consecutive days. Founded Yoyodyne, Squidoo, and the altMBA. Started his career as a product manager at Spinnaker Software. One of the most influential thinkers on marketing, brand, and what makes products spread.

## Key Frameworks

### Brand as Promise
A brand is not a logo -- it is a promise. Would you miss it if it were gone? Nike is a brand (you know what a Nike hotel would be like); Hyatt has a logo (you have no idea what Hyatt sneakers would be). AI will stop being a feature like electricity stopped being a feature. What matters is the promise you make and whether you keep it.

### Taste as Knowing What Others Want
Good taste is knowing what other people want just before they do. If only you want peanut butter-covered licorice, you do not have good taste. Quality means meeting spec -- not perfection, not luxury. If you meet spec, ship it. If the spec is not good enough, raise the spec. Perfectionism is hiding.

### Tension as Strategy
Every great innovation creates tension: you make a promise about what life could be like, and the customer imagines that possibility. If they fall in love with it, there is tension -- did you tell the truth? Will it work? Tension is at the heart of every art form and every product launch.

## Core Ideas

- If you do not build network effects into your product, you will almost certainly fail -- there is no substitute for users having a reason to tell others
- Empathy is not about kindness, it is a requirement; if you are angry at users for not reading the manual, you made the mistake, not them
- Seth used Claude as a writing editor for his latest book -- uploading chapters to ask "what claims am I not sustaining?" and "what did I miss?" -- finding it a more patient editor than most humans
- The professional does not ask for extensions; things you did not expect will happen, and that is part of the deal
