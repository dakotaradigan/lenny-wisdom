# Dhanji R. Prasanna

CTO of Block (formerly Square), overseeing 3,500+ people. Wrote an internal AI manifesto that catalyzed Block's transformation into one of the most AI-native large companies in the world. Previously worked at Google on Wave and other products. Led Block's reorganization from a GM structure to a functional org to enable deep technology investment.

## Key Frameworks

### Functional Org for AI Transformation
Block shifted from a portfolio of independent business units (GM structure) to a unified functional organization where all engineers report into one team. This eliminated siloed engineering practices and enabled company-wide AI adoption. Conway's Law is powerful: you ship your org structure, so reorganize first to enable the technical transformation.

### AI Productivity Measurement
Measure manual hours saved through a combination of self-reported data and check metrics (PR throughput, feature velocity). Block's AI-forward engineering teams report 8-10 hours saved per week, with the company trending toward 20-25% of manual hours saved overall. Critically, this is the baseline -- it will only improve.

## Core Ideas

- The biggest productivity gains from AI are not in engineering but in non-technical teams using agents to build their own software tools. Enterprise risk management built an entire system in hours that would have taken weeks waiting for an internal apps team.
- Block built Goose, an open-source AI agent, and Gosling (Goose for mobile) that automates UI testing using Android's accessibility API. Engineers come in the morning to find PRs opened by Goose based on Slack conversations it overheard about desired features.
- A boring but transformative change: shifting from GM structure to functional org. It was painful and not everyone was on board, but it enabled shared tools, shared policies, consistent engineering levels, and internal mobility -- prerequisites for AI adoption.
- Code quality and product success have nothing to do with each other. Many engineers overvalue clean code at the expense of shipping products that solve real user problems.
