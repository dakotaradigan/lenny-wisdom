## Key Frameworks

### Two Modes of Prompt Engineering
Conversational mode: iterating with a chatbot in real time ("make it more formal"). Product mode: crafting a single perfect prompt that runs millions of times per day -- this is where classical prompt engineering research lives and where the money is.

### Self-Criticism Technique
Ask the LLM to check its own response, criticize it, then improve it. This iterative self-reflection dramatically boosts output quality. Studies show bad prompts can drop performance to 0% while good prompts boost it to 90% on the same task.

## Core Ideas

- Few-shot prompting (giving examples of desired output) is the single highest-impact technique; showing the AI 2-3 examples of your email style is far more effective than describing your style in words
- Prompt injection is fundamentally unsolvable -- unlike classical security, you cannot fully prevent AI from being manipulated by adversarial inputs, which has major implications for AI agents managing finances or physical systems
- Trial and error teaches more than any course or resource; the best way to improve prompting skills is constant interaction with chatbots
- The concept of "artificial social intelligence" -- understanding how to communicate with AIs -- is becoming as important as interpersonal social intelligence

## Notable Perspectives
