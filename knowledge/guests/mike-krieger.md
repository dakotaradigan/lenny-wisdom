# Mike Krieger

Chief Product Officer at Anthropic and co-founder of Instagram. One of the most respected product builders in tech, now leading product at one of the most important AI companies. Brings deep experience shipping consumer products at massive scale to the frontier of AI product development.

## Key Frameworks

### Bottleneck Migration
When AI writes 90% of your code, the bottleneck shifts from engineering capacity to decision-making and alignment upstream (what to build) and merge/deploy infrastructure downstream. Product leaders must provide "minimum viable strategy" so people feel empowered to run and build at the speed AI enables.

### Product People on Research Teams
The highest-leverage product work at an AI company happens at the intersection of product and model research, not wrapping UX around off-the-shelf models. Embedding product people in post-training conversations -- where models learn specific skills -- yields differentiated features that cannot be replicated by anyone just prompting.

### Three Roles for Product in the AI Era
Product teams remain essential for: (1) strategy -- deciding where to play and how to win, (2) comprehensibility -- making powerful AI tools understandable and accessible, and (3) opening eyes -- demonstrating to people what is now possible that they did not realize.

## Core Ideas

- The biggest mind-shift since joining Anthropic is that AI models can now offer genuinely novel strategic perspectives, not just anodyne suggestions -- Opus 4 was the first model that changed how he thinks about product strategy
- AI timelines should be taken more seriously; predictions that seemed outlandish are consistently coming true on schedule
- Nurturing curiosity and the process of scientific inquiry in children matters more than any specific skill because the jobs that will exist when they grow up are unknowable
