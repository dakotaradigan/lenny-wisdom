# Camille Fournier

Author of *The Manager's Path*, considered the definitive guide for engineering management careers. Former CTO of Rent the Runway, VP of Technology at Goldman Sachs, and head of platform engineering at Two Sigma.

## Key Frameworks

### Four Things That Annoy Engineers About PMs
1) Hoarding credit instead of sharing it. 2) Acting like technical details don't matter. 3) Playing telephone instead of connecting people directly. 4) Hoarding all the ideas, which causes engineers to over-engineer as a creative outlet.

### Engineers Need Creative Ownership
When PMs exclude engineers from ideation, engineers redirect their creative energy into technology choices: new frameworks, rewrites, over-engineering. The best PMs aren't threatened by engineers having ideas.

## Core Ideas

- Great PMs share credit, show genuine curiosity about technical details, connect engineers directly to stakeholders, and invite engineers into the creative process
- Engineers want to understand the business and customer problem; don't underestimate their desire to engage beyond just writing code
- Platform engineering requires treating internal teams as customers with product thinking, discovery, and measurement
- Major rewrites are often traps driven by creative frustration rather than genuine technical need
- Fewer one-on-ones can sometimes be more effective; the right cadence depends on the team's maturity

## Notable Perspectives

> "When PMs just don't understand the details and act like they don't matter, it just shows a real lack of empathy for the work that engineers are doing."

> "The product managers that have done the best, they're not threatened by other people having ideas."
