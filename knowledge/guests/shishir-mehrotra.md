# Shishir Mehrotra

Co-founder and CEO of Coda. Previously VP of Product and Engineering at YouTube (6 years) and spent 6 years at Microsoft. Sits on the board of Spotify. A deeply first-principles thinker known for prolifically publishing frameworks as Coda docs, including his widely-read pieces on Eigenquestions and bundling theory.

## Key Frameworks

### Black Loops and Blue Loops
Two growth mechanisms: the Black Loop is viral spread (someone makes a doc, shares it, recipients make their own docs, repeat). The Blue Loop is content-driven growth (published docs attract new users who discover Coda through the gallery). Understanding which loop drives your business changes where you invest.

### Eigenquestions
The questions whose answers determine the answers to all subsequent questions -- like eigenvectors in linear algebra. Finding the right eigenquestion for a decision eliminates entire categories of debate. Instead of arguing about dozens of features, find the one framing question that, once answered, makes everything else obvious.

### Four Mitzvot of Bundling
A framework for understanding when and how subscription bundling works, developed from napkin sketches at YouTube and shared with Spotify's Daniel Ek (which led to Shishir joining Spotify's board). Explains why some bundles create enormous value and others destroy it.

## Core Ideas

- Reference checks are the best hiring signal -- far better than 30 minutes of artificial interview scenarios; people who worked with the candidate for years know things you cannot discover in an interview
- Product-market fit is not binary -- you have PMF with current users but never with the next cohort you want to reach, requiring constant reinvention of the first mile
- Encourage employees to publish content externally using your product; at YouTube the team made videos, at Coda the team publishes docs -- it builds empathy and creates organic growth
- Niches are much bigger than people think, both on YouTube and in Coda's doc gallery
