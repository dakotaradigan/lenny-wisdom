# Annie Pearl

Annie Pearl is Chief Product Officer at Calendly. Previously, she was CPO at Glassdoor and Director of Product at Box. She transitioned into product management from a law career and is frequently asked for advice on making similar transitions.

## Key Frameworks

### Product Strategy as Integrated Choices
Strategy is an integrated set of choices outlining how you'll win: What's your winning aspiration? Where will you play (markets, segments, personas)? How will you win with that target audience? Each choice constrains and enables the others.

### Four Paths Into Product Management
(1) Formal APM programs at companies of any size; (2) Apply to junior PM roles via internal job boards from product-adjacent roles; (3) Shadow and partner with PMs, volunteering for product work (become a subject matter expert); (4) Join an early-stage startup where everyone does everything.

## Core Ideas

- Calendly's PLG motion created an inherently viral product - every time someone sends a link, they create awareness and trial
- Building a sales team on top of PLG requires careful sequencing to avoid cannibalizing organic growth
- To share a Calendly link without it feeling like a power move: offer the recipient their choice first, and use "add times to email" to reduce friction
- Product management is accessible from non-technical backgrounds; the key is demonstrating product thinking through adjacent work

## Notable Perspectives

> "Strategy is really just an integrated set of choices that outline how you're going to win in whatever marketplace you choose."

> "Maybe you're in customer support, implementation, or maybe you're a sales engineer. You can look at the internal job board and find junior PM roles."
