# Georgiana Laudi (Gia)

Co-founder of Forget The Funnel, a consultancy that works hands-on with B2B SaaS companies to unlock growth through customer-led strategy. Former head of marketing at Unbounce for five years. Partners with Claire Suellentrop (former Calendly marketing lead), combining customer research with strategic marketing to drive growth.

## Key Frameworks

### Customer-Led Growth (Anti-Funnel)
Traditional funnels and pirate metrics (AARRR) are flawed because they put the business at the center instead of the customer. MQLs and SQLs are meaningless abstractions. Funnels end at acquisition, ignoring retention and expansion -- which is fatal for recurring revenue businesses. They also delete the problem stage (the customer's world before discovering you), losing critical marketing context.

### Customer Journey Over Funnel Stages
Map the customer journey from the problem they are living with (before they know you exist) through discovery, evaluation, first value, and ongoing expansion. This captures the full lifecycle that recurring revenue demands. Each stage has different customer needs and different marketing/product interventions.

## Core Ideas

- For recurring revenue businesses, thinking about growth as ending at acquisition is a death sentence; post-acquisition retention, expansion, and advocacy are where the business actually lives
- Nobody knows what MQLs and SQLs really mean, and they assume all customers and all products are the same
- The problem stage (the customer's world before your product) is where the most valuable marketing context lives; deleting it from your model is a critical error
- Customer research is the foundation of growth strategy, not an afterthought; without understanding why customers switch from their status quo, you cannot position effectively
- The feeling of "pushing people through a funnel" is gross because it is -- replace it with understanding and serving the customer's journey
