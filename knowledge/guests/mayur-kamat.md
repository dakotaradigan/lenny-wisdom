CPO at N26, one of the top five companies for hiring and producing great product managers. Previously Global Head of Product at Binance (during its rise from under $1M ARR to $400B peak valuation), VP of Product at Agoda ($100B+ company), and PM at Google and Microsoft. First PM on Google Hangouts.

## Key Frameworks

### Daily Meeting Product Management
For the most critical product problems, assign an owner who runs a daily meeting with all relevant people until the problem is solved. At Binance, the leadership team met every day (11 PM Singapore time, including weekends). No decision was blocked for more than 24 hours. This extreme cadence enabled 500-cell KYC conversion spreadsheets across 50 countries to be optimized cell by cell.

### Career Compounding Rate
The fastest career growth comes from joining fast-growing companies where learning compounds daily rather than yearly. Microsoft shipped products during Mayur's internship that had not launched by the time he left years later -- extremely low compounding. At Binance, something shipped every hour. Optimize for compounding rate, not compensation, in your first 15 years.

## Core Ideas

- Strategy is overrated for product; for most PMs, strategy should be "how fast can I go from hypothesis to data?"
- Know your superpowers and find roles where success depends on using them; do not optimize for weaknesses early in your career
- Decide early whether you truly want to be a C-level executive, because the path requires different career choices and sacrifices
- The most spectacular failure teaches that even unlimited resources (Google, Larry Page's support, thousands of engineers) cannot overcome a product direction that does not resonate
- Experimentation makes product management scientific: it resolves the challenge that everyone thinks they can do the PM's job
- When you put 500 people on a problem with no constraints except time, mountains move -- but the real skill is maneuvering them quickly
