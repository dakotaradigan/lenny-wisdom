## Key Frameworks

### PM as Potential-to-Kinetic Energy Converter
Product management converts the potential energy of a team (engineers, designers, salespeople) into realized value for customers with minimum loss. Early-stage, the builders themselves should do this. The PM role only becomes necessary when the conversion needs a dedicated coordinator -- which is why starting in another function first builds the foundation for great PM work.

### Decision Log
Product sense is just making good decisions with insufficient data. PMs need reps: document every decision, the rationale, and later the outcome. Over time, this builds pattern recognition that compounds into intuition. Without the log, you forget your reasoning and cannot learn from results.

### The Unsell Email
At offer stage, send candidates an email listing all the terrible things about the role and company -- every fear they might have, confirmed. If they read the full email and remain equally excited, you have found an A+ hire. This filters for genuine conviction and prevents costly mis-hires who leave within months.

## Core Ideas

- If you cannot sell or support your own product, you should not be trusted to build it -- PMs should regularly do sales calls and support tickets to stay grounded in customer reality
- Do not go straight into product management; start in engineering, design, sales, or support to build the domain expertise and empathy that makes PM work meaningful
- Automate user insight collection using AI to continuously process support tickets, sales calls, and feedback into themes -- this replaces periodic research sprints with always-on customer intelligence
- The best PM-engineer relationships come from PMs who protect engineering time, take accountability for ambiguity, and genuinely care about the craft of the people building the product
