# Zevi Arnovitz

Product Manager at Meta, previously PM at Wix. Has zero technical background (studied music in high school) but has become one of the most hands-on AI-building PMs in the industry. Engineers at Meta ask him to teach them his workflow. Builds real products entirely with AI coding tools like Cursor and Claude Code, demonstrating that non-technical PMs can ship software independently.

## Key Frameworks

### AI-Powered PM Workflow
A complete end-to-end process: use AI to explore and structure ideas in Linear, develop plans with AI assistance, build the product in Cursor, then have multiple LLMs review the code (use /review in Claude, plus have Codex and Cursor cross-review each other). Document everything and use the process as a learning opportunity to develop technical intuition.

### Cross-LLM Code Review
Since non-technical PMs cannot easily catch code mistakes, use multiple AI models to review each other's output. Have Claude review its own code via /review, then open Codex alongside Cursor and have each review the other's work. This creates a checks-and-balances system that compensates for lack of technical expertise.

### Building as Learning
Do not just use AI tools to ship -- use the process to develop your own understanding of how things work. Each project builds technical intuition that makes the next project better. The goal is not to replace engineers but to become a force multiplier who can prototype, validate ideas, and communicate more effectively with engineering teams.

## Core Ideas

- It is the best time to be a junior professional, contrary to doom narratives about AI eliminating junior roles; when else in history could you get out of school and build a startup on your own?
- The real challenge is not generating code but reviewing it; invest heavily in review workflows to maintain quality
- Titles and responsibilities will collapse as AI enables anyone to build; the people who embrace this earliest will have the greatest advantage
- You will not be replaced by AI -- you will be replaced by someone who is better at using AI than you
- Non-technical PMs who can build prototypes and validate ideas independently are dramatically more effective than those who rely entirely on engineering resources
