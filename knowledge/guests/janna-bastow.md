# Janna Bastow

Co-founder of Mind the Product (the world's largest PM community, 300K+ members) and CEO/co-founder of ProdPad. Former product manager who invented the Now/Next/Later roadmapping framework. Experienced conference organizer and public speaker.

## Key Frameworks

### Now / Next / Later Roadmapping
Replace timeline-based roadmaps with three buckets: Now (current sprint work), Next (upcoming priorities), Later (future exploration). The roadmap is not a plan -- it's a prototype for your strategy. Like any prototype, its value is in the prototyping process of sharing assumptions, getting feedback, and iterating. The value is not in the artifact.

### Roadmap as Strategy Prototype
At the feature level, you prototype with mockups. At the strategy level, you prototype with your roadmap. Lay out your assumptions about which problems to solve in what order, share them broadly with team and customers, check that you're on the right path, and iterate. Throw away the old version without attachment.

## Core Ideas

- The whole point of a roadmap is that it's not designed to be your plan. It's a prototype for checking strategic assumptions with stakeholders, customers, and anyone who will listen.
- Community building requires consistency above all else. Mind the Product grew by showing up every month with meetups and every year with conferences -- grassroots, collaborative, always present.
- Conferences are one of the most expensive and unlean things you can do. Mistakes happen in thousands of dollars, not hundreds. Speaker coaches, power poses, and finding friendly faces in the audience are key speaking tactics.
- Start with story narratives before building slides. The presentation should follow the narrative, not the other way around. Recording yourself and listening back, though painful, dramatically improves delivery.
