# Andrew "Boz" Bosworth

CTO of Meta. Joined Facebook in 2006 as one of the first engineers (~10th). Created the Facebook News Feed, built the original mobile ads platform, helped build messaging, groups, and the engineering boot camp. Now leads AR/VR and consumer hardware including Quest and Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses.

## Key Frameworks

### Leverage Your Leaders
The most common advice Boz gives: people under-leverage their managers, advisors, and mentors. Your job isn't to do it yourself; it's to get it done. Managers can bulldoze obstacles, provide context, and evaluate results more fairly when they know the real circumstances.

### Refuse to Rule
One of the most powerful things managers can do is decline to make a decision, signaling confidence in the report. "I think you've got it. The challenges you're facing are the right challenges. Just do your best there."

## Core Ideas

- Working 120 hours/week at early Facebook with no hobbies was not glamorous; romantic startup stories come from survivors, not the many who sacrificed equally without the same outcome
- The News Feed was easier than people think: everyone was outraged while immediately doubling their usage of the product
- Communication is the job at scale; strategy and mentorship through resources like podcasts fill a gap that traditional institutions like the ACM never addressed
- The early days required radical self-sufficiency: buying server components at Fry's Electronics, racking them at the colo, and running them yourself
- Nobody asked Boz to build anti-spam/anti-scraping; he just saw the problem and woke up every 4 hours for 2 years to check reports

## Notable Perspectives

> "I don't want to take away from the romanticism of it. It's just that most often, we hear those romantic stories from the successes."

> "The advice I find I have to give more frequently than any other is for people to more directly leverage their leaders."
