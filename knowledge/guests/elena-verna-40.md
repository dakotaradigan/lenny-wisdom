# Elena Verna

Head of Growth at Lovable, one of the fastest-growing companies in history ($200M+ ARR with ~100 people). Previously CMO at Miro, SVP Growth at SurveyMonkey, interim Head of Growth at Amplitude. In this latest role, she has had to throw out most of her 15-20 year growth playbook because the speed and dynamics of AI-native growth are fundamentally different.

## Key Frameworks

### 95% Innovation, 5% Optimization
At traditional companies, Elena spent roughly 5% of time innovating on growth and 95% optimizing. At Lovable, it is reversed: 95% innovation, 5% optimization. When competitors can launch a vibe coding business overnight, staying ahead requires constant reinvention of growth loops rather than optimization of existing ones. The answer is not optimization of the problem but reinvention of the solution.

### Building in Public + Founder-Led Social
One of Lovable's biggest growth strategies combines building in public with employee and founder-led social media. This creates authentic, ongoing engagement that paid channels cannot replicate. Coupled with generous free credit distribution to anyone who wants to run a hackathon or evangelize the product internally at their company.

## Core Ideas

- Only 30-40% of traditional growth knowledge transfers to an AI-native hypergrowth environment. The remaining 60-70% must be invented from scratch because the pace, competitive dynamics, and user behavior are fundamentally different.
- Give your product away generously. When a user volunteers to run a hackathon at their workplace using your product, do not block them with pricing -- give them free credits. They are doing your marketing and activation work for you.
- At hypergrowth AI companies, every competitor is starting the same business. Differentiation comes not from optimization but from creating new growth loops that others have not imagined yet.
- The role of Head of Growth at an AI-native company is more like a creative strategist than a metrics optimizer. Traditional A/B testing and funnel optimization are table stakes, not differentiators.
