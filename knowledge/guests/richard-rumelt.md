# Richard Rumelt

Legendary strategy professor at UCLA Anderson, author of Good Strategy Bad Strategy and The Crux. Former spacecraft designer for NASA turned business strategist. One of the most referenced strategy thinkers on Lenny's Podcast -- multiple guests cite his work as foundational.

## Key Frameworks

### The Kernel of Strategy
Every good strategy has three elements: (1) a Diagnosis of the situation -- what is actually going on, (2) a Guiding Policy for how to deal with it, and (3) Coherent Actions that implement the policy. If any element is missing, it is not a strategy. Most organizations produce bad strategy by substituting goals, aspirations, or word salad for these elements.

### Sources of Power and Asymmetry
Strategy requires a source of power -- some asymmetry that gives you an edge. This can be reputation, relationships, proprietary knowledge, being first to recognize something, network effects, or focus. Power must be concentrated on a target that can actually be affected, like sunlight through a magnifying glass. Without a source of power, strategy is just a wish.

### Bad Strategy Detection
Bad strategy takes four common forms: fluff (buzzword-laden descriptions of the situation), failure to face the challenge (no real diagnosis), mistaking goals for strategy ("grow revenue 20%"), and incoherent actions that work against each other. The most common failure is having 17 priorities instead of a focused few.

## Core Ideas

- The word "priority" means the first thing -- having 17 priorities means you have zero priorities
- Study history to develop strategic thinking; there is no science of strategy, so analogy to previous human experience is the primary tool
- Every new idea looks dumb at first, and the more disruptive it is, the dumber it looks -- the key is suspending disbelief long enough to investigate
- Focus is the fundamental source of power in strategy; trying to do too many things is the most common failure mode

## Notable Perspectives

> "Don't call it strategy, call it an action agenda. Begin to identify the one or two key challenges that can actually be addressed and what are we going to do about it?"

> "Strategy is a design for overcoming a high-stakes challenge."
