# Emilie Gerber

Founder and CEO of Six Eastern, a PR agency that has worked with 100+ tech companies from stealth startups to public companies including Perplexity. Previously led PR for Uber's business development team and worked at Box on product communications. Known for being exceptionally tactical about PR rather than theoretical.

## Key Frameworks

### Pattern-Matching Pitches
The most effective press pitches are straightforward and pattern-match to what reporters already cover. Founders get lost in massive problem statements and trend stories, but if you are very direct about what your company does and why it matters, it generally works. Simplicity and clarity beat cleverness.

### Finding the Nugget
Even products without obviously exciting stories have a PR angle. The key is finding the specific nugget -- a data point, a counterintuitive insight, a founder story, a surprising customer use case -- that makes a reporter care. This requires stepping outside your own messaging and seeing the story as a journalist would.

## Core Ideas

- Nobody gets as tactical about PR as they should. There is too much theoretical conversation and examining big blunders, but startups need to know the actual steps to get coverage for their company.
- The pitch email is everything. If a reporter cannot understand what your company does from the first two sentences, they will not read further. Lead with the story, not the company description.
- PR and product launches work best when comms is involved early in the product development process, not brought in at the end to "announce" something. The story shapes how you position the entire launch.
- Timing matters enormously in PR. Understanding news cycles, competitive announcements, and seasonal patterns can mean the difference between coverage and silence.
