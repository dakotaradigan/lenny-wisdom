# Sam Lessin

Partner at Slow Ventures, former VP of Product at Facebook, two-time founder. Author of a book on professional etiquette for the tech world. Teaches that etiquette is a skill for showing up in a room with a low heart rate -- enabling trust, partnerships, and better business outcomes.

## Key Frameworks

### Low Heart Rate Presence
Etiquette is not about manners for their own sake -- it is about showing up with calm confidence rather than anxious transactional energy. At a VC holiday party, the founder who arrives like an energizer bunny trying to collect business cards projects scarcity. The one who arrives with abundance mindset -- this is not my one shot, I will have other opportunities -- builds better relationships.

### Give More Than You Take
In conversations, play ping pong rather than interrogation. Ask questions but also offer ideas and value. Welcome others into conversations rather than monopolizing high-status people. The goal is building relationships, not collecting contacts.

## Core Ideas

- As software gets commoditized and AI changes trust dynamics, etiquette ironically matters more -- people need to trust you with their data and business
- Silicon Valley's narrative of "none of this matters, just focus on product" is wrong for 99.9% of startups; etiquette skills remove unnecessary uphill battles
- Practical rules: be early, have a firm handshake, repeat names back, do not make others feel bad about being late, maintain eye contact
- When you forget someone's name, introduce your partner first ("Jessica, I want to introduce you") and let the other person fill in their own name
