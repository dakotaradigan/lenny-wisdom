# Robby Stein

VP of Product at Google Search, responsible for AI Overviews, AI Mode, Google Lens, and search ranking. Previously Head of Product at Instagram where he led the launch of Stories, Reels, and Close Friends, growing Instagram to 500M+ DAU. Co-founded Artifact with Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger. One of the rare product leaders with massive impact across two global consumer platforms.

## Key Frameworks

### Embodying Relentless Improvement
The physical manifestation of two qualities: (1) relentlessness -- complete effort always exerted in a positive direction, and (2) always making things better, never being content. Success comes from compounding small improvements until a tipping point where the product becomes net useful. His wife's description: "dissatisfied" -- not unhappy, but driven by a deep desire to make the world better.

### AI as Expansion, Not Replacement
AI search is not replacing traditional search but expanding the types of questions people ask. Core search queries (phone numbers, directions, prices) remain unchanged. AI enables new query types: take a photo and ask about it, ask complex multi-constraint questions in natural language. The biggest shift is from "keyword-ese" to natural language input.

## Core Ideas

- Not every great thing will be invented by you -- adopting proven formats (like Stories from Snapchat) serves your users better than insisting on originality
- AI product development is becoming dramatically more human-like; you can increasingly just use natural language to configure AI behavior instead of heavy fine-tuning
- Google's AI search uses "query fan-out" where the model autonomously generates dozens of sub-queries to find the best information, then synthesizes results -- fundamentally different from other chatbots
- Product momentum comes from compounding daily improvements, not single breakthroughs; the tipping point arrives when enough small gains accumulate
