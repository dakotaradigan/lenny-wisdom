# Dan Hockenmaier

Head of Strategy and Analytics at Faire, previously scaled Thumbtack from 30 people through 10x+ growth, and built strategy consulting firm Basis One (acquired by Faire) where he built 20-30 growth models for startups. Co-creator of the Race Car Growth Framework with Lenny Rachitsky.

## Key Frameworks

### Growth Model Architecture
An analytical representation of how a business grows, built in a spreadsheet. Core building blocks for SaaS: acquisition channels, retention curves, and monetization. For marketplaces, add transaction frequency, AOV, unit economics, and supply-demand interaction. 50% of the value comes from simply building the model because it forces you to understand how the business actually works.

### Marketplace Gardener Principle
Running a marketplace is like being a gardener, not a construction worker. You must have a very light touch because changing one variable can produce cascading effects two months later. When something is working, tread carefully with the core incentives and mechanisms.

## Core Ideas

- Growth is much more sensitive to customer retention than anyone can intuit. Small percentage gains in retention compound enormously because retained customers drive referrals, content generation, and contribution margin reinvestment. Most companies are misallocating resources away from retention.
- Growth models are not forecasting tools -- they are opportunity assessment tools. Use them during annual planning to create a common currency for comparing different team investments across the business.
- For marketplaces, avoid one giant model. Instead, build one high-level conceptual model showing how the whole system works, plus mini-models for each product pod. The interaction between supply and demand is too complex for a single model to capture accurately.
- Payback period is a much better measure of paid marketing performance than LTV/CAC because the speed at which you recoup money to acquire the next customer determines compounding growth rate.
