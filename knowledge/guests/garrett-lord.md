# Garrett Lord

Co-founder and CEO of Handshake, the career platform for college students used by every Fortune 500 company, 1,500+ colleges, and 20+ million students. In early 2025, realized their proprietary network of PhDs and master's students was extremely valuable for AI model training. Launched a data labeling business from zero to $50M ARR in four months, on pace to exceed $100M ARR within 12 months -- surpassing their decade-old core business in under two years.

## Key Frameworks

### The Post-Training Opportunity
AI model gains have shifted from pre-training (ingesting the entire internet) to post-training (augmenting and improving data across specific domains). The models have gotten so good that generalists are no longer needed -- what labs need are experts across every economically valuable capability area (STEM, law, medicine, finance). The moat in human data is access to an engaged audience of qualified experts.

### Self-Disruption Through Platform Leverage
Handshake's existing audience (18M professionals, 500K PhDs, 3M master's students) gave them zero customer acquisition costs for their new AI business. The lesson: look at your existing platform assets through the lens of emerging AI demand. The most valuable pivot may be monetizing an audience you already have in a way you never imagined.

## Core Ideas

- Pre-training data has been exhausted; most frontier model gains now come from post-training with expert-generated data
- PhD students can break models in ways average users cannot, making them uniquely valuable for identifying weaknesses in AI reasoning
- Treating data labelers as experts (not low-cost labor) with community, onboarding, and instructional design produces higher quality output
- There will never be a time like this in business; unlimited demand means the imperative is to have zero regrets about effort and execution
- AI is not replacing young workers -- being AI-native is a huge advantage, just as growing up with Google was a resume skill for the previous generation
