# Jackson Shuttleworth

Group Product Manager at Duolingo leading the retention team. Responsible for the streaks feature, which is Duolingo's single most impactful growth lever. Over 9 million users have a year-plus streak. The team has run 600+ experiments on streaks in four years.

## Key Frameworks

### Unit of Use for Streaks
The streak should track the core unit of engagement that matters. Moving from XP-based streaks to "one lesson per day" was a massive win -- simple, meaningful, and easy to understand. But making it even easier (one exercise) produced zero DAU gain because it captured the least engaged users with a meaningless unit.

### Zero to Seven Day Retention Curve
The biggest retention jumps happen in the first seven days. After day seven, loss aversion kicks in and retention flattens. This insight drives disproportionate experimentation investment in the early streak experience -- getting users past the seven-day threshold is the highest-leverage retention work.

### Intentional Goal Setting
Letting users choose their own streak goal (14 days, 30 days, 50 days) with an opt-out button was a huge win. The act of intentionally selecting -- not being defaulted into -- a goal drives commitment. Pre-selecting a harder goal for users actually hurt because it removed the intentionality. The copy change from "Continue" to "Commit to my goal" alone was a massive DAU driver.

## Core Ideas

- Test everything, but not randomly. Start with a hypothesis informed by cross-team learnings, competitive analysis (especially games), and data patterns. About half of Duolingo's experiments lose, but even losing experiments generate valuable insights.
- Copy testing is dramatically underrated. If you have the user base, run copy tests constantly. Little string changes like "Commit to my goal" can produce outsized wins at near-zero cost.
- Engagement optimization is learning optimization. The easiest way to not learn on Duolingo is to not come back. Retention IS learning.
- When an experiment is neutral, shut it down to avoid adding cognitive load and complexity. Ship neutral only when it creates a platform for future DAU-positive experiments.
