# Hamilton Helmer

Author of 7 Powers, widely considered the best book on business strategy. Leaders like Patrick Collison, Peter Thiel, Reed Hastings, and Daniel Ek credit the book for helping them build durable companies. Hamilton's framework identifies the specific economic structures that create sustainable competitive advantage (power).

## Key Frameworks

### Power = Benefit + Barrier
Power requires both: a benefit (cost or price advantage over competitors) and a barrier (something durable that prevents competitors from replicating it). Warren Buffett's "economic castles protected by unbreachable moats" captures it -- the castle is the benefit, the moat is the barrier. Without both, you do not have power.

### Power Progression
Different types of power become available at different stages of a company's life. Even before product-market fit, you can ask which business propositions tilt toward the availability of power. After PMF, you must establish your source of power. In the stability phase, you must know your power to defend it. Iconic businesses often have second and third acts (AWS, Apple's iPhone), which restart the whole process.

### The Treadmill vs Power
Operational excellence (best team, fastest execution) is not power. You are on a treadmill: if you stop running, you get creamed, but the things that drive operational excellence can be mimicked. Power is what remains even when competitors try their hardest to catch up.

## Core Ideas

- Strategy should be thought about always, even pre-PMF -- but early-stage strategy is about tilting probabilities, not creating deterministic plans
- Strategy is about the fundamental determinants of business value (NPV of expected cash flow), which makes it a long-time-horizon concept distinct from tactics
- The seven powers are: scale economies, network effects, counter-positioning, switching costs, branding, cornered resource, and process power
- Founders should think about what underlying characteristics of their business propositions might tilt toward the availability of one or more powers
- Strategy as a concept is most useful when narrowed to "what drives durable business value" rather than "everything important this year"

## Notable Perspectives
