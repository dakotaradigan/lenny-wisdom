# Benjamin Lauzier

Former VP of Product and Growth at Thumbtack where he 3X'd growth in three years, and early employee (#30) at Lyft where he led driver-side product and growth, reaching 1% of US workers driving monthly. Teaches marketplace growth at Reforge and advises marketplace founders.

## Key Frameworks

### Market Health Metric
Find the leading indicator that predicts your marketplace liquidity. At Lyft, it was ETAs: if the closest driver was under 2 minutes away, conversion hit a ceiling. This metric is far more actionable for teams than the output metric (fill rate) alone.

### Focus on the Hard Side First
Pre-product-market fit, forget marketplace dynamics. Pick the hardest side to grow (supply ~80-90% of the time), find a hack or crutch for the other side, and nail the core value exchange. Thumbtack used Craigslist to bootstrap supply while validating demand.

### Liquidity as the Core Metric
Liquidity (demand fill rate) is how marketplaces win. It's the overlap between what supply wants to sell and what demand wants to buy. It's both the direct multiplier on marketplace efficiency and the ultimate engagement loop.

## Core Ideas

- Pre-PMF marketplace founders over-focus on marketplace dynamics when they should focus on basic product-market fit for one side
- You have two product-market fits in a marketplace: one for supply, one for demand; measure them independently using traditional methods
- Giving users too many filters fragments supply and hurts SLAs more than it helps; Sidecar's failure illustrates this perfectly
- The "smoke machine" problem: a checkbox that seems harmless can eliminate 95% of supply; use ranking not filtering
- Signs a marketplace is a good model: high fragmentation, uniform needs, and high barrier to matchmaking

## Notable Perspectives

> "If you don't have product market fit, and if you don't have a good enough growth strategy for at least one side of your marketplace, just forget about all this marketplace stuff."

> "Provide guardrails for what a good experience is in your marketplace, set a clear bar for quality, and provide the right coaching and tools for supply to be successful."
