# Ivan Zhao

Co-founder and CEO of Notion. Grew up in China, learned English from SpongeBob, was inspired by Douglas Engelbart's vision of augmenting human intellect. Built Notion over 10+ years through multiple rebuilds, including moving to Japan with his co-founder to rewrite the codebase from scratch.

## Key Frameworks

### Sugar-Coated Broccoli
Hide your ambitious vision inside a form factor people already understand. Notion's real vision is "Lego for software" -- everyone can create their own tools. But most people don't care about that. So they wrapped it in a productivity suite people use daily, hiding the no-code power inside.

### The Small Bus Metaphor
The smaller the bus, the easier it turns corners, accelerates, and maneuvers. As a leader, you decide who sits on the bus -- that dictates how fast it moves and your entire work experience. This metaphor helps the team internalize why staying lean matters: better systems beat brute force headcount.

### Craft and Values
Company philosophy centered on two pillars: craft (applying taste and technical skill to make clever trade-offs and create something new and useful) and values (your personal vision of what the world should have more of). Building a product is balancing these: too much self-expression means no users; too much business optimization means building a commodity.

## Core Ideas

- The first version of Notion was too much about what the founders wanted, not what users needed. It took 3-4 "lost years" to learn that most people don't care about creating software -- they care about getting work done. The product-market fit moment was gradual, not binary.
- Don't be afraid to reset. Progress through better abstractions compounds faster than linear building. If you find the right kernel, you can catch up to everything you threw away much quicker than you'd expect.
- Notion had no salesperson until 10M+ ARR, hired its first PM at 50 people, and stayed profitable by tracking talent density (revenue per employee) rather than headcount. The overhead of internal communication is the real cost of scale.
- Tools shape us as much as we shape them. Building software is the highest-leverage thing you can do for other people -- giving them the ability to augment their problem-solving capacity.

## Notable Perspectives

> "People don't want to eat the broccoli but people like sugar, so give them the sugar then hide your broccoli inside of it."

> "Too much of yourself, then there's no users. Too much for business, you're building a commodity."
