# Grant Lee

CEO and co-founder of Gamma, an AI-powered presentation and website design tool. Hit $100M ARR in just over two years with a team of around 30 people, valued at over $2 billion. Unlike many fast-growing AI startups, Gamma has been profitable for a long time and did not raise significant capital early on. Built in a category most investors dismissed -- one told him it was "the worst idea I have ever heard."

## Key Frameworks

### Micro-Influencer Marketing at Scale
Do not pursue big trendy creators with a million followers and hand them a script. Instead, find thousands of micro-influencers whose audience genuinely benefits from your product. Onboard each one manually so they understand the product deeply and tell your story in their own voice. This creates wildfire spread that feels authentic, not like an ad. Grant personally onboarded every initial influencer.

### Rapid Same-Day Experimentation
Have an idea in the morning, build a functional prototype, recruit legitimate prospective users with zero skin in the game, and run a full-scale experiment by the afternoon. Watch users struggle in real-time. By the next day, go through all findings together and fix what is broken. This cycle has been applied to every major feature.

### Growth from Day One
After being told the category was impossible, Grant made growth a first-class priority from the founding. The lesson: in hyper-competitive, crowded categories, growth cannot be an afterthought. If you can learn growth, anyone can learn growth.

## Core Ideas

- Product-market fit can be deceptive; winning Product Hunt (product of the day, week, month) felt like validation but turned out to be misleading about true PMF
- Profitable, sustainable growth in AI is possible and preferable to burning cash for growth; profitability from early on creates strategic optionality
- Building in a "dumb" category that incumbents dominate can be an enormous advantage because investors and competitors overlook you
- Pricing should be discovered through experimentation with real users, not through theoretical modeling
- A 30-person team serving 50+ million users globally is possible when the product is self-serve and the growth engine is organic
