# Nicole Forsgren

The developer productivity expert -- creator of the DORA metrics and co-author of the SPACE framework. Wrote the award-winning book Accelerate. Currently a partner at Microsoft Research leading developer productivity, experience, and strategy. Founded DORA (acquired by Google) and has helped the world's biggest companies measure and improve engineering performance.

## Key Frameworks

### DORA Four Keys
Two speed metrics (lead time for changes, deployment frequency) and two stability metrics (mean time to restore, change failure rate). The breakthrough finding: speed and stability move together -- shipping smaller changes more frequently leads to both faster delivery and greater reliability because the blast radius of each change is smaller.

### SPACE Framework
A holistic model for developer productivity covering Satisfaction and wellbeing, Performance, Activity, Communication and collaboration, and Efficiency and flow. Avoids the trap of brute-forcing productivity by including sustainability, community effects, and burnout reduction alongside throughput measures.

### Elite Performance Benchmarks
Deploy on demand, lead time under one day, time to restore under one hour, change failure rate 0-15%. These benchmarks hold regardless of company size -- there was no statistically significant difference between small and large companies. The only outlier was retail, which performed better due to survival pressure.

## Core Ideas

- The biggest misconception in engineering is that slowing down improves stability; the opposite is true -- forcing two-week change approval windows causes batch changes with massive blast radius and harder debugging
- 80% of organizations' biggest challenge is not measurement but agreeing on what problem they are actually trying to solve; teams will work for months without alignment on whether "developer experience" means friction, culture, or toolchains
- Ship smaller things more often is both the key to moving faster and the key to improving quality -- they are the same strategy
- Productivity, developer experience, and DevOps are related but distinct concepts; conflating them leads to misaligned interventions
