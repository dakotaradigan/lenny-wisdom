# Sachin Monga

Head of Product at Substack. Previously founded Cocoon (sold to Substack) and spent seven years at Facebook on growth, video, camera, and ads. Joined Substack with zero PMs and built the product function from scratch during Substack's transformation from a tool into a network.

## Key Frameworks

### Principled Product Prioritization
Substack starts from principles rather than metrics: writers should control their destiny, readers should control their experience. When multiple implementation paths exist, choose the one that maximizes user control. This principle-first approach guided the recommendations feature -- letting writers manually curate who they recommend rather than using algorithmic suggestions.

### Facilitator-First PM Leadership
When joining a startup with a strong founder-CEO, start as a facilitator rather than a decision maker. Bridge the gap between the CEO's vision and team execution. Over time, trust builds through reps, enabling more autonomous decision-making across teams.

## Core Ideas

- The Substack recommendations feature drove millions of new subscriptions by creating a viral goodwill loop: when you recommend someone, they get notified, creating reciprocity and organic network growth
- At startups, the product process is always becoming obsolete -- as soon as you figure it out, growth makes it irrelevant. Focus on getting better every week rather than finding the perfect process
- Structuring teams around customers (writers, readers, growth) rather than product surfaces (app, dashboard) creates more durable team structures that require fewer reorgs
- Subscription-based business models can unlock clearly better user experiences compared to ad-supported models by aligning incentives between creators and consumers

## Notable Perspectives
