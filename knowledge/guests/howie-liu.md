# Howie Liu

Co-founder and CEO of Airtable. Built the company over 13 years from a no-code platform vision into an AI-native app-building platform. Self-described product UX person at core who has become an IC CEO -- getting back into the details of building in the AI era.

## Key Frameworks

### Fast Thinking / Slow Thinking Org Design
Split the EPD org into two groups inspired by Kahneman. The "fast thinking" (AI platform) group ships new capabilities on a near-weekly basis -- entrepreneurial, autonomous, full-stack thinkers. The "slow thinking" group handles deliberate infrastructure work requiring careful architecture. The two complement each other: fast creates top-of-funnel excitement, slow converts seeds into durable deployments.

### The IC CEO
In the AI era, CEOs of software companies must return to individual contributor mode. You cannot taste the soup without participating in making it. AI capabilities evolve so rapidly that understanding the solution space requires hands-on engagement -- not just reviewing screenshots or decks.

### Prototypes Over Decks
Replace PRDs and slide decks with functional prototypes. AI products must be experienced, not described. Share prompts, chat threads, and deep research outputs directly rather than polished memos.

## Core Ideas

- Every software product must be "refounded" for AI. Ask yourself: if you were founding a new company with the same mission today, how would you execute using a fully AI-native approach? If you can't answer that affirmatively, consider selling.
- The best signal of a company's AI readiness is whether the CEO uses ChatGPT or Claude daily. Hourly is better.
- In the AI era, PM/design/engineering roles must become multidisciplinary. Each role needs minimum baseline competency in the other two, then can go deeper in their specialty.
- Encourage radical play with AI tools. Give people explicit permission to cancel meetings for a day or a week to experiment with every AI product relevant to their domain.
