# Lauryn Isford

Former Head of Growth at Airtable. Previously product growth lead at Facebook working on user growth, internet.org, and India growth. Led growth for Blue Bottle's e-commerce business. Deep specialist in onboarding as a growth lever.

## Key Frameworks

### Activation Rate Sweet Spot (5-15%)
A low activation rate (5-15%) is better than a high one (40%+) because it indicates high correlation with long-term retention. If 40% of users hit your activation metric, only a fraction will retain at month 12. Pick a more specific, harder-to-reach metric so that even small improvements have massive downstream effects on the business.

### Experiment Only When You Need To
Experimentation is expensive in engineering and analyst time. Run experiments primarily for risk mitigation when making dramatic changes, not to prove the precise impact of every feature. Build a culture that rewards doing right by customers, not just pointing to experiment-derived numbers in performance reviews.

## Core Ideas

- Airtable's guided onboarding wizard drove a 20% lift in activation by reducing cognitive load and showing users their workflow materializing in real time as they answered questions
- Segment users by learning style and building style rather than traditional demographics like job role; how someone builds matters more than what they want to build
- Retire tooltips in favor of immersive guided experiences for complex products where getting started has high cognitive load
- Ongoing education (Airtable called it "The Mole") should pop up contextually after initial onboarding, not dump advanced features on day one
- Take a portfolio approach to onboarding: guided wizard, personalization, and ongoing education as three complementary investment areas

## Notable Perspectives

> "An activation rate that falls in a lower percentage range is better than one that falls in a higher percentage range because it means there's likely much higher correlation with long-term retention."

> "What the user actually needs to be onboarded well is not the same as what your business wants the user to be onboarded to."
