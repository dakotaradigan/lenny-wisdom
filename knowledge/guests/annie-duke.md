# Annie Duke

Annie Duke is the author of bestselling books Thinking in Bets and Quit: The Power of Knowing When to Walk Away. She's a special partner at First Round Capital and a former professional poker player who won over $4 million in tournaments including the World Series of Poker. She now helps companies make better decisions.

## Key Frameworks

### Making Implicit Explicit
The single most impactful change for decision quality: force people to write down their predictions and reasoning before meetings, not during them. Meetings should be for discussion only - discovery and decision-making should happen before and after.

### Pre-Mortems with Kill Criteria
A pre-mortem is only valuable if you establish specific kill criteria upfront - concrete signals that, if observed, trigger predetermined actions. Without committed kill criteria, pre-mortems are just an exercise.

### Shortening Long Feedback Loops
There is no such thing as a long feedback loop. Identify things correlated with your desired long-term outcome and measure those now. This transforms seemingly distant outcomes into near-term actionable signals.

## Core Ideas

- Meetings should only be for discussion, never for discovery (sharing new info) or deciding - those happen before and after
- Daniel Kahneman's greatest quality was humility and curiosity: he was the first to say when his own research didn't replicate
- Adversarial collaboration (co-writing papers with people who disagree with you) produces better outcomes than defending positions
- Money and happiness: once basic needs are met, the correlation weakens but doesn't disappear entirely
- In parenting and leadership, the only thing that truly matters is that people know you care about them deeply
