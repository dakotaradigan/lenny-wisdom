# Adam Fishman

Adam Fishman was the first growth and marketing hire at Lyft, then led product and growth at Patreon for over four years, and was CPO at Imperfect Foods. He now advises companies on product and growth and is an EIR at Reforge.

## Key Frameworks

### Growth Competency Model
A four-quadrant framework for hiring and evaluating growth leaders: growth execution (channel fluency, experimentation, productizing learnings), customer knowledge (data fluency, user psychology, creative approach), growth strategy (loop modeling, capital allocation, prioritization), and communication/influence. The goal is balanced teams, not unicorn individuals.

### Onboarding as Promise Delivery
Onboarding is the only part of your product that 100% of users touch. Your brand is the promise you make; your product experience is the delivery of that promise. Mismatched expectations create disappointed customers.

## Core Ideas

- Don't pattern match when hiring growth leaders; use first-principles competency evaluation instead of "find me someone like the last person"
- Growth execution and customer knowledge are foundational skills; strategy and communication are more advanced and develop over time
- User psychology matters more than logical appeals - most users come to your product with an emotional frame, not a logical one
- Use the competency model as a reverse-interviewing tool to evaluate alignment with founders before accepting a role
