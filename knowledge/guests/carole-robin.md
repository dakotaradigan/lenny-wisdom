# Carole Robin

Taught Stanford GSB's legendary "Touchy Feely" course (Interpersonal Dynamics) for 20+ years. Founded Leaders in Tech nonprofit bringing these lessons to tech leaders. Author of *Connect* on building robust relationships.

## Key Frameworks

### Three Realities
In any interaction there are three realities: what's going on for me (my intent), what I did (my behavior), and what happened for you (your experience). We only have access to two of the three, which is why miscommunication is the default.

### Feelings vs. Accusations
"I feel that you don't care" and "I feel you're being insensitive" are not feelings; they're accusations disguised as feelings. This is where the biggest mistakes in feedback happen. Real feelings are emotions like hurt, disappointed, afraid.

### Anger as Secondary Emotion
Anger is always a distancing emotion covering a primary emotion (fear, hurt, disappointment). Effective communication means getting to the connecting emotion underneath the anger.

## Core Ideas

- Interpersonal competence is a determinant of both personal and professional success; people do business with people, not ideas or products
- Relationships exist on a continuum from dysfunction to exceptional; the skills to reach functional and robust are learnable and transformative
- Ask questions starting with what, when, where, how; stay away from why, which triggers defensiveness
- Mental models formed in youth limit our potential as adults; recognizing and updating them is essential for leadership growth
- Many students say the course was worth their entire Stanford tuition; others credit it with saving their marriages
