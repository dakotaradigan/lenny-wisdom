# Christina Wodtke

Author, speaker, and Stanford lecturer teaching product management and game design. Author of *Radical Focus* on OKRs. Consults with companies on OKR processes. Previously a product leader at LinkedIn, MySpace, Zynga, and Yahoo, plus founder of three companies.

## Key Frameworks

### OKRs as Vitamins, Not Medicine
OKRs don't fix broken organizations; they supercharge healthy ones. If you lack strategy, empowered teams, or psychological safety, OKRs will just reveal what's broken. Get your foundations right first.

### The Atomic Unit of OKRs
"What am I doing this week to get closer to our goals?" If you can answer this question consistently, you've captured the heart of OKRs. Everything else is structure around this core practice.

### Friday Celebrations
Celebrating weekly wins transforms team morale and culture. CEOs report that starting Friday celebrations before even implementing OKRs already changed things. Acknowledging what's awesome across teams makes people feel part of something special.

## Core Ideas

- The main benefits of OKRs: focus, alignment, cadence of progress, and a learning cycle that makes your company constantly smarter
- Companies fail with OKRs by having 20 OKRs per quarter instead of radical focus on the one big rock to move
- OKRs piggyback on temporal landmarks (quarters, Mondays) to create natural reflection points
- The quarterly grading creates a learning cycle: what got in our way, what should we try next? This compounds knowledge over time
- She runs her personal life on OKRs for the last 8-10 years; they help her ADHD by forcing weekly review of where to put attention

## Notable Perspectives
