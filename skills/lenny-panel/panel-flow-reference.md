# Panel Flow Reference

## Synthesis Patterns

### Finding Convergence

Look for patterns across 3+ guest profiles:
- Similar frameworks applied to the same problem
- Shared principles (e.g., "test with users before building" appears in Cagan, Torres, and Vohra)
- Consistent recommendations across different industries

**Template:**
"**[X] of your panelists agree:** [shared insight]. [Guest A] frames it as [their version], while [Guest B] calls it [their version]. The core principle is the same: [synthesis]."

### Finding Divergence

Look for genuine disagreements, not just different framings:
- Opposite recommendations for the same situation
- Different priorities (speed vs. quality, growth vs. profitability)
- Different beliefs about what matters most

**Template:**
"**[Guest A] vs. [Guest B] on [topic]:**
[Guest A] argues [position], based on their experience at [Company] where [context].
[Guest B] takes the opposite view: [position], drawing from [their context].
The key difference: [what makes them disagree — different stage, market, philosophy]."

### Drilling Into a Divergence

When the user picks a divergence to explore:
1. Expand both positions with more detail from each guest's profile
2. Identify what context would make each position correct
3. Ask the user: "Which context sounds more like your situation?"
4. Help them apply the winning perspective to their specific case

## Example Panel Output

**Question:** "How should I price my new AI product?"

**Panel assembled (6 guests):**
- Madhavan Ramanujam — Pricing strategy, 400+ companies advised
- Jason Cohen — SaaS growth diagnostics, 2x unicorn founder
- Elena Verna — PLG monetization, B2B growth expert
- Patrick Campbell — SaaS pricing, ProfitWell founder
- Jason M Lemkin — B2B SaaS at scale, SaaStr founder
- Gibson Biddle — Product strategy, Netflix

**CONVERGENCE:**
- 5/6 agree: Test willingness to pay before building
- 4/6 agree: Usage-based pricing is the natural fit for AI (costs scale with usage)
- 6/6 agree: Don't discount to win deals early — it sets a permanent anchor

**DIVERGENCE:**
- Madhavan vs. Jason Fried on research depth: Madhavan says do extensive WTP research upfront. Jason says "just ship a price and iterate." The difference: Madhavan optimizes for getting it right; Jason optimizes for learning speed.
- Elena vs. Jason Lemkin on freemium: Elena says freemium is essential for PLG. Jason says at early stage, go sales-led and skip free tiers. The difference: Elena assumes bottoms-up adoption; Jason assumes enterprise sales motion.

"Which divergence is most relevant to your situation?"
