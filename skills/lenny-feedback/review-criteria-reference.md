# Review Criteria Reference

## Core Critique Checklist

Use this checklist to systematically review product documents. Not every item applies to every document — use judgment.

### Problem & Audience
- [ ] Is the problem clearly stated? Could someone outside the team understand it?
- [ ] Is the target user/customer defined specifically (not "everyone")?
- [ ] Is there evidence the problem is real (data, research, customer quotes)?
- [ ] Are the stakes clear — what happens if we don't solve this?

### Strategy & Positioning
- [ ] Is the proposed solution differentiated? (April Dunford's positioning framework)
- [ ] Is there a clear "why now"? (Mike Maples' inflection thinking)
- [ ] Does the strategy pass the DHM test — Delightful, Hard-to-copy, Margin-enhancing? (Gibson Biddle)
- [ ] Are there explicit non-goals / what we're NOT doing? (Chandra Janakiraman)

### Metrics & Success
- [ ] Is there a clear North Star metric?
- [ ] Are success metrics defined and measurable?
- [ ] Is there a way to know if this FAILED (not just succeeded)?
- [ ] Are leading indicators identified, not just lagging ones?

### User & Market
- [ ] Has willingness to pay been tested? (Madhavan Ramanujam)
- [ ] Is the job-to-be-done clearly articulated? (Bob Moesta)
- [ ] Are the competitive alternatives identified — including "do nothing"? (April Dunford)
- [ ] Is the activation path defined — how does a new user get to the aha moment? (Sean Ellis)

### Execution & Risk
- [ ] Has a pre-mortem been done? What could kill this? (Shreyas Doshi)
- [ ] Are one-way door decisions identified? (Amazon framework)
- [ ] Is the MVP scoped — what's the smallest thing that tests the hypothesis?
- [ ] Are dependencies called out?

### Common Pitfalls to Flag
- **Building before testing pricing** — 72% of innovations fail because pricing is an afterthought (Madhavan Ramanujam)
- **Optimizing for positive ROI instead of opportunity cost** — teams default to safe bets (Shreyas Doshi)
- **Feature factory** — shipping features without measuring impact (Marty Cagan)
- **Consensus strategy** — "something for everyone" strategies that differentiate from nothing (April Dunford)
- **Vanity metrics** — measuring output (features shipped) instead of outcomes (user behavior changed)
- **Ignoring the adjacent user** — optimizing for power users while missing the next wave of growth (Bangaly Kaba)
